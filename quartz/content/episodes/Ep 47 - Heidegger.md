# Ep 47 - Heidegger

## What this lecture argues
Ep 47 is the deep dive into Heidegger. Vervaeke reads key passages from *On the Essence of Truth* (*Vom Wesen der Wahrheit*) and builds a layered schema: truth as propositional correspondence is grounded in the agent-arena relationship, which is grounded in **attunement** (Stimmung) — a pre-cognitive, non-subjective alignment with being that Vervaeke explicitly maps onto relevance realization. The history of philosophy (metaphysics) is the history of forgetting this attunement — trapping us at the propositional, having-mode level — which Heidegger calls the **forgetfulness of Being**. The structural error at the end of this forgetting is **onto-theology**: treating Being as if it were a supreme being. This is both the ground of nihilism and the theological expression of modal confusion. The episode also introduces Harman's speculative realist concept of the **thing beyond itself** (shining into subjectivity while simultaneously withdrawing from framing) as an explication of Heidegger and links Heidegger to Dreyfus (relevance realization, optimal grip) and Corbin (gnosis as dynamic coupling / salvational knowing).

**Attunement grounds truth**: Truth as propositional correspondence (statement matches reality) is only possible because of a deeper relationship: the agent-arena relationship that shapes what can be a standard for correspondence at all. But even that agent-arena relationship is grounded in attunement — a pre-cognitive, non-subjective fittedness-to-being that makes meaningful experience possible. Attunement is NOT a subjective feeling; Heidegger explicitly says "being attuned can never be understood as experience and feeling" — because it is what makes feeling and experience possible. Vervaeke maps attunement directly onto RR: the non-propositional, non-computational process of relevance detection that precedes all inference (Ep 47).

**Ek-sistence = salience**: Heidegger's term *ek-sistence* (standing out into the clearing) corresponds to salience. What stands out for Dasein is not random but shaped by the attunement — the existential-ontological analogue of RR's salience landscape (Ep 47).

**The thing beyond itself**: Against Kant's "thing in itself" (veiled by subjectivity and inaccessible), Harman proposes that every object simultaneously (a) *shines into* our subjectivity (phenomenological disclosure) and (b) *withdraws* from our framing (exceeds any frame we apply to it). Both shining and withdrawal are transjective: the withdrawal is not in our experience but contributes to the sense of the *realness* of things. In virtual reality, if a world never withdraws — if you can drain it dry — it loses its realness. The moreness must be an ongoing constraint on relevance, not just acknowledged as a limit. Vervaeke: this is sacredness as enacted, participatory resonance to the inexhaustibleness of reality (Ep 47).

**Truth as aletheia**: Lethe = covering/forgetting; *a-letheia* = uncovering, disclosure, un-forgetting. Truth as aletheia is attunement to the mutual disclosure in which things simultaneously shine and withdraw — a "deep remembering" (like sati, but existential) and deep disclosure. It is not propositional truth but the ground that makes propositional correctness possible. The two poles of aletheia: relevance (attunement side) + moreness/inexhaustibleness (independence of being side). Together they constitute the being-mode engagement with reality (Ep 47).

**Forgetfulness of Being / onto-theology**: When we trap ourselves at the propositional having-mode level, we forget the grounding attunement. We begin to misunderstand Being (capital B, the ground of realness) as a particular being (lowercase b). Pushing further, we posit a supreme being — the limit-case of having-mode conceptualization of Being — and this is onto-theology. For Heidegger, classical theism's presentation of God as supreme being is the philosophical crystallization of this modal confusion. Onto-theology + forgetfulness of Being → nihilism: because when the "supreme being" of onto-theology loses credibility, Being itself appears absent, generating the meaning crisis (Ep 47).

**Philia sophia vs. philia nikia**: Plato's distinction (which Heidegger recuperates) between love of wisdom (philia sophia — the transformative existential project) and love of victory (philia nikia — manipulation of propositions to win). Philia nikia mimics reasoning while forgetting the attuning quest. Heidegger sees the history of metaphysics as increasingly dominated by philia nikia (Ep 47).

**Dreyfus connection**: Dreyfus → Heidegger → 4E cognitive science. Dreyfus's quote: "Facts and rules are by themselves meaningless; they must be assigned relevance." Getting trapped at the propositional computational level produces combinatorial explosion (the frame problem). Dreyfus: Heidegger is the forerunner of the critique of purely computational cognitive science (*What Computers Can't Do*). Dreyfus → optimal grip → RR. This chain shows that Vervaeke's RR framework is not imposed on Heidegger but arises naturally from the phenomenological tradition (Ep 47).

**Corbin connection via gnosis**: Avens (*The New Gnosis*) links Heidegger to Corbin. Corbin was one of the first major translators of Heidegger into French (his translation of *Introduction to Metaphysics* was seminal). Corbin explicitly calls Heidegger's participatory knowing — "like can only be known by like," the dynamic coupling of self-knowing and world-knowing — gnosis: "a salvational redemptive knowledge because it has the virtue of bringing about the inner transformation of man." Gnosis = self-knowing and world-knowing dynamically coupled and mutually transformative. This is the response to Dasein: to respond appropriately to the being whose being is in question requires exactly this gnosis (Ep 47).
