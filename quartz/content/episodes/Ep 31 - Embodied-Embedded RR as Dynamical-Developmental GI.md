# Ep 31 - Embodied-Embedded RR as Dynamical-Developmental GI

## What this lecture argues
Ep 31 makes two large moves to establish relevance realization as a scientifically grounded, unified cognitive phenomenon. The first is ontological: RR is **transjective** — not subjective projection (romantic view) and not objective detection (empiricist view) but a real relation between the embodied-embedded organism and its environment, co-created by how they fit together. The body is constitutive of cognition (not a Cartesian appendage); the environment is constitutive of adaptivity (a great white shark is not intrinsically adaptive — it is adaptive only relative to its ocean environment). Relevance is to the organism-world relationship what graspability is to a hand and a bottle.

The second move shows that RR is not just dynamic but **developmental and unified**. Three exemplary opponent-processing mechanisms constitute RR: (1) **Cognitive Scope** — compression (data compression, integration, assimilation → efficiency, generalization) vs. particularization (overfitting, differentiation, accommodation → resiliency, context-sensitivity); (2) **Cognitive Tempering** — exploitation vs. exploration, governed by temporal dynamics (TDL vs. Inhibition on Return); (3) **Cognitive Prioritization** — flexible gambling between cost functions (focusing vs. diversifying). These operate at different scales within the same efficiency-resiliency opponent-processing architecture. When compression/particularization is running (which maps onto Piaget's assimilation/accommodation), the system is simultaneously integrating and differentiating — this is **complexification**, which generates emergent self-transcendence. RR is therefore inherently developmental: a self-organizing system develops by functioning and functions by developing.

The unification argument: Spearman's **Strong Positive Manifold** (any one cognitive task performance predicts all others) identifies a single underlying capacity — general intelligence (g). What psychometric tests probe for g — problem formulation, similarity judgment, pattern finding, dealing with ill-defined dynamic situations — is exactly the set of tasks where RR is required. Therefore RR is the best scientific candidate for general intelligence. Establishing this convergence (from psychology, AI, biology, and cognitive science) is described as the linchpin of the cognitive science side of the series: it will ground the later accounts of wisdom, rationality, and spirituality in a naturalistic theory.

## Concepts introduced or developed
- [[Transjectivity]] — introduced: relevance is neither subjective nor objective but transjective — a real organism-environment relation; anti-Cartesian; "realization" captures both making-real (objective) and coming-into-awareness (subjective)
- [[Complexification]] — introduced: simultaneous integration + differentiation = developmental self-transcendence; maps onto Piaget's assimilation/accommodation; produces emergent abilities
- [[Cognitive Tempering]] — introduced: exploitation vs. exploration trade-off; temporal dimension of the efficiency-resiliency opponent processing; TDL vs. Inhibition on Return
- [[General Intelligence]] — introduced as = RR: Spearman's g and Strong Positive Manifold converge on the capacity underwriting general problem solving; equated with relevance realization
- [[Efficiency vs Resiliency]] — deepened: three exemplary opponent-processing pairs (scope, tempering, prioritization) are shown as specific realizations of the efficiency-resiliency trade-off
- [[Relevance Realization]] — deepened: now fully shown to be embodied, embedded, transjective, dynamic, developmental, and unified (= g)
