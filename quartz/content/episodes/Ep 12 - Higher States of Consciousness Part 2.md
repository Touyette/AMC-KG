# Ep 12 - Higher States of Consciousness Part 2

## What this lecture argues
Ep 12 completes the HSC account by adding three levels — psychological, information-processing, and neural — and gives the full prescriptive argument for why HSC should rationally authorize transformation. Psychologically: de-centering is shown to promote wisdom via the **Solomon Effect** (Grossman), and the exaptation-of-self argument explains the world-disclosure of HSC — all the machinery bound up in ego-processing gets redirected onto the world, producing the characteristic intimacy and aliveness. At the machine-learning level: neural networks require disruptive strategies (noise/dropout) to avoid overfitting, reaching real invariants through compression — an exact analogy to what disruptive strategies do in humans. At the neural level: Newberg finds frontal-parietal hyperactivation then hypoactivation, with sustained thalamic integration, mirroring the break-frame/make-frame cycle; psilocybin increases metastability (simultaneous integration and differentiation = complexification), producing emergent cognitive functions. The prescriptive conclusion rests on **Plausibility** (not certainty): HSC optimizes for convergence (trustworthiness through independent lines of evidence), elegance (multi-apt power), and fluency — the same indispensable machinery the brain relies on everywhere, including in science. Therefore HSC states are good guides for transformation, though they are not sources of propositional metaphysical knowledge.

## Concepts introduced or developed
- [[Solomon Effect]] — introduced: Grossman's finding that describing a problem from the third-person perspective (de-centering) reliably produces wisdom-like reasoning; bridges de-centering to wisdom; connects to HSC's systematic deautomatization of egocentrism
- [[Plausibility]] — introduced: Vervaeke's technical construct: convergence (trustworthiness from independent evidence lines) + elegance (multi-apt power) + fluency, balanced = profundity; the indispensable epistemic standard that replaces certainty in both science and HSC
- [[De-centering]] — deepened: exaptation-of-self argument: all the machinery bound up in ego-processing can be turned onto the world, producing radical disclosure; radical de-centering is not loss of self but redeployment of its integrative power
- [[Higher States of Consciousness]] — completed: prescriptive case made; HSC is an optimization of plausibility machinery; propositional claims from HSC are unreliable and should not be taken as metaphysical pronouncements
