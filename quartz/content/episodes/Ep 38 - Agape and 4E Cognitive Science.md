# Ep 38 - Agape and 4E Cognitive Science

## What this lecture argues
Ep 38 brings together the two major threads of the series: (1) it deepens the response to the historical factors (the lost three orders) by grounding it in 4E Cognitive Science; and (2) it resolves Wolf's problem about meaning requiring objective attractiveness by showing that Agape is the transjective response.

**Wolf's problem and Agape**: Susan Wolf argues that meaning in life requires *subjective attraction* meeting *objective attractiveness* — we must find something salient AND it must have value independent of our valuing it. The problem: the scientific revolution (Galileo's primary/secondary quality distinction) eliminated objective attractiveness from the world — all values are now subjective, merely in the mind. Vervaeke's response: we don't need objective value in the transcendent supernatural sense. What we need is the *transjective* relationship itself (the `=` between subjective attraction and objective attractiveness). The key: RR is constitutively interested in itself as a process; it finds its own conditions inherently valuable. What I need to be connected to are *the conditions that afford meaning-making itself* — those conditions exist independently of my valuing them, because I emerge from them and participate in them; I do not create them by my valuing. This is **Agape**: caring about the conditions that make any caring possible. Agape is not subjective (my preference) or objective (value inhering in objects) but transjective — it is caring about the very ground from which agent and arena co-emerge (Ep 38).

**The full structure of enlightenment**: Parasitic processing → Counteractive Dynamical System; Modal Confusion → Sati; Reflectiveness Gap → Flow; Absurdity → Prajña; Anxiety → Indwelling the Sage; Alienation → Communitas; Existential Entrapment → Gnosis. All of these within a **wisdom framing**. And the entire project requires grounding in agape — the ecology of practices for addressing perennial problems must be agapically motivated, otherwise it collapses into having-mode self-improvement (Ep 38).

**4E Cognitive Science** (Varela's key insights, from "The Psychology of Awakening"):
- **Embodiment**: mind arises through immediate coping, not programmed symbol-manipulation; there is deep continuity between abstract cognition and sensory-motor action — Cognition → RR → Bioeconomy
- **Embeddedness / Niche Construction**: organisms shape and are shaped by their environment in ongoing mutual selection; culture is massive niche construction; the organism-environment relation is irreducibly transjective
- **Enactive** (Thompson): cognition is something we enact, not passively receive
- **Extended** (Clark/Chalmers): cognition extends through tools, environment, psycho-technologies, distributed cognition
- **Emergence**: mind emerges from embodied, embedded brain coupled to living environment; self-organizing → self-making (autopoietic) → self-identifying → self-transcending; this is the vertical dimension of being *without* two-worlds mythology
- **Emotion**: Damasio — without emotion (caring/coping), agents face combinatorial explosion; emotion is how RR is brought into salience landscaping; it shapes the agent-arena relationship at the level of caring/coping

**How 4E addresses the three orders**: Deep Continuity + Emergence gives back the **nomological order** (we belong in nature; our meaning-making belongs in the natural process of self-organizing complexity). Self-transcendence through complexification gives back the **normative order** (there is a direction of excellence — self-organizing → self-making → self-identifying → self-transcending). The **narrative order** may need a different response: Vervaeke suggests moving toward *post-narrative open-ended optimization* rather than cosmic telos — concern with the depth of living rather than horizontal historical direction, as modeled in higher states of consciousness (Ep 38).

**Transcendence into** (Goodenough): Instead of "transcendence above" (two worlds mythology — ascending out of the natural into the supernatural), what we need is "transcendence into" — into the depths of nature and the depths of the psyche, understood as reciprocally disclosing each other. This is the opposite of reciprocal narrowing: reciprocal opening up, mutual disclosure, experienced as a kind of love (Ep 38).
