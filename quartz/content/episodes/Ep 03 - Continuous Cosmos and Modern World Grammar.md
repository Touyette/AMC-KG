# Ep 03 - Continuous Cosmos and Modern World Grammar

## What this lecture argues
This episode establishes the mythological shift at the heart of the Axial Revolution: from the Continuous Cosmos — where the sacred, natural, and social worlds form an unbroken whole — to Two Worlds Mythology, in which a lower illusory world is opposed to a higher real world accessible through wisdom and self-transformation. Vervaeke frames this transition as "The Great Dis-embedding," by which the self becomes separable from the world and capable of self-transcendence rather than merely fitting into cycles of power. The Hebrew tradition and ancient Greece are identified as the two foundational loci for this transformation, with attention given to the Hebrew concept of Da'ath (participatory knowing), the psycho-technology of cosmic narrative time, and the Greek development of alphabetic literacy enhanced by vowels and standardized reading direction. Pythagoras is introduced as the figure who bridges shamanic soul flight with rational self-transcendence. The episode closes with a warning: the scientific worldview is re-embedding us in a one-world view, threatening our ability to sustain the psycho-technologies of wisdom and self-transcendence that the Axial world view made possible.

## Concepts introduced or developed
- [[Continuous Cosmos]] — defined here as the pre-Axial worldview of radical continuity between natural, social, and divine
- [[Two Worlds Mythology]] — introduced as the post-Axial mythological structure opposing the everyday and the real
- [[The Great Dis-embedding]] — Vervaeke's term for the Axial shift that separates self from world and makes self-transcendence possible
- [[Kairos]] — introduced as the turning point in the cosmic narrative; the right moment for moral redirection
- [[Da'ath]] — Hebrew participatory knowing; knowing by identification and reciprocal transformation
- [[Cognitive Fluency]] — introduced via Greek alphabet improvements; ease of processing increases perceived truth and confidence
- [[Axial Revolution]] — developed further; now analyzed via its specific forms in Israel and Greece

## Thinkers & traditions

- [[Hebrew Prophetic Tradition]] — develops cosmic narrative time and participatory knowing (Da'ath)
