# Ep 29 - Getting to the Depths of Relevance Realization

## What this lecture argues
Ep 29 probes what kind of account of Relevance Realization is possible. Three candidates are tested and found inadequate: representations presuppose aspectuality (which is already RR); computation via inference and rules presupposes relevance commitments; modularity is homuncular and cannot accommodate RR's multi-scale, simultaneous operation. Pylyshyn's FINSTING research provides empirical grounding: the mind tracks "hereness and nowness" — a pre-categorical, enactive demonstrative reference — before any categorisation or representation occurs. This suggests RR operates sub-semantically, at a level closer to participatory knowing than propositional knowing. The hierarchical dependency of propositional → procedural → perspectival → participatory knowing is made explicit. The episode ends with the methodological conclusion: we cannot have a theory of relevance, but we can have a theory of Relevance Realization — and that distinction will be the key to understanding meaning.

## Concepts introduced or developed
- [[Aspectuality]] — introduced: every representation selects a relevant subset of properties (an aspect); since aspectuality presupposes RR, representations cannot generate it
- [[FINSTING]] — introduced: Pylyshyn's term (Finding INSTances in experience); the mind's pre-categorical tracking of hereness and nowness; foundation of enactive demonstrative reference
- [[Enactive Demonstrative Reference]] — introduced: Vervaeke's term for the non-linguistic, enacted salience-tagging of here-nowness that underlies all categorisation
- [[Salience Tagging]] — introduced: the cognitive act of making something stand out as a "this" without yet categorising it
- [[The 4 Kinds of Knowing]] — extended: propositional depends on procedural, which depends on perspectival, which depends on participatory — RR must be grounded in participatory knowing
