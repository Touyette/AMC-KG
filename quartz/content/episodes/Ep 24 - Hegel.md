# Ep 24 - Hegel

## What this lecture argues
Ep 24 works through Hegel as the philosopher who attempts to rescue the Axial legacy by showing that reality just is the rational process of ideas developing through history. By rejecting Kant's unknowable "Thing in Itself," Hegel argues that the real is the rational and that Geist — a living, self-organizing system of patterns of intelligibility — unfolds through a dialectical process (thesis, antithesis, higher synthesis), culminating in self-aware Absolute Spirit. This grand secularisation of Christianity into philosophy makes Hegel the "Thomas Aquinas of Protestantism," but three critics expose fatal gaps: Schopenhauer finds the Will absent; Kierkegaard finds that Hegel loses perspectival and participatory knowing, making personal transformation impossible; Marx inverts the dialectic into material class conflict and politicises the project of meaning-making. These critiques collectively become the seedbed for the pseudo-religious ideologies of the 20th century.

## Concepts introduced or developed
- [[Geist]] — introduced: Hegel's term for the self-developing living system of patterns of intelligibility in which mind and reality are identical
- [[Absolute Idealism]] — introduced: reality just is the self-realisation of rational patterns of intelligibility; no thing-in-itself remains
- [[Dialectic]] — developed: extended from Socratic method (Ep 04) to Hegel's historical thesis-antithesis-synthesis dynamic of Geist
- [[Dialectical Materialism]] — introduced via Marx's inversion of Hegel: history as the conflict of socioeconomic classes, not ideas
- [[Totalitarian Ideology]] — introduced: pseudo-religious secular systems that claim to totalise history and guide humanity to a secular utopia
