# Ep 22 - Descartes vs Hobbes

## What this lecture argues
Ep 22 unpacks the Descartes–Hobbes debate as still live and unresolved. Descartes argues that matter — purposeless, meaningless, normatively inert — cannot generate reasoning, consciousness, or the Qualia that constitute experience; this is a rigorous refusal of strong AI, not a retreat to religious sentiment. Yet the cost of Descartes's defence is catastrophic: mind and matter share no properties, making mind-body interaction, knowledge of other minds, and even self-knowledge deeply mysterious. The Cartesian self collapses to an atomic moment of self-awareness adrift in Pascal's infinite spaces. Pascal counter-poses the Spirit of Finesse to the Spirit of Geometry, diagnosing the meaning crisis as the loss of procedural, perspectival, and participatory knowing — the very knowing that makes transformation possible. The episode closes by previewing the pseudo-religious ideologies that will arise to fill the gap.

## Concepts introduced or developed
- [[Mind-Body Problem]] — fully articulated here as the irresolvable consequence of Descartes's successful argument against Hobbes
- [[Qualia]] — introduced: the subjective secondary qualities that consciousness possesses and matter cannot
- [[Spirit of Finesse]] — Pascal's term for the procedural, perspectival, participatory knowing lost in the scientific revolution
- [[Strong AI vs Weak AI]] — Searle's distinction explained: weak AI does useful things; strong AI would require solving all of Descartes's objections
- [[The 4 Kinds of Knowing]] — extended: Pascal is read as lamenting the loss of everything except propositional knowing
