# Ep 50 - Tillich and Barfield

## What this lecture argues
This final episode completes the prophets arc by presenting Paul Tillich and Owen Barfield as integrators of everything built since Ep 46. Tillich synthesizes Heidegger (critique of onto-theology as idolatry), Jung (individuation, depth psychology), and the imaginal tradition (Corbin-adjacent symbol theory) into a practical framework: courage and faith as ultimate concern are the response to the meaning crisis, where faith = participatory/aspirational da'at directed at the inexhaustible ground of being. God is neither a supreme being nor an absence, but the simultaneous ground of meaning, reality, and their relationship — accessible only through an anagogic method of correlation (existential questioning ↔ revelation), and pointing toward the God beyond the God of theism (non-theism). Barfield then provides the final piece: poiesis and final participation — the recovery of the original transjective resonance of participation, integrated with rational science. Where Heidegger took only Eckhart's gelassenheit (letting-be), Barfield adds Durchbruch (breakthrough), making creativity synergistic rather than passively receptive. Vervaeke closes by locating his own relevance realization theory as the missing science of meaning cultivation that completes Barfield's project — the series' final self-placement.

## Concepts introduced or developed
- [[Faith]] — Tillich: not propositional assent but being grasped by ultimate concern; participatory, aspirational, Hebrew da'at structure
- [[Ground of Being]] — Tillich's non-onto-theological name for God; simultaneous ground of meaning, reality, and their interrelation
- [[Method of Correlation]] — Tillich's theology as anagoge between ekstasis (depths of reason) and miracle/mystery (depths of reality); misread as propositional
- [[Non-Theism]] — rejects four shared premises of theism and atheism; "God beyond the God of theism"; applicable to Buddhism and Taoism
- [[Epektasis]] — Gregory of Nyssa/Eriugena: infinite self-transcendence in God's inexhaustibleness; no resting in God; transjectivity and transframing never stop
- [[Onto-Theology]] — Tillich deepens: critique of onto-theology = critique of idolatry; God beyond the God of theism is the explicit response
- [[Infinity of Reality]] — early Romantics via Barfield: inexhaustible moreness that draws finite beings into self-transcendence; finite longing for the infinite (Schlegel)
- [[Poiesis]] — Barfield: participatory creativity as ekstasis; synergistic (neither passive like Gelassenheit nor controlling); found in language, flow, and re-exaptation
- [[Final Participation]] — Barfield: recovery of original participation integrated with rational science; both perspectival/participatory AND science of meaning cultivation
- [[Gelassenheit]] — Heidegger takes this from Eckhart (passivity) but forgets Durchbruch (breakthrough); Barfield corrects this
- [[Divine Double]] — Tillich frames as essential self (normatively ahead, beckoning existential self); connects to S1/S2 and sacred second self language
- [[Aspiration]] — Tillich: theonomy as the resolution to the autonomy/heteronomy paradox; neither pure active making nor pure passive reception
- [[Individuation]] — Tillich puts individuation in tonos with participation in being; individual psychic process must be complemented by ontological participation
