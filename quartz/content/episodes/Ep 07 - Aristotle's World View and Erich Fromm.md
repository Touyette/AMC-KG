# Ep 07 - Aristotle's World View and Erich Fromm

## What this lecture argues
This episode completes Aristotle's contribution by introducing the Conformity Theory of Knowing and the Agent-Arena relationship, then introduces Erich Fromm's Having Mode / Being Mode distinction as a complement and transition to Buddhism. Aristotle's conformity theory holds that to know something is to have the same structural-functional organization (idos) as the thing, making knowing a form of participation and being rather than merely belief. This grounds his geocentric worldview as an "attuned" cosmology — the world is organized by natural motion (purposeful, meaningful movement), and this meshes with the conformity theory to produce Worldview Attunement, a mutual reinforcement between scientific understanding and existential dwelling. From worldview attunement emerges the Agent-Arena relationship: the co-identification of the kind of agent one is with the kind of arena one is in, producing the meta-meaning system in which individual actions take on significance. Vervaeke coins the term "Nomological Order" for a worldview that reliably generates such existential modes. Fromm's Having Mode vs. Being Mode is introduced: the having mode meets needs through categorical control and manipulation (I-It); the being mode meets developmental needs through becoming and expressive encounter (I-Thou). Modal Confusion — trying to meet being needs within the having mode — is introduced as a structural cause of the meaning crisis and as the mythological meaning of Siddhartha's palace. The Three Orders (nomological, normative, narrative) are introduced as the framework whose fragmentation constitutes the meaning crisis.

## Concepts introduced or developed
- [[Conformity Theory of Knowing]] — introduced; knowing as the mind taking on the same structural-functional organization as what is known; contact epistemology
- [[Agent-Arena Relationship]] — introduced; the co-identification of agent type and arena type that constitutes a meta-meaning system
- [[Worldview Attunement]] — introduced; the mutual reinforcement between scientific understanding of the world and existential modes of dwelling
- [[Having Mode]] — introduced via Fromm; existential mode organized around categorical control and problem-solving (I-It relations)
- [[Being Mode]] — introduced via Fromm; existential mode organized around becoming and expressive encounter (I-Thou relations)
- [[Modal Confusion]] — introduced; the error of trying to meet being-needs (maturity, love, growth) within the having mode
- [[Three Orders]] — introduced; the nomological (natural), normative (moral), and narrative (story) orders whose fragmentation produces the meaning crisis
