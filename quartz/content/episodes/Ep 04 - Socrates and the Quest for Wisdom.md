# Ep 04 - Socrates and the Quest for Wisdom

## What this lecture argues
This episode centers on Socrates as the embodiment of the Greek Axial Revolution and introduces the core Socratic understanding of wisdom as the tight coupling of truth-tracking and salience/relevance. Vervaeke shows how Socrates' response to the Delphic Oracle's pronouncement — that no human is wiser than Socrates — led him to a method of systematic cross-examination (elenchus) aimed at exposing how people bullshit themselves by letting what they find salient rush ahead of what they actually understand. The Sophists are introduced as the antithesis of Socrates: they excel at disconnecting relevance from truth through rhetoric. Two forms of failure in knowing are distinguished — the Natural Philosophers give "truth without relevance," the Sophists give "relevance without truth" — and Socratic wisdom is framed as the project of keeping these two coupled. The episode also introduces the concepts of Bullshit (via Frankfurt), Self-Deception, Confirmation Bias, the Socratic Method, and Eros as knowing how to love well (Ta Erotika), ending with Socrates' willingness to die for philosophy as evidence of how central wisdom is to meaningful life.

## Concepts introduced or developed
- [[Socratic Method]] — defined here as systematic cross-examination (elenchus) to expose self-deception and aporia
- [[Bullshit]] — introduced via Frankfurt; disconnection from truth-tracking by capturing salience without concern for truth
- [[Self-Deception]] — explained mechanistically: you bullshit yourself by directing attention to what's salient until it overtakes truth-tracking
- [[Confirmation Bias]] — the bias Socrates is a corrective for; seeking what confirms beliefs rather than what challenges them
- [[Aporia]] — the state of productive perplexity induced by elenchus; prerequisite for genuine transformation
- [[Eros]] — introduced as "ta erotika": Socrates' claim to know how to love well, i.e., how to keep caring rightly coupled to what is real
- [[Wisdom]] — developed further: wisdom as keeping the truth-tracking machinery and relevance/salience machinery tightly coupled
- [[Ontological Depth Perception]] — introduced via Thales; the rational capacity to see into the underlying structure of reality

## Thinkers & traditions
- [[Socrates]] — primary figure; inventor of the Socratic method and paradigm of the philosophical life
- [[Natural Philosophy]] — truth without relevance; Thales as example
- [[Pythagoras]] — reviewed; invents "philosophy" as community of lovers of wisdom
