# Ep 37 - Reverse Engineering Enlightenment Part 2

## What this lecture argues
Ep 37 works through each perennial problem in turn, proposing the practice or practice-set that addresses it, and shows how these practices need to be integrated into a wisdom-framed ecology. The episode constructs the positive case for reverse-engineering enlightenment by naming the cognitive-existential mechanisms at work in each traditional wisdom practice.

**Perennial Problems and their responses** (the full schema):
| Perennial Problem | Level | Practice Response |
|---|---|---|
| Parasitic Processing | Functional / Self-organizing | Counteractive Dynamical System (eightfold path as prototype) |
| Modal Confusion | Functional / Self-identification | Sati — mindfulness practices; remembering the being mode |
| Reflectiveness Gap | Functional / Self-reflection | Flow State — dynamic integration of immersion and reflection; Daoism |
| Absurdity (clash of perspectives) | Structural / Self-world | Prajña / Scientia Intuitiva — non-dual interpenetration of all perspectives |
| Existential Anxiety (inner conflict) | Structural / Self-self | Indwelling the Sage — internalization + indwelling; inner dialogue |
| Alienation | Structural / Self-others | Communitas — collective flow; authentic discourse; circling practices |
| Existential Entrapment | Developmental | Gnosis — open-ended mythos; higher states of consciousness |
| All perennial problems | Framing | Wisdom — the metacognitive style that integrates all of the above |

**Counteractive Dynamical System**: Parasitic processing is a dynamical system; one-shot interventions cannot counter it (it reconfigures around them). The response must be an internalized, self-sustaining, counteractive dynamical system. The goldsmith parable (Buddha): gold requires all three — looking (awareness of imperfections, but no change), heating (energy, effort, something like flow), and hammering (shaping, reconfiguring skills). Each alone fails; together they produce a higher-order capacity. The eightfold path as a self-rolling wheel is a prototypical example: practices of right aspiration, right mindfulness, right concentration etc. are complementary — each has strengths the others cover — and over time they begin to interact, reinforce each other, and become a counteractive dynamical system that takes on a life of its own (Ep 37).

**Flow state and the reflectiveness gap**: Velleman proposes Daoism as the philosophical resolution to the reflectiveness gap, and Vervaeke agrees: Daoism is the religion of flow — yin/yang, making-frame/breaking-frame. The flow state is the state in which immersion (wantonness) and reflection (Hamlet) are dynamically, optimally integrated — not at a fixed midpoint but as a living oscillation (Ep 37).

**Prajña / Scientia Intuitiva and absurdity**: Absurdity is a clash of perspectives (self-world); the remedy is not an argument (the argumentative generators of absurdity are invalid) but a cognitive-existential state that dissolves the clash. Prajña (DT Suzuki: simultaneously looking as deeply in as you can and as deeply out as you can) and Spinoza's scientia intuitiva (seeing the whole of an argument in each premise AND each premise in the whole — bottom-up and top-down fully interpenetrating, like letters ↔ words ↔ sentences simultaneously) both describe a state of **non-duality** in which the perspectival clash is resolved by the interpenetration of all perspectives. "The eye by which I see God is the same eye by which God sees me" (Eckhart). This state carries a characteristic joy analogous to humor's insight-flash, but at the highest level (Ep 37).

**Indwelling the Sage and anxiety**: Inner conflict generates nebulous anxiety because the various centers of the psyche are at war with no common ground. The remedy: build a capacity for inner dialogue through (1) *internalization* — making the sage's perspective part of one's metacognitive machinery; and (2) *indwelling* (Polanyi) — practicing what it is like to see from the sage's perspective, so it becomes a skill one can inhabit rather than an idea one holds. Jung's active imagination, Lectio Divina, and the Stoic practice of asking "what would Socrates do?" are all variants of this. The goal: the ability, as Antisthenes described learning from Socrates, "to converse with yourself" (Ep 37).

**Communitas and alienation**: Turner (and Durkheim's concept of collective effervescence): communitas = collective flow in which participants feel real communion and communication, and experience a shared identity beyond their individual roles. Contemporary expressions: circling practices, authentic discourse movement (Peter Limberg), Jordan Hall's "coherence." These address alienation by creating a communal dynamical system that marshals distributed cognition and helps people access non-propositional modes of knowing together (Ep 37).
