# Ep 46 - Conclusion and the Prophets of the Meaning Crisis

## What this lecture argues
Ep 46 has two movements. First, it synthesizes the entire wisdom-enlightenment-religio-sacredness nexus into a single practical schema: the **Wise Cultivation of Enlightenment (WCE)** — wisdom integrated with enlightenment, enhanced religio, and encounter with the sacred, situated within a naturalistic worldview and a co-op network of communities of practice coordinated by a wisdom wiki. This is Vervaeke's positive answer to how we can individually and collectively awaken from the meaning crisis. Second, it opens a new arc: a "prophets" survey — thinkers who diagnosed the meaning crisis and proposed responses. The lecture introduces Husserl's phenomenology as an attempt to restore **contact epistemology** (direct engagement with the world via disciplined attention to intentionality) and then Heidegger's critique: Husserl failed because he missed **participatory knowing** and remained trapped within the Cartesian cultural cognitive grammar. Heidegger's corrective is **Dasein** — the concept that we are the beings whose being is in question, which simultaneously opens access to our participatory knowing (existential modes) and to ontology itself.

**WCE Schema**: Wise Cultivation of Enlightenment, situated within (a) a scientific worldview (run off RR machinery; consistent with 4E cognitive science; secular but not hostile to religion) and (b) a co-op network of communities of practice in reflective equilibrium with a wisdom wiki (bottom-up practitioner input ↔ top-down researcher input). This already exists in nascent form (existing contemplative/wisdom communities). The schema shows how wisdom and enlightenment overlap: both produce a counteractive dynamical system against parasitic processing. Wisdom additionally enhances religio → sacredness → meaning in life (Ep 46).

**Prophets network roadmap**: Heidegger is central (Husserl, Eckhart's gnosis, Kant as influences; existentialism, postmodernism, speculative realism, Kyoto School as downstream effects). Jung and Tillich share the Gnosis background — both view the psyche as sacrament (Dourley's argument); both are non-theists. Corbin is deeply influenced by Heidegger and Iranian Sufi sources; key for the imaginal and divine double. Barfield draws on German Romanticism (Schlegel, Coleridge) and Gnosis. Nishitani's *Religion and Nothingness* is Vervaeke's highest-ranked response to the meaning crisis from outside the Western tradition — requires knowing Heidegger, William James, and Buddhism to read (Ep 46).

**Husserl's phenomenology**: Not mere introspection — a disciplined, reflective, experimental, exploratory, probative attention paid to the **transjective relationship** (how consciousness and world are co-constituted). Husserl's keyword is **intentionality**: any mental directedness — perceiving, acting, thinking — is always directed at something (noesis ↔ noema). The goal is to get back to "things themselves" — to a contact epistemology after the Cartesian subjectivization had severed us from the world. Phenomenology influenced 4E cognitive science precisely because it models the transjective structure of meaning (Ep 46).

**Heidegger's two criticisms of Husserl**: (1) Husserl did not give us genuine contact because he did not account for participatory knowing — the modal way in which agent and arena are fundamentally co-constituted. Merleau-Ponty partly repairs this via embodiment. (2) Husserl remained trapped in the Cartesian cultural cognitive grammar (explicitly titles a book *Cartesian Meditations*) — ultimately still operating from within subjectivity, making his "realism" ultimately a kind of idealism. Heidegger's response: phenomenologically explore what grounds the intentional structure — i.e., explore *being* (Ep 46).

**Dasein**: Heidegger's neologism for the human being. *Da-sein* = "being-there" — thrown into existence, flowing into the world. The decisive point: **Dasein is the being whose being is in question**. Unlike a gazelle (born as a gazelle, essence set) or a water bottle (its being is not in question to it), a person's who-and-what is undetermined. Existence precedes essence (existentialism). By phenomenologically exploring Dasein's being, we simultaneously access (a) our existential-participatory modality (the being mode) and (b) the question of being itself. This is Heidegger's bridge: self-knowledge of Dasein is also ontological questioning. This breaks us out of the Cartesian grammar that cut us off from participatory knowing (Ep 46).
