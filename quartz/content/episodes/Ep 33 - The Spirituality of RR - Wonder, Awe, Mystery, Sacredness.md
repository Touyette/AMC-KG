# Ep 33 - The Spirituality of RR - Wonder, Awe, Mystery, Sacredness

## What this lecture argues
Ep 33 pivots from the naturalistic account of RR (Eps 26–32) to its spiritual significance. The core move: the entire right-hand side of the convergent RR diagram — self-transcendence, connectedness, perspectival/participatory knowing, caring, altered and higher states — is what Vervaeke calls **Religio**. RR is pre-conceptual, pre-propositional, pre-inferential, pre-egoic, and pre-normative: it makes possible everything downstream (concepts, beliefs, ego, normative judgments of truth/goodness/beauty) without being reducible to any of it. This primordiality, depth, and groundedness is exactly what human beings have historically experienced as spiritual.

**Wonder and Awe** (drawing on Paolo Costa and Robert Fuller) are the non-focal, participatory-perspectival states through which we become aware of Religio. Where curiosity (having mode) is focal and problem-solving, wonder (being mode) is open, non-focal, and participatory — a sense of being immersed in the "atmosphere of significance." Awe is wonder scaled up: it pushes toward ongoing accommodation, toward the inexhaustibleness of the combinatorial explosive nature of reality. Together they induce the trajectory of transframing — not solving a focal problem but disclosing the machinery of Religio itself.

**Mystery**: Vervaeke carefully distinguishes phenomenological mystery from theoretical inexplicability. RR is phenomenologically mysterious because you can never make the framing process itself a focal object — every attempt to frame the framing process leaves the framing outside the frame (James's I/Me distinction: I always see *by means of* the I, never the I itself). But this does not imply theoretical inexplicability — one can theorize about what one cannot exemplify (as a theory of light need not itself be light).

**Sacredness** (psycho-existential): citing Geertz, sacredness functions as a *meta-meaning* system — not a specific meaning but the condition for all meaning systems. It homes us and the world together, protecting against domicide/horror/alienation and enabling worldview attunement. But Vervaeke criticizes Geertz's account as necessary but insufficient: sacredness is also transgressive (the Gnostic side), pointing to something beyond mere worldview attunement. This leads to Otto's **Numinous** (to be developed in Ep 34): the primordial experience of sacredness that involves awe, dread, and wonder at the transgressive, uncanny side of the sacred.

**The Sacred vs. Sacredness**: The Sacred is the metaphysical proposal (what grounds sacredness? — typically answered as something supernatural). Sacredness is the psycho-existential proposal (what is the experience of encountering the sacred?). Vervaeke is working from the psycho-existential side and will later offer an alternative to the standard supernatural metaphysical proposal.

## Concepts introduced or developed
- [[Religio]] — introduced: the whole right side of the RR diagram; pre-egoic, pre-normative binding; derived from 'religare' (to bind together); the machinery of fundamentally grounded meaning-making
- [[Wonder]] — introduced: being-mode, non-focal, participatory-perspectival awareness of Religio; contrasted with curiosity (having mode); induces accommodation and openness to inexhaustibility
- [[Awe]] — introduced: wonder scaled up; produces ongoing accommodation; sense of inexhaustibleness of reality; draws toward transframing trajectory
- [[Mystery]] — introduced: phenomenological mystery of RR (can never focal-ize the framing process itself); carefully distinguished from theoretical inexplicability; James's I/Me distinction
- [[Sacredness]] — introduced: psycho-existential account; Geertz's meta-meaning function; homing against horror and domicide; worldview attunement; but more than that (transgressive, numinous)
- [[The Sacred]] — introduced: the metaphysical question (what grounds sacredness?); standard answer = supernatural; Vervaeke will offer an alternative
- [[Numinous]] — introduced (to be developed in Ep 34): Otto's term; primordial experience of sacredness before moral/health associations; linked to glory not moral righteousness; involves the transgressive/uncanny side of the sacred
