# Ep 45 - The Nature of Wisdom

## What this lecture argues
Ep 45 presents the Vervaeke & Ferraro (2013) process theory of wisdom, introduces sophrosyne as its overarching cognitive style, critiques the model's lacunae (no participatory knowing, no theory of understanding, no account of aspiration), develops a two-dimensional account of profound understanding (horizontal: plausibility generation; vertical: aligning the 4 kinds of knowing), and introduces Callard's aspiration as incremental transformative knowing — arriving at wisdom's final definition.

**Vervaeke & Ferraro (2013) model**: A process theory of wisdom structured around three interacting competences: (1) AOM + Inferential competence (propositional knowing; sophia; overcoming fallacious reasoning); (2) Mindfulness + Insight competence (procedural knowing; phronesis; overcoming misconstrual/misframing); (3) Perspectival knowing/Internalization (takes 1 and 2, integrates them via internalizing the sage; overcoming egocentrism). All three directed toward an optimized relevance realization and regulated by sophrosyne. Rules (propositional) + Routines (procedural) + Roles (perspectival). Directed toward morality, meaning in life, mastery (Ep 45).

**Sophrosyne vs. enkrateia**: The ancient Greeks distinguished two ways of practicing virtue. Enkrateia (self-control): Tom fights the temptation to lie through willpower — the temptation arises and is resisted. Sophrosyne: Susan finds lying "unthinkable" in Frankfurt's sense — not because she suppresses it but because her salience landscape has been so fundamentally reoriented that the temptation doesn't arise. Sophrosyne is the perfected, second-nature disposition — like an adult regarding children's toys: they are simply not salient as temptations. Augustine: "Love God and do what you want" — if agape flows through you (sophrosyne's highest form), you will naturally be tempted toward the good. Sophrosyne is an optimization of perspectival knowing directed toward virtue, meaning, and mastery. The sage's salience landscape naturally organizes toward seeing through illusion and into reality (Ep 45).

**Lacunae in Vervaeke & Ferraro**: (1) No account of participatory knowing → cannot incorporate transformative experience, gnosis, altered states of consciousness; cannot explain how wisdom connects to identity-level change. (2) Relationship between kinds of knowing underdeveloped (perspectival knowing naively positioned as integrator of propositional and procedural, but the full 4-level model is more complex). (3) Missing theory of understanding. (4) Missing explicit account of aspiration. Wisdom not connected to gnosis (Ep 45).

**Understanding**: De Regt's "effectiveness standard": understanding is not grasping the truth but effectively grasping the relevant implications and connections — which is why scientific idealizations (the circle-and-rings atom diagram) work to generate understanding without being true. Basic understanding = good construal → optimal grip → grasping relevance connections; context-sensitive and context-relative. Profound understanding adds a horizontal dimension (generating plausibility: convergence of multiple independent lines of evidence into a coherent construal) and a vertical dimension (aligning propositional, procedural, perspectival, and participatory knowing — grounding downward from propositional to participatory; emergence upward) (Ep 45).

**Aspiration and proleptic rationality (Callard)**: Callard (2016) argues that transformative knowing can be incremental (aspiration) as well as sudden (inspiration/LA Paul). Aspiration = the rational process of trying to become someone who has values one does not yet have. Music appreciation paradox: if you were a good student, you'd already appreciate music for its own sake — but then you wouldn't need the class. Aspiration breaks this by: (a) proleptic rationality — one's future (S2) self normatively guides current (S1) self before the transformation is complete; (b) placeholder/symbol — an interim value that connects the future you to the current you, allowing entry into the liminal space where new values can be explored. Aspiring to rationality or wisdom cannot itself be irrational — that would be a performative contradiction. Wonder is deeply connected to aspiration (opens up, motivates identity questioning). Aspiration connects to gnosis (entering existential entrapment, using symbols to navigate out) (Ep 45).

**Wisdom — final definition**: "An ecology of psychotechnologies and cognitive styles that dynamically enhance relevance realization in inference, insight and intuition, internalization, understanding, and gnosis, transformation, and aspiration." All of these are ways RR is enhanced at increasingly deep levels: inference (propositional), insight (procedural/perspectival), internalization (perspectival/social), understanding (cognitive-integration), gnosis (participatory/transformative), aspiration (prospective, identity-aspiring). This ecology is counteractive to self-deception machinery and affords self-organized transformation toward a deeply meaningful life (Ep 45).

- [[Aspiration]] — introduced: Callard; incremental transformative knowing; proleptic rationality; placeholder; wonder + gnosis

- [[Transformative Experience]] — deepened: aspiration (incremental) complements inspiration (sudden/LA Paul); both involve non-inferential identity change
