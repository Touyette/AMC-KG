# Ep 10 - Consciousness

## What this lecture argues
This episode develops the cognitive scientific account of consciousness and introduces the concept of Relevance Realization as the core function of consciousness. Drawing on Global Workspace Theory (Baars/Shanahan), Integrated Information Theory (Tononi), and Bor & Seth's work, Vervaeke argues that what consciousness fundamentally does is help the organism zero in on relevant information — selecting from a vast array of environmental stimuli, memory, and combinatorial possibilities. This process creates a Salience Landscape (featurization, foregrounding, figuration, framing), within which the agent achieves an Optimal Grip through sizing up, generating a Presence Landscape of affordances, and then a Depth Landscape of causal patterns. Together these constitute a Significance Landscape. The problem of Ontonormativity is then introduced: higher states of consciousness are experienced as "more real" and normatively demand transformation, even though they are temporary, ineffable, and do not cohere with everyday experience in the way one uses to reject dreams. Vervaeke ends by arguing that higher states of consciousness are rational not because they provide new propositional knowledge but because they improve wisdom — they transform the entire salience/significance landscape, analogous to the child developing past the conservation illusion. The goal is to work out a descriptive and prescriptive account of this transformation.

## Concepts introduced or developed
- [[Relevance Realization]] — introduced as the core function of consciousness; zeroing in on relevant information
- [[Salience Landscape]] — developed as a four-layer model: featurization → foregrounding → figuration/framing → significance
- [[Optimal Grip]] — introduced via Dreyfus/Merleau-Ponty; dynamic adjustment to achieve most adequate engagement; the process of "sizing up"
- [[Ontonormativity]] — introduced; higher states of consciousness present reality as more real and normatively demand transformation
- [[Higher States of Consciousness]] — developed; distinguished from ordinary altered states by their ontonormative quality
- [[Insight]] — further developed as "systematic insight" = insight of consciousness rather than in consciousness

## Thinkers & traditions
- [[Siddhartha Gautama]] — his awakening re-described as a systematic insight of consciousness
- [[Plato]] — the meta-drives (fullness of being, contact with reality) are what the ontonormativity of HSC switches on
