# Ep 32 - RR in the Brain, Insight, and Consciousness

## What this lecture argues
Ep 32 grounds the theory of relevance realization in plausible neural mechanisms, connecting **firing** (Self-Organizing Criticality) and **wiring** (Small World Networks) to g, consciousness, and insight — while arguing these two mechanisms mutually reinforce each other.

**Self-Organizing Criticality (SOC)**: When neurons fire in synchrony, they are doing compression/integration. When they go asynchronous (the "neural avalanche"), they are doing particularization/differentiation. This oscillation is scale-invariant in the brain — happening at every level from individual neuron clusters to the whole brain — which makes it a plausible implementation of RR. The sand-dune analogy (Per Bak): grains self-organize into a mound, avalanche, rebuild on a broader base with emergent capacity. Thatcher et al. found that greater flexibility in the synchrony/asynchrony oscillation predicts higher intelligence — convergent with RR = g.

**Small World Networks (SWN)**: Of three network topologies — regular (resilient but inefficient), random/chaotic (efficient but not resilient), and small world (optimizes both) — the brain tends toward small world networks. SWN is the most optimal for the efficiency-resiliency trade-off. Langer et al. found SWN structure correlates with higher general intelligence; Monti et al. found that consciousness is lost as the brain breaks down from SWN into local regular networks, and regains consciousness as SWN reforms. Insight (Schilling, 2005) is when a regular network is converted into a small world network — a sudden optimization of RR, accompanied by a flash of salience and altered consciousness.

**SOC and SWN mutually reinforce**: the more the brain fires in SOC patterns, the more it wires as a SWN; the more it wires as a SWN, the more it fires in SOC patterns. Together they constitute the neural machinery of RR.

Finally, Vervaeke expands the **salience landscape** to its three-dimensional structure: Aspectuality (A — things are always experienced as aspects, not wholes), Centrality (C — experience is always indexed on the subject; "hereness"), and Temporality (T — timing is constitutive; "nowness"). This ACT structure gives consciousness its phenomenological signature: hereness, nowness, togetherness. And at the core of the whole system is **caring** — RR is never cold calculation; affect, motivation, and caring are structurally present throughout.
