# Ep 28 - Convergence To Relevance Realization

## What this lecture argues
Ep 28 builds a sustained convergence argument that Relevance Realization is the core of intelligence, drawing on categorisation, communication, and action in turn. Nelson Goodman's argument shows that logical similarity is indefinitely large; only psychological similarity — zeroing in on relevant features — enables categorisation. Grice's maxims of communication all reduce to a single maxim of relevance (per Sperber and Wilson). The robot battery problem introduces the Frame Problem: the proliferation of side effects makes action impossible unless the agent can somehow ignore irrelevant ones without checking them — Relevance Realization is required. Working Memory (Hasher) and Selective Attention are shown to function as relevance filters. The convergence argument is then positioned as the ground from which Relevance Realization will be shown to explain consciousness, wisdom, meaning, and self-transcendence.

## Concepts introduced or developed
- [[Relevance Realization]] — convergence argument completed: categorisation, communication, action, working memory, and selective attention all converge on RR as their core
- [[Frame Problem]] — introduced: how a cognitive system determines which side effects of action are relevant; the deeper version (Shanahan's Relevance Problem) cannot be solved by a relevance-definition
- [[Working Memory]] — introduced: Hasher's account of working memory as a relevance filter that screens irrelevant information from deeper processing
- Selective Attention — introduced in the RR framework: the first stage of zeroing in on relevant environmental information
- [[Combinatorial Explosion]] — extended: the proliferation of side effects is another instance of combinatorial explosion requiring RR to navigate
