# Ep 26 - Cognitive Science

## What this lecture argues
Ep 26 introduces Vervaeke's vision of cognitive science as Synoptic Integration: not Generic Nominalism (a label for related disciplines) nor Interdisciplinary Eclecticism (polite borrowing), but the creation of plausible, potentially profound constructs that bridge across the levels of neuroscience, AI, psychology, linguistics, and anthropology. The key methodological tool is Plausibility: a construct is plausible when it is both trustworthy (produced through convergent, independent lines of investigation) and elegant/multi-apt (capable of bridging many domains with insight). The episode then introduces the problem of intelligence as General Problem Solver, following the seminal work of Newell and Simon, and sets up the Problem Space formalism (initial state, goal state, operators, path constraints) as the frame through which the next several episodes will show that Relevance Realization is the core of intelligence. Meaning Cultivation is proposed as the preferred metaphor over "meaning making."

## Concepts introduced or developed
- [[Synoptic Integration]] — introduced: Vervaeke's definition of cognitive science as profound bridging across disciplines, addressing equivocation, fragmentation, and ignorance of causal relations between levels
- [[Multi-Aptness]] — introduced: property of a plausible theoretical construct that bridges across many domains insightfully
- [[Plausibility]] — developed: Rescher-inspired account; balance between convergence (trustworthiness/bias reduction) and elegance (multi-aptness)
- [[Meaning Cultivation]] — introduced as preferred metaphor: meaning neither purely made (Romantic) nor found (Empiricist) but cultivated between agent and world
- [[Heuristics]] — introduced formally via Newell and Simon: non-algorithmic, fallible strategies that avoid combinatorial explosion by biasing the search space
- [[Relevance Realization]] — convergence argument begins: general problem solving requires zeroing in on relevant information
