# Ep 02 - Flow, Metaphor, and the Axial Revolution

## What this lecture argues

Vervaeke argues that the Upper Paleolithic Transition (~40,000 BCE) was not biological but cognitive: shamanism emerged as the first set of psycho-technologies for inducing the flow state, which in turn generates an insight cascade and enhanced implicit learning, culminating in a massive capacity for metaphorical cognition. This enhanced meaning-making machinery gave shamanic groups a decisive adaptive advantage. The lecture then traces the same logic forward: the Bronze Age collapse (~1200 BCE) creates a cognitive bottleneck that, like the Paleolithic one, forces radical experimentation. The result is the Axial Revolution (~800–300 BCE), driven by two new psycho-technologies — alphabetic literacy and coinage — whose exapted products are second-order thinking and abstract symbolic reasoning. People now become aware both of their capacity for self-transcendence and of their capacity for self-deception, and this double awareness is the engine of Axial Age philosophy and religion.
