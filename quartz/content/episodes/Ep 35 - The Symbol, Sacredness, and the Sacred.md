# Ep 35 - The Symbol, Sacredness, and the Sacred

## What this lecture argues
Ep 35 completes and crystallizes the theory of the symbol, grounds it in Mythos as the psycho-technology for Sacredness, and then makes the central metaphysical move of the whole series: arguing against the supernatural account of The Sacred and proposing an alternative grounded in the transjective coupling of two inexhaustiblenesses.

**Symbol structure — EPIC**: Vervaeke synthesizes his account into an acronym: symbols are **Ek-static** (they help one stand beyond oneself — from *ek-stasis*, to stand outside), **Participatory** (they work by getting you to participate in what they symbolize, not merely represent it), **Integrative-anagogic** (they integrate the agent *and* the arena in a coupled, self-transcending feedback cycle — not just part-to-whole integration but Anagoge), and **Complex** (the reality they disclose is multifaceted, not a single static thing; symbols like Athena draw together wisdom, weaving, and warfare simultaneously — reconfiguring the salience landscape). The EPIC quality is what makes symbols capable of driving meta-assimilation (worldview attunement) and meta-accommodation (numinous encounter) simultaneously (Ep 35).

**Symbolic resonance**: the core dynamics of how a symbol works. Using Michael Polanyi's framework: in normal focal/subsidiary awareness, one integrates subsidiary processes into focal awareness of an object of interest. In symbolic engagement, the symbol *is* the focal object but the real interest is in the integration process itself. The symbol then does something more: it discloses an aspect of reality (breath → impermanence; balance scales → justice). Then: more disclosure → more powerful integration → more powerful disclosure → more powerful integration → Anagoge. The symbol functions as a *resonant* feedback loop between inner integration and outer disclosure; this is "symbolic resonance." One gets increasingly sensitized to the symbol precisely because one gets more integrated, and more integrated because more sensitized (Ep 35). The Buba/Kiki cross-modal experiment (Ramachandran) illustrates that humans are pre-consciously, automatically symbolic animals — the cerebellar integration of different sensory modalities happens spontaneously.

**Mythos**: Vervaeke distinguishes *mythos* from "myth" (which connotes mere fictional story). Mythos = the enmeshment of symbols, story, and ritual. Ritual here is *enactive analogy / enactive Anagoge* (not neurotic routine): the processes by which one enacts and activates the machinery of transformation. Story connects symbols to perspectival and participatory knowing and makes them enactable. Mythos *about Religio* — activating, articulating, accentuating, and celebrating Religio itself — *is* Sacredness. This works because RR is constitutively set up to find itself inherently interesting (it is self-organizing, self-correcting, and self-transcending; it would not be RR if it were not interested in itself). So Mythos that activates Religio is doubly valuable: Religio is foundational to agency, and RR finds the activation of its own deepest machinery intrinsically fascinating (Ep 35).

**The Sacred — rejection of supernatural essentialism**: the standard metaphysical proposal is that The Sacred = that which is supernatural (above nature), and its supernatural status means it is absolutely, always, essentially relevant. Vervaeke argues this commits a **category mistake**: it supposes there is an *essence* to relevance — a thing that is always and absolutely relevant. But relevance realization has no essence; it is a process, not a product, and nothing — not even RR itself — is absolutely relevant to it (RR can be hijacked so that one no longer finds one's own existence salient). Confusing products of RR with the process of RR is exactly the kind of cognitive error that produces meaning-traps (Ep 35).

**The Sacred — alternative proposal**: The Sacred is the **transjective relationship between two inexhaustiblenesses**: (1) the combinatorially explosive, no-thing-ness of Reality — reality always transcends any framing of it, nothing fully captures it, it is never a static object; (2) the no-thing-ness of the self — the 'I' that can never be fully framed, the ongoing process of RR that is constituted by its own evolution and never arrives at completion. These two inexhaustiblenesses are deeply, primordially coupled at the level of Religio. The Sacred is this coupling — the inexhaustible fount that powers the experience of Sacredness and drives the trajectory of transframing. This is compatible with what many mystics were pointing at without requiring the supernatural category (Ep 35).
