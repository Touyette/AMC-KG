# Ep 42 - Intelligence, Rationality, and Wisdom

## What this lecture argues
Ep 42 deepens the account of rationality and begins the transition to wisdom. It develops the full S1/S2 dual processing model, introduces cognitive leaping as the valuable (not merely problematic) dimension of S1, shows that AOM and mindfulness are opponent cognitive styles suited to different contexts, demonstrates the existential character of rationality via Dweck's mindset research, and proposes the recursive schema Intelligence→Rationality→Wisdom.

**Psychotechnology — precise definition**: A socially generated and standardized way of formatting, manipulating, and enhancing information processing that is readily internalizable into human cognition and can be applied in a domain-general manner. Must reliably extend cognition across domains and be highly generalizable. Prototypical instances: literacy, numeracy, graphing. This distinguishes psychotechnology from domain-specific tools — the key criterion is domain-generality and internalizability (Ep 42).

**Problem finding and the problem nexus**: Arlin (1990) argues that problem *finding* — the capacity to identify significant problems that others have not yet seen — is central to wisdom (not just creativity). Vervaeke extends this: the mark of a genuinely great problem finder is the capacity to generate a *problem nexus* — a core problem whose solution would cascade and make significant impact on multiple existing problems in the space. Vervaeke's own career move is an example: identifying relevance realization as a problem nexus for cognition (how we deal with combinatorial explosion, how we do insight, how we achieve flow, what self-deception is) that cascades across multiple domains (Ep 42).

**Wonder vs. curiosity — Plato/Aristotle distinction**: Both Plato and Aristotle agree that philosophy begins in wonder, but they differ on what wonder does. For *Plato*, wonder should be deepened into awe (meta-accommodation) — wonder is the engine of anagoge; philosophy's goal is to sustain and intensify it. For *Aristotle*, wonder should be shaped into curiosity (meta-assimilation) — resolved into answers to questions; philosophy stabilizes and integrates. Wonder (being mode: open, participatory, atmospheric) vs. curiosity (having mode: focal, problem-targeting, information-seeking). Need for cognition requires both (Ep 42).

**Dual Processing fully developed**: S1 (intuitive, associational, implicit, fast, coping, caring — the background mode operative in most behavior) vs. S2 (deliberate, inferential, explicit, slow, planning, theorizing — relies heavily on working memory). S2 has a corrective/override function for S1. But S1 also does valuable work: cognitive leaping. Treating S1 as purely problematic (interference to be suppressed) misses this (Ep 42).

**Cognitive leaping (Baker-Sennett & Ceci)**: The capacity to leap from minimal cues to an accurate Gestalt of the final pattern. Tested by presenting partially-revealed patterns and measuring how few cues are needed to accurately predict the whole. Directly predictive of insight problem-solving. This is the positive, insight-generating dimension of S1 leaping. Trying to suppress all leaping (through AOM) would undermine insight capacity. Trade-off: leaping-to-conclusions (S1 interference with S2 = problem) vs. leaping-to-insight (S1 completing Gestalts = resource) (Ep 42).

**AOM vs. Mindfulness — two opponent cognitive styles**: Active open-mindedness (Baron/Stanovich): foregrounds S2, protects it from S1 interference — suited to *theorizing* contexts where explicit inferential rigor is needed. Mindfulness (Teasdale/Jacobs): foregrounds S1, constrains S2 (inner speech, deliberate inference) — suited to *therapeutic/insight* contexts where transformative reconstrual is needed. Both involve training attention but in opposite directions. Wisdom requires coordination of both — knowing which to foreground in which context (Ep 42).

**Mindset and the existential aspect of rationality (Dweck)**: Fixed mindset (intelligence is a static trait): error = permanent revelation of defect → avoid challenge → produce self-deception (40% lie about performance) → performance worsens after exposure to hard problems. Malleable mindset: error = signal to improve skills and effort → seek challenge → honest self-report → performance improves. Triggered by: trait praise ("you're so smart") → fixed mode; process praise ("you're working really well") → malleable mode. Conclusion: how you identify with your cognitive processes has profound effects on your reasoning, problem-seeking, and self-deception. Rationality is an existential issue, not merely an information-processing one (Ep 42).

**Intelligence → Rationality → Wisdom**: Using intelligence to improve intelligence (recursively) = rationality. Using rationality to improve rationality (recursively) = wisdom. Rationality is highly malleable (unlike raw intelligence). Stanovich's practical implication: we care too much about intelligence and not enough about rationality, even though rationality is what can be cultivated (Ep 42).
