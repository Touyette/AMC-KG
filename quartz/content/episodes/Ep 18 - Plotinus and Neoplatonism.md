# Ep 18 - Plotinus and Neoplatonism

## What this lecture argues
Ep 18 completes the Gnostic account and then presents Plotinus as the grand unified field theory of ancient spirituality. The Gnostic mythology (Demiurge as evil overlord, divine spark, God beyond all gods) is a mythological scaffolding for the enacted anagoge already described — a narrative framework for getting people to stop serving the cultural patterns that entrap them and to seek self-transcendence. The dark side: Gnosticism tends toward conspiracy thinking (those who worship the evil Demiurge are agents of our prison), which historically fed into anti-Semitism and Nazism. The task is to salvage gnosis from Gnosticism.

Plotinus integrates Plato's anagoge, Aristotle's theory of knowing (conformity/levels of actuality), and Stoic therapy into a single unified system. His key insight: **things are more real as they are more unified and integrated**. Understanding something means finding the underlying integration of its parts. The anagogic ascent is therefore simultaneously an epistemological and ontological process — as the agent conforms to deeper levels of reality, they become more integrated and more real themselves. At the apex is **The One** — not a being but the principle by which everything is and is known; like light, which is invisible but makes everything visible; one cannot know The One propositionally but only *be* one — this is the highest gnosis. In Plotinus, spirituality, science, and therapy are completely integrated; there is no split between them.

## Concepts introduced or developed
- [[The One]] — introduced: Plotinus's apex principle; not knowable as an object (thought breaks things apart) but only accessible through becoming unified; the principle by which everything is integrated and real; gnosis of The One = being one
- [[Anagoge]] — deepened: Plotinus grounds it in a full metaphysics of levels of reality; anagogic ascent is simultaneously becoming more integrated as a self AND conforming to more integrated levels of reality; self-realization as making real and becoming aware of what's more real
- [[Neoplatonism]] — introduced as a tradition: Plotinus's synthesis of Plato, Aristotle, and Stoic therapy; the no-split between spirituality, science, and psychotherapy; direct influence on Christianity, Gnosticism, Augustine, and (later) Corbin, Barfield
