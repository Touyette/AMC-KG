# Ep 49 - Corbin and Jung

## What this lecture argues
Ep 49 has two major movements. First, it completes the analysis of aspiration by confronting Strawson's **paradox of self-creation** (genuine self-creation requires both continuity S1=S2 AND real novelty S1≠S2, which seems contradictory) and resolving it through Callard's non-logical identity plus a crucial asymmetry: S1 is temporally prior (causes S2) but S2 is normatively primary (only S2 can justify S1's aspirational actions). The divine double is renamed the **sacred second self** (to avoid theism-binding and allow non-theistic parallels: Buddha nature, Atman/Brahman). Second, it introduces Carl Jung as the next prophet, bridging from Corbin via the sacred second self: Jung's *Modern Man in Search of a Soul* addresses the same meaning crisis; his **individuation** process is the psychological articulation of the aspiration to the sacred second self. Jung replaces Freud's hydraulic (Newtonian) model with an organic/autopoietic one; **archetypes** are virtual engines of psychic self-organization (imaginal, not imaginary). The lecture ends by identifying Jung's limitation (intra-psychic/Kantian — doesn't capture the transjective dimension) and gesturing toward the synthesis of Corbin, Jung, and Buber that Vervaeke proposes.

**Strawson's paradox of self-creation**: For genuine self-creation, two things are simultaneously required: (1) Continuity: S1 and S2 must be the same self, or it is not *self*-creation (random external change); (2) Real novelty: S2 must be fundamentally different from S1, or it is not *creation* (just more of the same). These requirements contradict: S1=S2 AND S1≠S2. The two collapse options: empiricism (radical novelty, no underlying self — the blank slate is rewritten from outside) or romantic expressionism (pure continuity, no novelty — the self just expresses what was always already there). Strawson claims self-creation is self-contradictory; Callard and Vervaeke disagree (Ep 49).

**Resolution via non-logical identity and participation**: The paradox dissolves when you replace logical identity with non-logical identity (A becomes A' through genuine transformation — same person, not same properties). And the relationship between S1 and S2 is neither passive reception nor active making but *participation in emergence*: S2 emerges out of S1 such that S1 disappears into S2. Neither empiricism (all novelty) nor romanticism (all continuity) captures this (Ep 49).

**S1 temporally prior, S2 normatively primary**: S1 causally produces S2 (S1's actions are necessary for the emergence). But S2 is normatively primary — only S2 can justify, explain, and legitimate S1's aspirational actions. Only the person who has come to appreciate classical music can explain *why* the earlier self was rational in pursuing music appreciation. The aspired-to self exists as a symbolic/imaginal self that anagogically reorders the current self: I internalize the symbolic self, begin indwelling the world differently, which allows me to re-internalize the self more deeply — and this coupled loop is the participation in emergence (Ep 49).

**Sacred second self**: Vervaeke renames the divine double as the *sacred second self* to: (a) avoid committing to theism (the divine double is bound to traditional religious imagery); (b) allow the same structure to appear in non-theistic traditions — Buddha nature (the ultimate nature things aspire to), Atman/Brahman in Vedanta. The "sacred second self" may be what the concept of "soul" was always trying to name — not the ghost in the machine but the self one is aspiring through and to (Ep 49).

**Carl Jung and individuation**: The bridge from Corbin: the sacred second self is what Jung means by "soul" in *Modern Man in Search of a Soul*. The loss of the soul = the loss of a real relationship to the sacred second self. Jung replaces Freud's hydraulic model (psyche as Newtonian pressure machine) with an organic/autopoietic model (psyche as self-organizing dynamical system). Individuation is the lifelong process by which the ego — the archetype of the conscious mind — enters into a dialogue with the Self (the archetype of the archetypes, the ultimate virtual engine of psychic self-organization = the sacred second self) through the imaginal archetypes. As the ego engages the archetypes, its perspectival knowing and participatory being are fundamentally reoriented — it individuates anagogically (Ep 49).

**Archetypes**: Jung's psychological analog of Platonic forms. Archetypes are NOT mere images in the mind (that is the imaginary misreading); they are structural-functional organizations — virtual engines — that regulate how the psyche self-organizes by shaping salience landscaping. The hero archetype, active in someone, does not mean they carry hero-images in their head; it means their salience landscape is being reorganized toward heroic forms of being. Archetypes are best understood adverbially (a way of anagogically coming to be) rather than adjectivally (an inner thing one possesses). Like the Self, they are autopoietic — they have a life to them as the psyche's way of making itself (Ep 49).

**Jung's limitation and the synthesis**: Jung understands individuation as intra-psychic — happening within the psyche. His Kantianism makes the archetypes ultimately subjective rather than transjective. This is Corbin's criticism: the mystical should disclose the depths of the *world* as well as the psyche; reducing the imaginal to something intra-psychic loses the being-mode's transjective engagement with reality. Buber's criticism: Jung misses the existential modes (I-Thou vs. I-It) because he has no way to represent the transjective relationship. What Jung does have that Corbin lacks: the psychology — how the imaginal gets internalized into the psyche, how individuation works. Vervaeke's synthesis: Corbin + Jung + Buber = transjective imaginal (Corbin) + psychic mechanism of internalization (Jung) + existential modes (Buber) (Ep 49).

## Concepts introduced or developed
- [[Paradox of Self-Creation]] — introduced: Strawson; S1=S2 AND S1≠S2; resolved by non-logical identity + participation in emergence
- [[Archetypes]] — introduced: Jung; structural-functional virtual engines (NOT mere images); regulate psychic salience landscaping; imaginal not imaginary; psychological analogs of Platonic forms
- [[Individuation]] — introduced: Jung; ego-Self dialogue through archetypes; anagogic participation in psychic emergence; neither passive nor active but participatory
- [[Divine Double]] — updated: renamed to sacred second self; Buddha nature, Atman/Brahman parallels; = what "soul" was always naming
- [[Non-Logical Identity]] — deepened: resolves Strawson's paradox; S1 participates in S2's emergence; S1 temporally prior, S2 normatively primary
- [[Aspiration]] — deepened: normative authority of S2 over S1; imaginal symbol works anagogically; participation in emergence

## Thinkers & traditions

- [[Galen Strawson]] — paradox of self-creation; refuted by Callard via non-logical identity and participation
