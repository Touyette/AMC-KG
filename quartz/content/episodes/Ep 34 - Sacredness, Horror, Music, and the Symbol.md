# Ep 34 - Sacredness, Horror, Music, and the Symbol

## What this lecture argues
Ep 34 develops the structure of sacredness through its two poles — worldview attunement and the numinous — then explains how symbols work as the psycho-technology for navigating between them.

**The full structure of the Numinous** (Otto): the numinous has three aspects — mystery (trajectory of transframing, sensibility transcendence), fascination (super-salience, flow-like absorption), and horror. These are not separable: the numinous is simultaneously compelling and terrifying. It constitutes the *meta-accommodation* pole of sacredness: while worldview attunement (Geertz's meta-meaning function) is meta-assimilation (everything fits together, we are homed), the numinous is meta-accommodation (the machinery of the agent-arena relationship is blown apart and forced to restructure). Together they constitute higher-order opponent processing of Relevance Realization at the existential level.

**Horror** is not startle-and-fear (most horror movies get this wrong) — it is losing one's grip on reality and intelligibility, associated with madness rather than danger. The key mechanism: **inter-categorical things** (Mary Douglas). Monsters are prototypically inter-categorical: the werewolf violates person/animal, the ghost violates living/dead, the vampire violates consuming/generating. When core categorical distinctions break down, intelligibility breaks down. On a continuum: yucky → uncanny → eerie → horror. Awe borrows power from this edge: it takes us to the very precipice of intelligibility, forcing maximal accommodation and deep humility — the recognition that we are ultimately finite, limited meaning-making beings. This prevents inflation.

**Music**: music is not referential or propositional — yet life would be a mistake without it (Nietzsche). Music seriously plays with the machinery of salience landscaping and relevance realization for its own sake, which is exactly why it is so deeply connected to sacredness. It insinuates its way participatorily into the agent-arena relationship, restructuring how the world is disclosed and how agency is constituted.

**Symbol vs. Sign**: A sign *refers* (the heart → thinks of love). A symbol *refers AND exemplifies by participation* — it gets you to participate in what it refers to, triggering deeper cognitive machinery. Symbols use profound metaphors (not ornamental ones — the ones structuring cognition itself) through neural **exaptation** (Michael Anderson: circuit reuse). The justice/balance scale example: justice is abstract, hard to hold in mind; the balance-scale symbol lets you trace back through the exaptation chain, activating the cerebellum's balance-coordination machinery, and bringing that machinery up to give participatory-perspectival insight into justice. The process is bidirectional: bottom-up projection (embodied practice up to abstract concept) AND top-down activation (abstract domain selects which embodied machinery to reactivate).
