# Ep 06 - Aristotle, Kant, and Evolution

## What this lecture argues
This episode introduces Aristotle's dynamical extension of Plato, centered on the concept of change as growth and development (actualization of potential via in-formation). Aristotle's distinction between causes (events that make things happen) and constraints (conditions that shape possibility) is developed via Alicia Juarrero's Dynamical Systems Theory: a system that self-organizes through the interplay of enabling constraints and selective constraints constitutes a Virtual Engine. Darwinian evolution is presented as the first full dynamical systems theory in science. These ideas are then applied to the cultivation of character: your character is the virtual engine regulating your self-development, and virtues are the enabling and selective constraints that shape your growth. Akrasia (acting against one's better judgment despite knowing the right course) is explained as insufficient character rather than weak will. The episode also discusses Kant's problem with circular explanations in biology (trees make leaves that make trees) and how Juarrero's framework resolves it by distinguishing actuality from potentiality. The episode closes previewing Aristotle's account of worldview attunement.

## Concepts introduced or developed
- [[Virtual Engine]] — introduced via Juarrero; a dynamical system of enabling and selective constraints that regulates a self-organizing feedback cycle
- [[Enabling Constraints]] — introduced; constraints that open up possibilities for a system
- [[Selective Constraints]] — introduced; constraints that reduce options, shaping what happens in the feedback cycle
- [[Autopoiesis]] — introduced via Varela/Thompson; self-making systems that seek conditions for their own continuation
- [[Dynamical Systems Theory]] — introduced as the framework for understanding growth, development, and evolution
- [[Akrasia]] — introduced; acting against better judgment due to insufficient character, not weakness of will per se
- [[Character]] — introduced as the virtual engine of a person's development; the cultivated set of constraints regulating growth
- [[Virtue]] — introduced as a specific enabling/selective constraint cultivated through practice; Aristotle's account of character
- [[Golden Mean]] — introduced; virtue as the dynamically calibrated mean between excess and deficiency

## Thinkers & traditions
- [[Aristotle]] — primary figure; extends Plato with a dynamical account of growth, character, and wisdom
- [[Immanuel Kant]] — introduced via the problem of circular explanation in biology
- [[Alicia Juarrero]] — introduced; develops Aristotelian ideas into modern Dynamical Systems Theory
