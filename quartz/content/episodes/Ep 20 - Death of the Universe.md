# Ep 20 - Death of the Universe

## What this lecture argues
Ep 20 traces the accelerating breakdown of the three orders that Augustine synthesized. Four forces work together. First, the **Rhineland Mystics** (Eckhart) transform the normative order: since Aquinas cut reason off from God, spirituality can no longer mean anagogic ascent through love-and-reason. Instead, love is not what moves the will — love is how the will moves when it negates itself. Spirituality becomes a descent (God flowing into the emptied self), and inner conflict becomes valorized as the essential spiritual structure. Wisdom as the cultivation of self-transcendence is severed from spirituality.

Second, **Ockham's Nominalism** destroys the rational basis of the nomological order: God's primary faculty is will, not reason; any order in the universe is arbitrary fiat. Universals (like "book-ness") don't exist in the world — only raw individuals exist; categories are names we impose through language. Knowing is inner coherence of language, not conformity with the world's rational structure. The world is inherently absurd.

Third, the **Black Death** validates this worldview (the cosmos appears chaotic, God's order arbitrary), disrupts the feudal social structure, and launches the rise of commercialism, the secular state, and corporate capitalism — creating a secular alternative to the Church's meaning-provision. New **mathematical psycho-technologies** (Hindu-Arabic numerals, algebra, zero) empower propositional-quantitative thinking.

Fourth, **Copernicus and Galileo** complete the epistemological catastrophe. Copernicus shows that all the Aristotelian marks of knowing can be satisfied — clear senses, calm agreement — and we're still wrong about geocentrism. Experience is a veil between mind and world, not contact with it; only math cuts through. Then Galileo's **inertial motion** kills teleology: things don't move purposefully; they are inert, moved by external forces. The universe is dead. Humans become "ontological castaways." And the **primary/secondary quality distinction** completes the entrapment of meaning: mathematical properties are objective (in the world); beauty, purpose, meaning, value — all subjective, only inside the mind. The world is a purposeless machine; everything that mattered is locked inside the head.

## Concepts introduced or developed
- [[Nominalism]] — introduced: Ockham: only individuals exist; universals are names; any order is imposed by language/will, not discovered in the world; severs the conformity theory; world is inherently absurd
- [[Primary and Secondary Qualities]] — introduced: Galileo: mathematical properties are objective (in the world); all other qualities (beauty, meaning, purpose, value) are subjective (only in mind); completes the entrapment of meaning inside the head
- [[Three Orders]] — deepened: all three under severe stress; normative order's ascent-spirituality replaced by descent-spirituality; nomological order loses rational grounding via Nominalism; narrative order loses teleological direction via Galileo
- [[Conformity Theory of Knowing]] — refuted by Copernicus: even when all Aristotelian epistemic marks are satisfied, we can be systematically wrong; experience is a veil
- [[Psycho-Technology]] — deepened: Hindu-Arabic numerals, algebra, zero as mathematical psycho-technologies enabling the commercial revolution and quantitative science
