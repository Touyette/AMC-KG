# Ep 39 - The Religion of No Religion

## What this lecture argues
Ep 39 has two main threads: (1) Vervaeke proposes structural features of the "Religion That Is Not A Religion" — a post-credal, religio-centered practice ecology grounded in the insights developed across the series; (2) he begins the cognitive science of wisdom, introducing McKee and Barber's convergence argument that all philosophical and psychological theories of wisdom point toward systematic "seeing through illusion and into reality."

**Religio vs. Credo — and the function of credo**: The religion that is not a religion is centered on Religio rather than Credo. But credo has a legitimate function that must not be dismissed: understood through **Signal Detection Theory**, credo = setting the criterion on religio. Every cognitive system must determine what counts as genuine signal (relevant information) vs. noise (irrelevant/misleading information) — and this is always a trade-off. Setting the criterion too high (the overly cautious Bill-gazelle) = treating noise as signal (exhaustion, false positives). Setting it too low (the epistemically rigid Tom-gazelle) = missing genuine signal (killed by leopard). There is no algorithm for the correct criterion; it requires contextually sensitive, situationally aware judgment. Consciousness itself (Lau) functions to set the criterion for perception — it is the organ of relevance realization within perspectival knowing. Credo sets the criterion for religio — determines which practices, symbols, and mythos actually put one in contact with Religio. The problem is not credo but **credal dominance**: when credo is set absolutely and finally rather than dynamically and open-endedly. The religion that is not a religion always keeps credo in the service of religio, never as its master (Ep 39).

**Structure of the Religion That Is Not A Religion**:
1. Religio-centered (not credal-dominant); credo always in service of religio
2. Open-ended mythos (never absolutely criterion-setting; grounded in inexhaustible sacredness)
3. Mythos beholden to three levels: (a) unconscious/participatory — where RR constitutes the agent-arena relationship; (b) consciousness/perspectival — salience landscaping, situational awareness; (c) cultural/distributed — propositional/communicable content. Mythos must run top-down (propositional → participatory) AND be open to bottom-up emergence (participatory → perspectival → propositional)
4. Ecology of psycho-technologies: complementary practices with mutually compensating strengths and weaknesses; a self-sustaining counteractive dynamical system at the community level
5. **Meta-psycho-technology**: explicit, collectively cultivated capacity for designing, evaluating, and iterating on the ecology of practices — moving from intuitive construction of practices to principled engineering; analogous to wisdom as meta-virtue at the individual level. Co-op structure of communities; credo-as-wiki (collectively maintained, dynamically updated paradigmatic content) (Ep 39)

**Cognitive Science of Wisdom — beginning**: Sternberg's 1990 anthology launched psychological work on wisdom. McKee and Barber (1999) perform a reflective equilibrium between all prior philosophical theories (Socratic, Aristotelian, Stoic, Platonic) and all prior psychological theories. Both converge on the same core: wisdom is the **systematic seeing through illusion and into reality** (or what is more real). "Seeing through illusion" = seeing through cognitive and existential self-deception. "Systematic" = not just solving one problem but achieving a multi-domain insight that intervenes simultaneously across many related problem instances — like a gestalt shift that restructures the entire landscape rather than one point. "And into reality" = because you can only know you've seen through illusion if you come to something less illusory; the two poles are inseparable. This is a profound kind of insight — systematic transframing — and it connects directly to everything Vervaeke has developed about RR, self-deception, and the four kinds of knowing (Ep 39).
