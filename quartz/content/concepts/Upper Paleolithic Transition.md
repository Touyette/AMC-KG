# Upper Paleolithic Transition

> The behavioral revolution ~40,000 BCE that marked the emergence of modern human cognition through shamanism, art, and metaphor — not a biological change but a cognitive one.

## Vervaeke's framing

Vervaeke argues the Upper Paleolithic Transition was not driven by a genetic mutation but by the invention of the first psycho-technologies. Shamanism, with its ritual practices for inducing altered states of consciousness, enabled a cascade of cognitive enhancements: the flow state, enhanced implicit learning, and an explosion in metaphorical cognition. These gave shamanic hunter-gatherer groups a decisive adaptive edge over those without this machinery, explaining why the transition appears universal across cultures. The key innovation was not a new biological capacity but a new way of deploying existing cognitive machinery — cognitive exaptation at a civilizational scale (Ep 02).
