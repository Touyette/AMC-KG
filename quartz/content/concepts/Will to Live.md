# Will to Live

> Schopenhauer's term for the blind, irrational, relentless drive underlying all conscious experience — the real driver of existence that the ego merely serves, and whose relentless purposelessness reveals the nihilistic underside of Romanticism.

## Vervaeke's framing
Schopenhauer takes Kant's model (the mind imposes structure on experience, but the thing-in-itself is unknowable) and inverts it further: the irrational is not just what's closest to reality, but is the real driver of the whole system. The Will to Live is like Ockham's arbitrary God now internalized — it frames, filters, and structures all our experience without rational purpose. The ego (the rational mind) sits atop it like a small man on the shoulders of a giant, serving it without understanding it. Schopenhauer's famous example: sex promises the fulfilment that religion once offered, but after the act, none of that fulfilment accrues — and then you die. This relentless, purposeless willing is the source of nihilism: meaning making is revealed as an illusion sustained by an irrational drive. The only reprieve is art and music — moments of disinterested contemplation that quiet the Will (Ep 23). Vervaeke reads Schopenhauer as the godfather of nihilism, with Nietzsche as his heir who tries — but only partly succeeds — in converting the Will to Live into the life-affirming Will to Power.
