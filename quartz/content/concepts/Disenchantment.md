# Disenchantment

> Max Weber's Entzauberung: the historical process by which the scientific-rational worldview stripped the world of its magical, sacred, and teleological dimensions, leaving a universe of cause-and-effect mechanisms devoid of inherent meaning, value, or purpose; the cultural-historical dimension of the meaning crisis.

## Vervaeke's framing
Weber's "disenchantment of the world" (Entzauberung der Welt) describes the overarching cultural effect of the scientific revolution and rationalization of Western culture: the systematic elimination of magical, supernatural, and sacred forces as explanations of natural events, replaced by naturalistic causal explanations. The disenchanted world is a world in which everything that was once understood as sacred, purposeful, or meaning-bearing has been explained away — or relocated into the subjective interior, where it has no grip on reality (Ep 01).

**Historical process**: Disenchantment is not a single event but an ongoing historical process: the scientific revolution removed God as an explanation of natural phenomena; the Enlightenment subjected moral and political authorities to rational critique; the industrial revolution reorganized social life around efficiency rather than meaning; the 20th century has seen further rationalization of health, education, and relationships. Each step reduces the domains in which meaning is understood to have objective purchase (Ep 01, Ep 22).

**What was "enchanted"**: The "enchanted" world of pre-modern Europe was one in which: spirits animated natural forces; the cosmos had a teleological structure oriented toward divine ends; the church calendar organized time around sacred commemorations; the landscape was populated with sacred sites; illness could be understood as spiritual attack; luck, fortune, and fate were genuine forces. The enchanted world was not primarily a world of false beliefs but a world of genuine participatory engagement with meaning-structures that were understood as real (Ep 01).

**Disenchantment and the Cartesian grammar**: Disenchantment is closely related to the [[Cartesian Cultural Grammar]]: the primary/secondary quality distinction relocates all qualitative meaning (beauty, sacredness, purpose) from the world into the subjective mind, simultaneously disenchanting the world (which is now only primary qualities) and problematizing the mind (which is now the repository of everything real but epistemically suspicious) (Ep 22).

**Re-enchantment**: Vervaeke's project is not a return to pre-modern enchantment (a flight from science) but a re-enchantment through a more adequate naturalism: showing that meaning, value, and even sacredness are not mere subjective projections but real features of the organism-environment relationship, grounded in relevance realization and the deep continuity of cognitive and natural structure. This is the "scientific naturalism completed" move (Ep 38, Ep 50).
