# Problem Nexus

> Vervaeke's extension of Arlin's problem finding: a core problem whose solution would cascade and make significant impact on multiple existing problems — the mark of genuinely creative and wise problem-finding.

## Vervaeke's framing
Arlin (1990) argues that the ability to *find* problems — not just solve given ones — is a post-formal cognitive operation central to wisdom and creativity. Most people can solve well-defined problems once they are presented; the genuinely wise and creative can identify problems that others have not yet noticed (Ep 42).

Vervaeke extends this to the concept of the *problem nexus*: a specifically high-value form of problem finding in which the generated problem is a hub that connects to, and whose solution would cascade across, multiple existing problems in a domain. The contrast is between simply adding another problem to a list (trivial problem finding) and identifying the *core* problem from which the others derive or which, if solved, would advance solutions to the others (Ep 42).

Vervaeke offers his own research program as an example: identifying relevance realization as a problem nexus for cognitive science (connecting insight, flow, self-deception, combinatorial explosion, attention, the frame problem, consciousness) and later for the meaning crisis (connecting gnosis, wisdom, the sacred, enlightenment, and the response). This is not modest — but it is the intellectual wager that has organized the AMC series.

The connection to wisdom: generating a genuine problem nexus requires the kind of synoptic integration and multi-frame comprehension that is itself a mark of wisdom. It requires seeing across domains, perceiving the deep structural similarities that allow one problem to illuminate many, and having the courage to name the connection. This is why problem finding is not merely an intellectual skill but involves the whole person (Ep 42).
