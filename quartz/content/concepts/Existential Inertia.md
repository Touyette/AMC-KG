# Existential Inertia

> The condition of knowing where one wants to be existentially but being unable to make the new worldview viable — unable to enact the sensibility transcendence that would transform the agent-arena relationship.

## Vervaeke's framing
Existential inertia is not ignorance about what one wants or where one wants to go. A person in existential inertia can articulate the life they want, imagine it, draw inferences about what it would be like — but cannot make it *viable*. They cannot bring about the participatory-perspectival change that would make the new way of being their actual way of being. Every effort to get there circles back to here. This is what Jung recognized as the primary presenting complaint in depth psychology: not a particular problem but an experience of being stuck, thwarted, not moving (Ep 17).

The mechanism: one's identity is organized around a pattern (Frankfurt's "unthinkable" — kicking out the beloved son is not a viable option) and the machinery of caring cannot be redirected by propositional effort. One can think "I should change" but thinking is insufficient because the change required is at the level of perspectival and participatory knowing, not propositional knowing. The remedy is serious play and enacted analogy — ways of inhabiting the liminal zone between two worldviews so that the new one gradually becomes viable. See also [[Existential Entrapment]] for the combined condition when stupefaction (L.A. Paul's transformative-experience ignorance) is added to stuckness.
