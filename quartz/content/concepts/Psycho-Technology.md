# Psycho-Technology

> A socially standardized, internalizable, domain-general practice for enhancing information processing — not a tool in the hand but a tool in the mind.

## Vervaeke's framing

Vervaeke offers a precise definition in Ep 42: "a socially generated and standardized way of formatting, manipulating, and enhancing information processing that's readily internalizable into human cognition, and that can be applied in a domain general manner." It must extend and empower cognition in some reliable and extensive manner and be highly generalizable among people. Prototypical instances: literacy, numeracy, graphing. Not just anything used mentally qualifies — the defining criteria are domain-generality and internalizability. The key features are: it is socially generated (not invented alone), it is internalizable (becomes part of how you think, not just an external aid), and it is domain-general (the gains transfer beyond the original context). Shamanic rituals, alphabetic literacy, coinage, mathematics, mindfulness, and the Socratic method are all examples. What makes psycho-technologies so powerful is that they can be exapted — their enhanced cognitive capacities get used for purposes far beyond those that originally generated them. Vervaeke returns to this concept throughout the series as the mechanism by which each civilizational leap occurs (Ep 02, recurring).
