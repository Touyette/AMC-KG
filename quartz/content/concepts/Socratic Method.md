# Socratic Method

> Systematic cross-examination aimed at exposing the gap between what people find salient and what they genuinely understand — inducing aporia as the first step toward wisdom.

## Vervaeke's framing
The Socratic method (also called elenchus) works by asking interlocutors to define the important things they are pursuing (happiness, courage, wisdom) and then showing through careful questioning that their definitions are inconsistent or inadequate. The point is not to leave people in nihilistic ignorance but to make them viscerally aware of how they are perpetually pursuing things they do not understand — pursuing salience ahead of truth. This provokes one of two responses: anger (like those who had Socrates killed) or a genuine insight into the need for transformation. The Socratic method is a psycho-technology for combating self-deception and bullshit by coupling the truth-tracking machinery with the relevance machinery. Socrates uses it in the social arena — going into the marketplace — which is why Plato saw dialogue as the primary vehicle: the social medium (thymos) is enlisted in service of the rational project (Ep 04).
