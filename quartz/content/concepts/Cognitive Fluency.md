# Cognitive Fluency

> The ease with which information is processed; Vervaeke treats it as a signal the brain uses to gauge contact with reality, while recognizing it can mislead.

## Vervaeke's framing
When processing is more fluent — even through trivial changes like better font contrast — people judge information as more true and credible, independent of its actual content. Vervaeke introduces this in the context of the Greeks' improvements to alphabetic literacy (adding vowels, standardizing left-to-right reading): these increased fluency amplified the cognitive power of literacy. He treats fluency as a heuristic the brain uses, generally reliably, to track how much it is genuinely in contact with a pattern in reality; high fluency corresponds to, and partly constitutes, the Flow State. However, fluency can mislead — a salient or catchy claim can feel true simply because it processes easily, which is part of the mechanism of bullshit and self-deception (Ep 03).

Ep 11 deepens this via Topolinski and Reber (2010): insight is specifically a **fluency spike** — a sudden, significant increase in how fluently the system is zeroing in on information. This is why insight feels like clarity and revelation: the brain's fluency heuristic is reading an abrupt improvement in contact with real patterns. The heuristic is domain-general and evolutionarily grounded — it is not perfectly reliable, but across real-world situations, high fluency very generally tracks genuine pattern-detection. This grounds the Continuity Hypothesis: fluency is the shared variable that links ordinary processing → insight → flow → higher states of consciousness, each stage representing a more massive and sustained fluency increase (Ep 11).
