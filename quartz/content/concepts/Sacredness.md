# Sacredness

> The psycho-existential experience of encountering what serves as a meta-meaning ground — not one meaning system among others, but the condition for all meaning systems; homing us and the world together against horror and domicide; requiring both worldview attunement and transgressive opening.

## Vervaeke's framing
Vervaeke approaches sacredness from the psycho-existential side — what is the experience of encountering the sacred? — before addressing the metaphysical question (what grounds that experience?). Drawing on Clifford Geertz (Ep 33), he argues that sacredness functions as a **meta-meaning** system: not a specific meaning (legal, moral, aesthetic) but the condition that makes all meaning systems possible. If the agent-arena relationship breaks down — as in domicide, solitary confinement, or radical culture shock — no particular meaning system can function, because they all presuppose that one is *at home* in a meaningful world.

The first function of sacredness is therefore **homing**: it homes us *into* the world and homes the world *around* us, providing what Geertz calls the meta-meaning that protects against horror, alienation, absurdity, and existential anxiety. This is why people embedded in spiritual communities show greater resilience in the face of life's tragedies — they have reliable access to the meta-meaning function. And this feeds directly into **worldview attunement** (the agent-arena relationship functioning well, with coherence, significance, and purpose mutually reinforcing) (Ep 33).

However, Vervaeke criticizes Geertz's account as necessary but not sufficient. Sacredness is not only about homing and attunement — it also has a transgressive face. The Gnostic tradition repeatedly shows this: the sacred is not simply what confirms and shelters; it is also what *overturns the grammar of a worldview*. This transgressive dimension — what Otto called the **Numinous** — is what takes the experience of sacredness beyond mere social cohesion into genuine self-transcendence. Sacredness, fully experienced, involves both being homed and being disrupted — both worldview attunement and worldview transgression (Ep 33).

Ep 34 clarifies the full structure: sacredness is **higher-order opponent processing** of Relevance Realization at the existential level. The two poles are: (1) **meta-assimilation** — worldview attunement (Geertz's homing function: everything is coherent, significant, purposeful, the agent-arena relationship is well-functioning); and (2) **meta-accommodation** — the numinous (mystery + fascination + horror; the machinery of the agent-arena relationship is disrupted, transgressed, and restructured at the highest level). Neither pole alone is sacredness: pure worldview attunement without the numinous collapses into a comfortable closed world immune to self-transcendence (potentially leading to inflation, fanaticism, tribal closure). Pure numinous without worldview attunement dissolves into madness, cosmic terror, inability to function. The sacred holds both in dynamic tension, doing at the existential level what RR does moment-to-moment at the cognitive level (Ep 34).
