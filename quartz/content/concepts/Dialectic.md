# Dialectic

> A method or process of truth-seeking through structured opposition: Socratically, the cross-examination that reveals contradiction and drives toward wisdom; Hegelian, the historical movement of ideas through thesis–antithesis–higher synthesis.

## Vervaeke's framing
Vervaeke introduces dialectic in its Socratic sense as a core psycho-technology: the cross-examination (elenchus) that triggers aporia, exposes self-deception, and trains second-order thinking. Unlike sophistry (which wins arguments), the Socratic dialectic genuinely seeks truth by following the logic of what is said regardless of social pressure or comfort. It is a form of spiritual exercise and the paradigm of philosophy as a way of life (Ep 04).

In Ep 24, Hegel radically reinterprets the dialectic as the mechanism by which Geist — the living system of patterns of intelligibility — develops historically. Ideas are not merely cross-examined but undergo a real developmental process: a thesis articulates itself, generates a contrasting antithesis, and both are taken up into a higher-order synthesis that includes and transcends them. This process repeats at ever greater scales until it achieves self-awareness in Absolute Spirit. Vervaeke notes that Hegel's dialectic severs dialectic from Anagoge: for Plato, dialectic drove toward personal transformation; for Hegel, it drives toward epistemic self-transcendence of Geist, leaving personal transformation behind (Ep 24).
