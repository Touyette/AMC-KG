# Mystery

> The phenomenological impossibility of making the framing process itself a focal object — you can never frame the framing; carefully distinguished from theoretical inexplicability; the reason Religio is experienced as irreducibly deep and pre-egoic.

## Vervaeke's framing
Vervaeke introduces mystery (Ep 33) with a crucial distinction: **phenomenological mystery** (what cannot be perspectivally framed) is not the same as **theoretical inexplicability** (what cannot be explained propositionally). To equivocate them is to confuse Perspectival and Propositional Knowing. One can theorize what one cannot exemplify — a theory of light need not itself be light; a theory of war need not be an instance of war.

What is phenomenologically mysterious about Religio and RR is this: whenever you are thinking or perceiving, you are inside a frame. But what is precisely *not* inside any frame is the framing process itself — the machinery of Relevance Realization. If you try to frame the framing (to make RR itself a focal object), you simply produce a new, larger frame — with the *new* framing process again outside it, in an infinite regress. This is not a failure of intelligence; it is a structural feature of any relevance-realizing system. You can never see *by* your "I" the "I" that is seeing (William James's I/Me distinction: I always see by means of the I, but never the I itself) (Ep 33).

This makes the machinery of Religio perpetually present as a **subsidiary awareness** — you are always aware *through* it, never able to make it a focal object. In this sense it is deeply mysterious in experience, even while being theorizable from the outside. And this phenomenological mystery is precisely what opens up the trajectory of transframing: each attempt to focus on the framing process reveals a still-deeper framing, opening up the sense of inexhaustible depth that wonder and awe disclose.

Vervaeke also notes: the phenomenological impossibility of experiencing your own non-existence (e.g., trying to frame what it's like to be dead) is a different application of the same principle — not proof of immortality, but evidence that certain experiences have structural limits that do not map onto metaphysical claims (Ep 33).
