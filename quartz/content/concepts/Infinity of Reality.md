# Infinity of Reality

> Early Romantics (Schlegel) via Barfield: reality has an inexhaustible surplus that cannot be fully framed, computed, or encompassed; it is this inexhaustibleness that continually draws finite beings into self-transcendence.

## Vervaeke's framing
The early Romantics (post-Kantians like Friedrich Schlegel, distinguished by Di Fuccia from the later Romantics and Hegel) emphasized what they called the infinity of reality: not infinity in the sense of uncountable, but in the sense of *non-finitude* — incapable of being fully bounded, framed, or exhausted by any finite representation. This is the inexhaustible moreness (Ep 50).

Schlegel's formulation: *the finite longing for the infinite*. Finite beings — always already framed, always with limited cognitive resources — are epek-statically drawn toward the combinatorially explosive surplus that exceeds any frame they deploy. This drawing-out is what Schlegel calls *eduction* (drawing out, the root of "education" in its aspirational sense) — it is the disclosure of sacredness through the ongoing transframing encounter with the inexhaustibleness of reality (Ep 50).

**Connection to Barfield's final participation**: The infinity of reality is what Barfield's poiesis encounters in genuine creativity. When we are in the flow of creative act — the ekstasis of creativity — we are not imposing a frame on a passive world (the Cartesian picture) but are being drawn by the inexhaustible moreness of what we are in relation with. The self-organizing character of poetic and creative language reflects this: words with dual inner/outer meanings (pneuma/spiritus, see/understand) are traces of the original participation in which the inexhaustible interafforded the inner and the outer before the Cartesian grammar divided them (Ep 50).

**Connection to Vervaeke's RR theory**: The infinity of reality corresponds precisely to the combinatorial explosion that makes relevance realization cognitively necessary: because the space of possible framings is inexhaustible, finite cognitive systems cannot enumerate — they must home in, resonate, be drawn. Sacredness as transjective property is the experienced quality of this inexhaustible moreness when the agent-arena relationship is properly attuned (Ep 50).

**In Tillich**: Tillich's ground of being is the same infinity of reality framed theologically: the no-thingness of God is not an absence but an inexhaustible fount of meaning cultivation. Epektasis is the structure of the human trajectory within it — never coming to rest, always disclosing more.
