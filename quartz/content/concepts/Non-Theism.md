# Non-Theism

> Tillich/Vervaeke: a position that transcends both theism and atheism by rejecting the four shared premises that make their debate possible — applicable to Buddhism, Taoism, and the "God beyond the God of theism."

## Vervaeke's framing
Non-theism is not a compromise between theism and atheism, nor is it atheism by another name. It is the rejection of the presuppositions shared by both sides of the classical theism/atheism debate. Vervaeke offers four such shared premises that non-theism rejects (Ep 50):

1. **God is the supreme being.** Theist: yes. Atheist: no (and so rejects God). Non-theist: rejects the premise itself — God as ground of being is not a being at all, supreme or otherwise.

2. **God is accessed primarily or solely through belief.** Theist and atheist both accept this framing (they just disagree about whether belief is justified). Non-theist rejects it: the appropriate mode of access is participatory and aspirational knowing (da'at, faith-as-ultimate-concern), not propositional belief.

3. **Theology/anti-theology does not require transformative anagoge.** Theist and atheist agree: you just need possession of the right propositions and their implications. Non-theist rejects this entirely — if you are not undergoing transformation, you are not doing it at all.

4. **Sacredness is personal or impersonal.** Theist says personal, atheist says impersonal, but both accept the dichotomy. Non-theist rejects the Cartesian grammar that drives it: sacredness is *transjective* and *participatory* — neither personal (in the Cartesian sense of inner subjectivity) nor impersonal (in the objectivist sense) (Ep 50).

**Tillich's formulation**: The "God beyond the God of theism" is Tillich's explicit name for what non-theism points toward. This is deeply transgressive of classical theism and deeply Gnostic in structure: the demiurge-God of propositional theology entraps us within existential entrapment; moving to the God beyond that God is the liberating gnosis.

**Relation to other traditions**: Non-theism is the correct and appropriate framework for understanding Buddhism (shunyata ≠ nihilism but inexhaustible ground) and Taoism (the Tao that can be named is not the eternal Tao). Nishitani's *Religion and Nothingness* is the most extended philosophical examination of this aspect-identity shift (Ep 50).

**Anatheism**: Richard Kearney's related concept — the kind of theism one arrives at *after* going through atheism. Vervaeke prefers "non-theism" as more precise because it names the rejection of shared premises rather than a post-atheistic return.
