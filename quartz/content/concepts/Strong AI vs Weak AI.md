# Strong AI vs Weak AI

> Searle's distinction between making machines that do intelligent things (weak AI) and making a machine that is genuinely an instance of mind — consciousness, meaning, normativity and all (strong AI).

## Vervaeke's framing
Vervaeke (following Searle) distinguishes two projects. Weak AI — producing machines that accomplish tasks requiring intelligence (calculators, chess programs, recommendation systems) — is entirely legitimate and has transformed the world; it makes no claim to producing genuine mind. Strong AI (now also called Artificial General Intelligence, AGI) would require actually succeeding at Hobbes's project: making a material mind. To show this has been accomplished, one must answer all of Descartes's objections — explain how matter can possess meaning, purpose, normativity, and consciousness. This remains genuinely unresolved in cognitive science and philosophy. Vervaeke treats this as deeply connected to the meaning crisis: if we eventually create strong AI, it will radically alter our understanding of who and what we are; if we cannot, that too tells us something profound about mind (Ep 22). The series implicitly uses this question to motivate the investigation of Relevance Realization.
