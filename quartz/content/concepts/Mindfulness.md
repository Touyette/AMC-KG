# Mindfulness

> In Vervaeke's re-framing (via sati): the psycho-technology of recovering the being mode through trained attentional flexibility — not simply "paying attention to the present moment" but a deep modal remembering that counteracts parasitic processing and modal confusion.

## Vervaeke's framing
The Pali term sati literally means "to remember" or "to bring to mind," but not in the sense of recalling a fact. It is a modal memory — remembering what it is like to be in the being mode, as when returning to a place reactivates an identity one had there. The popular definition of mindfulness as "paying attention to the present moment in a non-judgmental way" is criticized as language of training imported uncritically into the language of explanation: "the present moment" and "non-judgmental" are indexicals that explain nothing. What mindfulness is actually doing, on Vervaeke's account, is training a flexible optimization of attentional scaling — the capacity to scale down (step back and look at the framing processes of the mind, breaking inappropriate gestalt and de-automatizing cognition) and to scale up (reach toward bigger patterns and deeper contact with reality). The interplay of these two, practiced through meditation (scaling down, Vipassana) and contemplation (scaling up), together with moving practices like Tai Chi that train flowing between them, is what mindfulness as a system is actually cultivating: the conditions for systematic insight and modal transformation (Ep 08, Ep 09).
