# Aspiration

> Callard: the incremental, rational process of trying to become someone who has values and ways of seeing that one does not currently have; requires proleptic rationality — guidance of current self by a future self one has not yet become.

## Vervaeke's framing
Callard (2016) argues that transformative knowing is not always sudden (LA Paul's transformative experience). There is also an incremental form: *aspiration* — the process by which one rationally pursues the cultivation of values, appreciations, and perspectives one does not currently possess. This is the standard experience of education and moral development (Ep 45).

**The music appreciation paradox**: The goal of a music appreciation class is to come to value music for its own sake. But if you already valued music for its own sake, you wouldn't need the class. And the only rational basis for joining the class is that you want to reach the endpoint — but you can't rationally value the endpoint until you're there. This is not a vicious circle but a genuine paradox that Callard resolves with *proleptic rationality*.

**Proleptic rationality**: The future (S2) self — who already has the values one is aspiring toward — normatively guides the current (S1) self before the transformation is complete. Current choices are evaluated in light of the perspective one is becoming, not the perspective one currently has. This is not irrational: to claim that aspiring to wisdom or rationality is itself irrational is a performative contradiction (my aspiring to rationality must itself be a form of rationality) (Ep 45).

**The placeholder/symbol**: Aspiration requires an interim value — a *placeholder* — that connects the future self to the current self and motivates entry into the liminal space where the new value can be tried on. In Vervaeke's terms, this placeholder functions as a *symbol*: it participates in both the current and future orientations, facilitating the anagogic movement between them. E.g., "I will practice doing difficult things I don't yet value" — this is not music appreciation itself, but it can get one into a space where music appreciation can begin to emerge (Ep 45).

**Connection to wonder and gnosis**: Aspiration is deeply connected to wonder — wonder opens up one's worldview and motivates identity questioning, creating the openness needed for aspirational change. Aspiration is also deeply connected to gnosis: entering the liminal space where one's existential entrapment can begin to loosen. The pathway: wonder → aporia → gnosis (not through inference but through participatory engagement with the symbols/practices of the new mode of being) (Ep 45).

**Ep 49 — Strawson's paradox and resolution**: Galen Strawson identifies a paradox in self-creation: continuity (S1 = S2, required for self-creation) contradicts real novelty (S1 ≠ S2, required for creation). Callard and Vervaeke resolve this through non-logical identity (S1 → S2 is genuine transformation, not logical identity or non-identity) and by articulating the normative asymmetry: S1 is temporally prior (causally produces S2) but S2 is normatively primary — only S2 can justify, explain, and legitimate S1's aspirational behavior. This is proleptic rationality made precise: the future self that has come to appreciate classical music can retrospectively explain why the prior self's music class was rational; S1 cannot do this from its own standpoint. Aspiration is thus the process by which S1 participates in the emergence of S2 — neither making it (pure activity) nor receiving it (pure passivity) but participating in it (Ep 49).

**Ep 49 — The aspired-to self as internalized imaginal symbol**: Vervaeke articulates the cognitive mechanism: the aspired-to self (sacred second self) is a symbolic self that one internalizes anagogically. I internalize Socrates (or the divine double, or the Christ figure) as a symbolic model of the aspired-to self, and this internalization changes how I perceive and inhabit the world. This altered world-indwelling allows deeper re-internalization, which transforms the world-indwelling further — the coupled, anagogic loop of aspiration/individuation. The symbol is the cognitive hinge that makes the non-logical identity between S1 and S2 navigable (Ep 49).
