# Cognitive Interactional Fittedness

> The quality of a dynamical coupling between a cognitive agent and its environment in which each is structured to draw out the best response from the other; not a static match but a mutually constitutive resonance between agent and world.

## Vervaeke's framing
Cognitive interactional fittedness names the quality Vervaeke is tracking when he talks about an organism being "at home" in its environment — not merely capable of surviving in it, but coupled to it in a way that allows the organism's deepest capacities to be realized. Fit is not a one-way relation (organism adapts to environment) but a mutual structuring: the organism structures the environment through enaction, and the environment structures the organism through affordances and resistance (Ep 30).

**Fit vs. mere survival**: An organism can survive in an environment to which it is poorly fitted — it gets by, but without the resonance, flow, and deep engagement that characterize genuine fittedness. Vervaeke distinguishes minimum viable functioning from optimal cognitive interactional fittedness: the former is the floor of biological sufficiency, the latter is the condition under which meaning, insight, and flourishing become available (Ep 30).

**Dynamical coupling**: Interactional fittedness is not a static snapshot but a dynamical property. It requires ongoing recalibration as both agent and environment change. The cognitive tempering skill — managing the efficiency-resiliency trade-off — is precisely what allows an agent to maintain fittedness across changes: being committed enough to exploit current fit but flexible enough to re-fit when the environment shifts (Ep 30).

**Connection to affordances and salience**: When an agent is well-fitted to an environment, the environment's affordances are salient to that agent — the right features are foregrounded, the right actions feel available. This is relevance realization working well: the RR machinery is calibrated to the environment such that what matters to the organism tracks what is actually relevant. Poor fittedness produces a salience landscape that is out of sync with reality — relevant features are ignored, irrelevant ones are foregrounded (Ep 30).

**Implication for the meaning crisis**: One way to characterize the meaning crisis: modern humans are living in environments (informational, social, institutional) to which their cognitive and affective machinery is poorly fitted. The machinery was shaped for environments of evolutionary adaptedness; it is now navigating environments that differ so radically that the RR systems produce systematic misalignment. Restoring meaning requires not just individual therapy but redesign of the cognitive ecology to support genuine interactional fittedness (Ep 30).
