# Prajña

> Sanskrit: a state of wisdom-knowing described by DT Suzuki as simultaneously looking as deeply inward as possible AND as deeply outward as possible, such that the perspective reaching into the self and the perspective reaching out to all of reality interpenetrate — a non-dual state that resolves the perspectival clash underlying absurdity; in the West, Spinoza's *scientia intuitiva* describes the same state.

## Vervaeke's framing
Vervaeke introduces Prajña (Ep 37) as the practice-state that addresses the perennial problem of **absurdity** — the clash of incommensurable perspectives (self-world). The argumentative generators of absurdity are logically invalid (Nagel), so the remedy is not better arguments but a cognitive-existential state that overcomes the perspectival clash directly.

Prajña is described by DT Suzuki as: "simultaneously looking as deeply in as you can, and simultaneously looking out as deeply as you can" — such that the inward and outward perspectives stop being opposed and begin to interpenetrate. Suzuki quotes Meister Eckhart (a Christian Neoplatonist) to gloss this: "The eye by which I see God is the same eye by which God sees me." The perspective that reaches up and outward toward what is ultimate is numerically the same as the perspective that reaches inward to the deepest self. They are not two clashing perspectives — they are one.

The Western analog is Spinoza's **scientia intuitiva** (the third kind of knowledge in the Ethics). Vervaeke's reading: the Ethics has a rigorous logical structure — premises build into arguments, arguments build into a meta-argument — but the *point* of practicing the Ethics is not to trace this structure propositionally. It is to practice seeing the whole argument in each premise AND each premise in the whole simultaneously; like reading a word and seeing the letter in the context of the word while seeing the word in the context of the sentence while seeing the sentence in the context of the text — all levels interpenetrating, bidirectionally. This simultaneous bottom-up and top-down interpenetration at maximum depth *is* scientia intuitiva (Ep 37).

The state of Prajña is a form of **non-duality** (Ep 09): simultaneously at maximal breaking-frame (scale up — universal perspective) and maximal making-frame (scale down — pure consciousness event), optimally dynamically integrated. In practice, one alternates between scaling down (mindfulness toward pure awareness) and scaling up (resonant at-onement with everything), until — with sufficient practice — the two converge into a state where the clash of perspectives is dissolved from the inside. This state carries a characteristic joy analogous to the insight-flash in humor, but at the highest cognitive-existential level (Ep 37).

Prajña is not a belief or proposition — it cannot be achieved by better arguments. It is a state cultivated through practice: Lectio Divina with Spinoza's Ethics; alternating mindfulness and contemplation; meditation practices that move between deautomatization and integration. It is the cognitive-existential response to absurdity because it directly resolves the perspectival clash rather than attempting to argue one perspective into submission.
