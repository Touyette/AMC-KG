# Individuation

> Jung: the lifelong organic/autopoietic process by which the ego enters into a dialogue with the Self (sacred second self) through the imaginal archetypes, transforming its perspectival knowing and participatory being; neither passive reception nor active making but participatory emergence.

## Vervaeke's framing
Individuation is Jung's central developmental concept — simultaneously a theory of how the psyche functions and how it develops (function and development are unified in a self-organizing system). It is the response to the meaning crisis at the level of individual psychological transformation (Ep 49).

**The model**: The psyche is not a Newtonian pressure machine (Freud's hydraulic model) but an organic, autopoietic, self-organizing dynamical system. Individuation is the name for the self-organizing process by which the psyche grows toward greater wholeness through the dialogue between:
- **Ego** (archetype of the conscious mind; the virtual engine organizing conscious experience)
- **Self** (archetype of the archetypes; the ultimate virtual engine; the sacred second self; the principle of psychic autopoiesis)
- **Imaginal archetypes** (the mediating virtual engines between ego and Self)

As the ego engages the archetypes in an imaginal (not imaginary) way, that engagement is internalized — the archetypes begin to shape how the ego perceives and inhabits the world. The ego indwells the world differently → can better internalize the archetype → indwells the world differently still → etc. This is the anagogic loop of individuation (Ep 49).

**Participation, not making or receiving**: Individuation is neither something the ego makes happen (active making) nor something that just happens to the ego (passive reception). Like aspiration (Callard), it is participation in emergence: the Self emerges out of the ego-archetype dialogue such that the ego disappears into a more comprehensive Self. S1 participates in the emergence of S2 (Ep 49).

**Active imagination**: Jung's psychotechnology for individuation — deliberate, disciplined engagement with the imaginal archetypes through fantasy, dream work, and creative practice; not as imaginary products to possess but as imaginal entities to dialogue with.

**Connection to sacred second self**: The Self in individuation is the sacred second self — what one is aspirationally becoming through the lifelong process. The ego's relationship to the Self is the psychological articulation of the S1 → S2 aspiration structure. The archetypes are the imaginal symbols that make the non-logical identity between ego and Self workable (Ep 49).

**Jung's limitation (intra-psychism)**: Jung's Kantianism leads him to understand individuation as happening *within* the psyche — as an intra-psychic process. Corbin's criticism: the mystical also discloses the depths of the *world* — the imaginal is transjective, not merely subjective. Buber's criticism: individuation so understood can't account for the genuine I-Thou encounter with what transcends the psyche. Vervaeke: Jung correctly accounts for the psychological mechanism of internalization that Corbin lacks; Corbin gives the transjective world-disclosive dimension that Jung undersells (Ep 49).
