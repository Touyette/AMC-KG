# Enabling Constraints

> Conditions that open up rather than close down a system's possibilities; one half of the virtual engine structure.

## Vervaeke's framing
Juarrero distinguishes enabling constraints from selective constraints: whereas selective constraints reduce the options available to a system (like resource scarcity in evolution), enabling constraints increase the options (like genetic variation in evolution). Both types are needed for directed growth: without enabling constraints, a system has no generativity; without selective constraints, the generated options are undirected. Enabling constraints make things possible that would not otherwise be possible — they are conditions, not events, that shape the space of possibilities. Vervaeke applies this to character development: certain virtues and practices function as enabling constraints, opening up new modes of response and engagement that would not be available to the untrained self (Ep 06).
