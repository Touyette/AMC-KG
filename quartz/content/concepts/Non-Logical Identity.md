# Non-Logical Identity

> The identity relation that holds across genuine transformation: A-now and A-then are the same (there is a real identity) yet they do not share all the same properties (logical identity A=A fails); the core structure in aspiration, transframing, and the divine double.

## Vervaeke's framing
Logical identity (*A=A*) requires that two things be identical if and only if they share all the same properties. This is fine for mathematical objects. But genuine qualitative development — the kind that constitutes growth in wisdom, rationality, or personhood — involves a person becoming so different that logical identity fails: John born in Hamilton in 1961 and John in Toronto now do not share all the same properties. Yet they are the same person. This is non-logical identity (Ep 48).

**Why it matters**: Non-logical identity is the structure of every genuine transformation — transframing, gnosis, aspiration, the divine double journey. Each of these involves a movement in which who you are after the transformation is both continuous with (identity) and deeply different from (non-logical) who you were before. The problem: you cannot reason or infer your way from S1 to S2 because reasoning from S1 can only get you more-of-S1; to get to S2 requires a different kind of rationality — aspirational, imaginal, gnostic (Ep 48).

**Narrative's training**: Narrative trains us to work with non-logical identity across time (we trace out how John-in-Hamilton became John-in-Toronto through a temporal sequence of connected events). This training is valuable. But it can be exapted: instead of applying non-logical identity horizontally across narrative time, apply it *vertically* — across the ontological depth between the current self and the divine double/aspirational self. This vertical move is what Eckhart's "live without why" points to (Ep 48).

**Non-logical identity and the symbol**: The imaginal symbol is the cognitive tool that makes non-logical identity workable. The symbol simultaneously: (a) belongs to the current frame (you understand it from where you are now); (b) participates in the new frame (it opens you toward where you are going). This double participation in both frames is what makes the non-logical identity navigable — not through inference, but through the imaginal mode (Ep 48).

**Connection to aspiration**: Callard's "proleptic rationality" is one answer to the non-logical identity problem: the future S2 self normatively guides the current S1 self before the transformation is complete. The placeholder/symbol is the cognitive expression of this proleptic guidance. Vervaeke: the imaginal symbol (divine double) is the most powerful form of this placeholder (Ep 48).
