# Non-Duality

> In Vervaeke's account: the integrated mystical state that combines and transcends the Pure Consciousness Event and Resonant At-Onement — consciousness simultaneously at the depths of itself and the depths of reality, dissolving the subject-object divide without collapsing into either solipsism or self-dissolution.

## Vervaeke's framing
Non-duality is the third kind of mystical experience Vervaeke distinguishes. Unlike the Pure Consciousness Event (awareness without any object, from radical scaling down) and Resonant At-Onement (empathetic flowing participation with everything, from radical scaling up), non-duality holds both at once: consciousness is pressed simultaneously to the depth of the self and to the depth of reality, and these are experienced as perfectly continuous — not two. This is the prajña state, one meaning of wisdom in the Sanskrit tradition. It is the state that matters for Siddhartha's awakening because it affords a systematic insight — not an insight into this problem or that problem, but an insight of consciousness: a transformation of the whole machinery of the salience landscape, presence landscape, and depth landscape simultaneously. It is Vervaeke's account of what "awakening" actually is cognitively: the agent and the arena being pushed to their limits and found to be co-arising (Ep 09, Ep 10).
