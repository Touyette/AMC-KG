# Phronesis

> Aristotle's practical wisdom: contextual sensitivity — the domain-general capacity to know what virtue requires in *this* specific situation; not expertise (domain-specific) but the ability to sense what is relevant and fitting across all domains of life.

## Vervaeke's framing
Schwartz & Sharpe (2006) develop phronesis as the higher-order ability that resolves the four problems of virtue (relevance, conflict, specification, development). Phronesis is the capacity to sense which virtue is relevant here, how to balance conflicting virtues, how to apply a general principle to this specific context, and when to recognize that one lacks a needed virtue and must aspire to develop it. It involves knowing how (procedural knowledge) and knowing what it is like (perspectival/situational awareness) — the combinatorial application of procedural competence to a specific, context-laden situation (Ep 43).

Vervaeke's key critique of Schwartz & Sharpe: they describe phronesis as *expertise*, which creates a confusion. Expertise is domain-specific — tennis expertise generates interference in squash. But phronesis must be domain-general: one can be foolish in every domain of life simultaneously, so wisdom must address foolishness domain-generally. The capacity to be contextually sensitive is itself a domain-general ability (I must be contextually sensitive in professional, personal, civic, spiritual contexts). Better models: intelligence, rationality, relevance realization — all of which have a contextually sensitive component but are not locked to any particular domain (Ep 43).

**Sophia vs. phronesis**: Aristotle distinguishes sophia (theoretical wisdom — cross-contextual principles, deep perception of structural invariants) from phronesis (practical wisdom — contextual fitting). Schwartz & Sharpe treat sophia as mere rule-possession (propositional) and undervalue it. Vervaeke argues that sophia is not rules but *ontological depth perception* — the capacity to perceive deep generalizable principles that hold across situations. Phronesis then puts these principles into process; sophia regulates processes with principles. Their relationship should be opponent: principles into processes AND processes regulated by principles. This is how Aristotle means it: both are needed and each disciplines the other (Ep 43).
