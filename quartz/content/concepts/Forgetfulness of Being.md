# Forgetfulness of Being

> Heidegger: the epochal, structural tendency to mistake beings (particular things) for Being (the ground of realness and disclosure); to forget attunement and become trapped at the propositional-having-mode level; the philosophical expression of the meaning crisis.

## Vervaeke's framing
Heidegger's diagnosis of the entire Western philosophical-religious tradition is that it is a history of forgetfulness: we have progressively lost contact with Being (capital B) and become trapped at the level of beings (lowercase b) — things we can manipulate, possess, and propositionally define. This is not a single mistake by one philosopher but an epochal, structural tendency that has unfolded across the whole history of metaphysics, from Plato through the Scholastics to Descartes and onward (Ep 47).

**What is forgotten**: Attunement — the pre-cognitive, non-subjective fittedness-to-being that makes meaningful experience possible. When attunement is forgotten, we lose access to truth as aletheia (the disclosing uncovering of things as simultaneously shining and withdrawing). What remains is truth as correctness: statements match facts, but the ground of that matching — the open comportment toward being — is no longer attended to (Ep 47).

**The result**: Modal confusion. When attunement is forgotten, we slip into the having mode: beings become things to be grasped, manipulated, and conceptually possessed. "Man clings to what is readily available and controllable even where ultimate matters are concerned" (Heidegger). This applies even to God: classical theology makes God into the supreme being — a conceptually definable, ultimate being — which is the ultimate having-mode mistake (onto-theology) (Ep 47).

**Vervaeke's mapping**: Forgetfulness of Being = forgetting relevance realization at the ontological level. Just as modal confusion is the existential mistake of being stuck in the having mode (Fromm, Batchelor), forgetfulness of Being is its deep philosophical-ontological counterpart. Heidegger's project: to re-awaken us to Being through phenomenological questing (not theoretical argument) — a project structurally analogous to the Socratic awakening from self-deception (Ep 47).

**Why Heidegger's texts are deliberately difficult**: The very way we read — propositionally, seeking definite conclusions — is the forgetfulness Heidegger is trying to overcome. His texts deliberately resist propositional closure to force the reader into a different mode of engagement, like a Socratic elenchus at the ontological level (Ep 47).

**Connection to the meaning crisis**: For Vervaeke, forgetfulness of Being is the deep philosophical name for what the series has been tracing historically. The progressive loss of the three orders (nomological, normative, narrative), the Cartesian subjectivization, the scientific disenchantment — all of these are expressions of the forgetfulness of Being at the cultural-historical level (Ep 47).
