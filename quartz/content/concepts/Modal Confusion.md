# Modal Confusion

> The error of trying to satisfy being-needs (maturity, love, meaning, wisdom) within the having mode — generating a self-reinforcing cycle of frustrated pursuit and ever-greater consumption.

## Vervaeke's framing
Fromm's central diagnosis is that modern Western culture systematically confuses the modes: commercial culture profits by inducing people to meet their being needs through commodity acquisition. You need to become mature — here is a car you can have. You need to be in love — here is sex you can have. Because the mode is wrong, the real need is never met; instead the frustrated need drives ever-greater acquisition, which still fails to satisfy, producing the cycle of consumption Vervaeke identifies as a structural feature of the meaning crisis. Modal confusion is also self-reinforcing at the cognitive level: the more you operate in the having mode, the more you perceive everything categorically, and the harder it is to access the being mode at all. Siddhartha's palace is the mythological representation of a life locked in modal confusion (Ep 07). Heidegger's "forgetfulness of being" is identified as its deepest philosophical form (Ep 07).
