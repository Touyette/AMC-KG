# Transjectivity

> Relevance — and later symbols, sacredness, and the sacred itself — is neither purely subjective (projected from inside the mind) nor purely objective (detected as a pre-existing feature of the world), but a real co-created relation between the embodied organism and its environment.

## Vervaeke's framing
Vervaeke introduces "transjective" (Ep 31) to name the correct ontological category for relevance, resisting two common errors. The **empiricist** error: relevance is a property detected in objects (something out there has an intrinsic relevance waiting to be read off). The **romantic** error: relevance is projected from inside the subject (the mind imposes meaning on a neutral world). Both of these are "inherence" claims — they say relevance inheres in either the object or the subject. Both are wrong.

Relevance is instead a real relation between organism and environment, like a biological adaptation or like the graspability of a bottle. A bottle is graspable — but not because of the bottle alone, not because of the hand alone, but because of how they can be fitted together. Graspability is not projected; it is not detected; it is realized in the fitting. Similarly, adaptive fitness is not a property of the organism per se (a great white shark dies in the Sahara) — it is a real relation between organism and environment. Relevance is the cognitive version of this (Ep 31).

The word "**realization**" is chosen because it triangulates: the objective sense (making real — bringing a latent relation into actuality) and the subjective sense (coming into awareness — the organism becoming aware of what matters). Both senses apply simultaneously. This is why Vervaeke uses "relevance realization" rather than "relevance detection" or "relevance projection" (Ep 31).

Transjectivity is not limited to relevance. Later, Vervaeke applies it to the symbol (Ep 34), sacredness (Ep 33), and the sacred itself — all are transjective properties of the agent-arena relationship, not properties of mind alone or world alone.
