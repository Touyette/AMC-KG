# Existential Entrapment

> The combined condition of being stuck (existential inertia) AND stupefied (L.A. Paul's radical epistemic ignorance about transformative experience) — unable to move and unable to rationally decide whether to move.

## Vervaeke's framing
Existential entrapment compounds two incapacities. First, existential inertia: one cannot bring about the sensibility transcendence needed to make a new worldview viable despite wanting to. Second, the stupefaction described by L.A. Paul: one cannot calculate whether to undergo the transformation because one doesn't know what one will lose (the old self and world become "unthinkable" after transformation) and doesn't know what one is missing (the new self can only be known from within the transformed state). Together, these produce a condition in which the agent is neither able to stay (existential inertia makes the current mode self-destructive) nor able to rationally choose to leave (Paul's ignorance makes the calculus impossible) (Ep 17).

The person often manifests this as two-sided description of the same trait: "I'm stubborn" (negative) / "I'm persistent" (positive) — they simultaneously want to change and are identified with the very thing they want to change. Gnosis and serious play are the responses: not propositional argument but ritual contexts, enacted analogies, and altered states of consciousness that enable the liminal movement between worlds.
