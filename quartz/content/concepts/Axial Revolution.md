# Axial Revolution

> The global transformation ~800–300 BCE — triggered by the Bronze Age collapse — in which second-order thinking emerged and produced the religious and philosophical traditions still formative for us today.

## Vervaeke's framing

Karl Jaspers named the "Axial Age" to capture the fact that the figures and texts from this period (Socrates, Plato, Buddha, Confucius, the Hebrew prophets) still feel relevant to us, while earlier Bronze Age civilizations do not. Vervaeke takes this observation seriously and explains it structurally: the Bronze Age collapse (~1200 BCE) was the greatest civilizational catastrophe in history — more cities destroyed, more cultures lost, more literacy erased than at any other time — and this catastrophe functioned like the Paleolithic bottleneck, forcing radical cognitive and social experimentation. The experiments that took hold were two new psycho-technologies: alphabetic literacy (invented in Canaan, spread by the Phoenicians to Greeks and Hebrews) and coinage. Their exapted products — second-order thinking and abstract symbolic reasoning — gave people an unprecedented awareness simultaneously of their capacity for self-transcendence (we can correct our thinking!) and self-deception (we are systematically wrong in ways we could not previously see). This double awareness is the engine of Axial philosophy and religion: Socrates' dialectic, the Buddha's insight practices, the Hebrew prophets' moral critique all express it (Ep 02, 03, 04).
