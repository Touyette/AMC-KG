# Normativity on Construal

> Stanovich and West's concept: rationality requires an *independent* normative standard for how problems are construed (formulated, sized up, interpreted), not only standards for how conclusions are inferred; this non-inferential dimension connects rationality to insight.

## Vervaeke's framing
The impasse created by Smedslund's fallacy/misunderstanding distinction: we cannot use inferential success as the criterion for correct construal without circularity, because understanding a problem correctly just *is* drawing the right identities, contradictions, and implications — i.e., reasoning like the experimenter does. But then we cannot independently assess construal and inference: if participants understand the problem, they reason like scientists; if they don't reason like scientists on the task, maybe they misunderstood it. To break this deadlock, Stanovich and West argue we need a normativity on construal that is independent of inferential norms (Ep 41).

Vervaeke develops this further than Stanovich does. The normativity on construal is already studied in *insight problem-solving* research: a bad construal leads to combinatorial explosion (search space explodes), salience misdirection (wrong features are foregrounded), and irresolvable ill-definedness (the problem cannot even be made well-defined). A good construal does the opposite: it constrains the search space appropriately, directs attention to genuinely relevant features, and transforms ill-defined problems into tractable ones. This is what insight achieves at the meta-level — not a solution within a frame, but a better frame itself (Ep 41).

Therefore: **insight** (non-inferential, perspectival, pre-propositional) is a core component of rationality, not merely a psychological curiosity or a lucky accident. Rationality = insight + inference. This is why Stanovich's account of rationality as active open-mindedness (dealing with inferential biases) must be augmented with a normativity on construal (dealing with framing biases). And this is why rationality and wisdom increasingly overlap: wisdom is systematic transframing — which is systematic normativity on construal extended to the existential level (Ep 41).
