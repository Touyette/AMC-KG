# Religion That Is Not A Religion

> Vervaeke's working concept for a post-credal, religio-centered practice ecology that does what religion used to do — comprehensive transformation of consciousness, cognition, character, and culture — without requiring credal dominance, supernatural metaphysics, or institutional religion; grounded in RR, Religio, and Sacredness understood naturalistically.

## Vervaeke's framing
The need (Ep 39): many people (the "Nones" — those with no religious affiliation) are spiritually hungry but cannot return to organized religion in good conscience given the history of credal dominance, the fragmentation of the three orders, and the trauma of pseudo-religious political ideologies. Yet they need what religion provided: a comprehensive psycho-technology for transformation of consciousness, cognition, character, and culture, set within a community of practice with a legitimizing worldview.

Structural features of what this would look like (Ep 39):

**1. Religio over Credo**: Centered on the participatory-perspectival-procedural cultivation of Religio (the deep binding of agent to world through RR), not on assenting to propositional creeds. Credo has a legitimate function (signal detection — setting the criterion on what counts as genuine contact with Religio) but must always remain in the *service* of Religio, not its master. Credal dominance (credo as absolute, final criterion) is the pathological form to be avoided.

**2. Open-ended Mythos**: The symbols, stories, and rituals should never be treated as definitively and finally capturing The Sacred. They are indispensable vehicles (for particular people, communities, histories) but not metaphysically necessary. Sacred as inexhaustible grounds open-ended, evolving mythos.

**3. Three-level structure**: (a) Unconscious / participatory — the primordial RR that constitutes agent-arena relationship; (b) Consciousness / perspectival — salience landscaping, situational awareness, the criterion-setting function; (c) Cultural / distributed — propositional/communicable content (credo). Mythos must reach top-down (propositional → participatory) AND be open to bottom-up emergence (participatory → perspectival → propositional).

**4. Ecology of psycho-technologies**: Not a single practice but a counteractive dynamical system of mutually reinforcing, complementary practices — analogous to the eightfold path, but explicit and engineered rather than inherited. Practices should address all the perennial problems comprehensively.

**5. Meta-psycho-technology**: A collectively cultivated capacity for designing, evaluating, and evolving the ecology itself — explicitly and principled rather than intuitive. Co-op structures among communities; credo as wiki (collectively authored, dynamically updated paradigmatic content that remains always in service of religio). Individual wisdom (the meta-virtue) is the counterpart of the collective meta-psycho-technology: both operate at the level of coordinating and evolving the lower-order practices and virtues (Ep 39).
