# Credo

> Vervaeke: religion understood primarily as assent to propositional beliefs — the historically dominant (post-Reformation) mode of understanding what religion IS; contrasted with religio (participatory binding of attention and being) as a distortion of what religion can do.

## Vervaeke's framing
Credo (Latin: "I believe") is Vervaeke's term for the dominant modern mode of understanding religion: religion as a set of propositions one assents to (creed, doctrine, dogma). On this view, what makes one a Christian, Muslim, or Buddhist is primarily the beliefs one holds — which doctrines one affirms (Ep 39).

**How credal dominance developed**: The Protestant Reformation, particularly Luther's emphasis on faith as propositional belief over works and sacramental practice, accelerated the credalization of Christianity. Combined with the Cartesian grammar (mind = inner propositional representation space), credal religion became the default understanding: religion = inner beliefs that might or might not correspond to outer reality (Ep 39).

**Why credo is a distortion**: Credalization strips religion of what Vervaeke calls religio — the participatory, transformative, binding function: re-linking the self to the ground of being through attentional and existential practices. When religion becomes primarily credal: (1) it loses its psychotechnological function (practices that transform perspectival and participatory knowing); (2) it becomes merely propositional, subject to the same weaknesses as any proposition (easily asserted, easily denied, not transformative); (3) it generates the false dichotomy of theism/atheism (do you believe or not?) that obscures the deeper participatory question (are you in relationship to the ground of being or not?) (Ep 39).

**Credo vs religio**: The contrast Vervaeke draws is not that belief is unimportant but that making assent to propositions the *primary* or *exclusive* criterion of religious life is a category error — like defining skill by the ability to describe it propositionally. Religio names the participatory cultivation of relationship to what ultimately grounds meaning; credo names the having-mode grasp at propositions in place of that relationship. Tillich's faith as ultimate concern (participatory da'at directed at the ground of being) is precisely the recovery of religio against credal dominance (Ep 39, Ep 50).
