# Metacrisis

> The intertwining of multiple simultaneous civilisational crises — ecological, socioeconomic, political, mental-health, and existential — each of which amplifies the others, and whose resolution requires comprehensive transformation of consciousness, cognition, character, and culture.

## Vervaeke's framing
Vervaeke adopts the term from Tomas Björkman to name the condition that goes beyond any single crisis. The meaning crisis is not one problem among others but a constitutive feature of the metacrisis: because the meaning crisis erodes the shared orientations, wisdom traditions, and psycho-technologies needed to address any large-scale challenge, it makes the other crises harder to meet. The only historical institutions capable of delivering the 4C transformation (consciousness, cognition, character, culture) in an interdependent way have been religious traditions, but modernity has been traumatised by pseudo-religious ideologies and cannot simply return to traditional religion. The metacrisis thus sharpens the dilemma: we need something that functions like religion — systematic, participatory, transformative — but that is not a religion in the creedal, institutional sense (Ep 25). Vervaeke's cognitive science of Relevance Realization is offered as part of the intellectual groundwork for any genuine response.
