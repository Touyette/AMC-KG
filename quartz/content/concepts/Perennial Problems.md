# Perennial Problems

> Structural vulnerabilities that are inherent in the machinery of Relevance Realization and Religio, which make all people, in all cultures and all times, prone to specific forms of self-deceptive, self-destructive processing that undermine the agent-arena relationship and generate experiences of meaninglessness, despair, absurdity, and alienation — not as historical accidents but as necessary features of the adaptive machinery itself.

## Vervaeke's framing
The central insight (Ep 36): the same machinery that makes us adaptive — RR, Religio, the capacity for self-transcendence — is also the machinery that makes us prone to specific pathological patterns. We can't jump over our own shadows. These are *perennial* because they are features of the machinery, not of particular historical moments; they recur across cultures, eras, and individuals. The history of wisdom traditions (Buddhism, Stoicism, Neo-platonism, Christianity, etc.) is, in large part, the history of practices developed in response to these problems.

Vervaeke organizes the perennial problems around the three aspects of RR/Religio:

**Functional aspect** (how RR operates):
- *Self-organizing* → **Parasitic Processing**: complex cycles of self-deception and self-destruction that take on a life of their own and take life from us (addiction, avoidance, rumination, etc.)
- *Self-identification* → **Modal Confusion**: mistaking the having mode for the being mode; losing proper contact with participatory reality by trying to possess and control everything
- *Self-reflection* → **Reflectiveness Gap**: the trade-off between immersion (wantonness) and reflection (Hamlet's paralysis); neither extreme is good, and no stable middle exists — it must be dynamically calibrated

**Structural aspect** (the meta-meaning of the agent-arena relationship):
- **Absurdity**: the connectedness between self and world is lost; a clash of incommensurable perspectives in which meaning from within one frame is undermined by another frame; can escalate into horror
- **Alienation**: the connectedness between self and others is lost; isolation, loss of communitas, inability to participate in shared meaning
- **Existential Anxiety**: disconnection from oneself; not fear of a specific object but the nebulous, objectless threat of losing the self — Tillich's non-specific terror

**Developmental aspect** (the capacity to grow and transform):
- **Existential Inertia**: trapped in a worldview; unable to activate Anagoge and move to a more expansive one; the psyche has "frozen" in its current form
- **Existential Entrapment / Existential Ignorance**: indecision about transformation; one doesn't know what one will lose by transforming or what one will lose by not transforming; paralyzes the developmental process

The meaning crisis (Ep 36) = historical factors (fragmentation of worldview, delegitimization of traditions) × these perennial problems. The historical factors destroy the ecologies of practice that wisdom traditions developed to address the perennial problems, leaving both sides active: the problems persist, the responses are unavailable.

The response Vervaeke is working toward: **Reverse-Engineering Enlightenment** — using the RR machinery to understand what worked, why it worked, and reconstruct it in a naturalistic, scientifically integrable form.
