# Pseudo-Religious Ideology

> A secular movement that performs the structural functions of religion — integrating cognition, culture, and identity around a totalising narrative — without the systematic psycho-technologies that make genuine transformation possible.

## Vervaeke's framing
Vervaeke coins this term to name the chain of cultural movements that attempt to fill the gap left by the erosion of Christianity after Descartes and the Reformation. Romanticism is the first: it promises re-contact with reality through imagination, art, and irrational experience, sweeping Europe and reshaping culture, but it delivers only words, images, and promises — no practices, no institutions, no psycho-technologies. What follows is worse: Hegel's Absolute Idealism becomes the template for totalising secular ideologies (Ep 24). Nationalism, Marxism, and eventually Nazism are each pseudo-religious ideologies in this sense: they secularise the Hebraic promise of a historical utopia, replace God's plan with class struggle or racial destiny, and require violent participation in history's turning point (a secularised Kairos) as the path to salvation. All drench the world in blood precisely because they inherit the passion and the structure of religion without its safeguards or wisdom (Ep 25). We cannot return to these, and we cannot return to religion naively — this is the core dilemma of the meaning crisis.
