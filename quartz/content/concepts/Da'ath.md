# Da'ath

> Hebrew term for participatory knowing — knowing by identification, mutual transformation, and immersion — paradigmatically modeled on the intimacy of sexual union.

## Vervaeke's framing
Da'ath is the Hebrew word used for "knowing" in contexts like "Adam knew his wife Eve," which signals that it means something radically different from propositional belief. To know in the Da'ath sense is to participate in what is known: you are changed by it as you change it; you are immersed in it as in a course or a stream. It is the sense of being "on course," of correctly reading how history is flowing and acting accordingly. Faith (in its original Hebrew sense) is essentially this participatory knowing — the felt sense of being engaged in and aligned with the unfolding story of creation. Sin, conversely, is missing the mark not through immorality per se but through self-deception that takes one off course. Vervaeke contrasts Da'ath with modern propositional knowing to illustrate how much the depth of the knowing relationship has narrowed (Ep 03).
