# Metanoia

> Greek: radical transformation of the salience landscape and perspectival mode — not propositional "conversion" (change of beliefs) but participatory-perspectival rebirth; a new mode of being in the world.

## Vervaeke's framing
The word *metanoia* is routinely translated as "repentance" or "conversion," which imports a modern meaning of changing one's declared beliefs. The Greek etymology corrects this: *noia* = noticing, perspectival awareness; *meta* = beyond. Metanoia is therefore a "going-beyond-your-current-perspectival-mode" — a radical restructuring of what it is like to be you, what strikes you as salient, how you inhabit the world. Jesus's call to "be born again" is Vervaeke's entry-point: this is not asking for a new creed but for the kind of developmental transformation the series has been building toward — the agent-arena relationship reconfigured from within by a transformative encounter (Ep 15).

Metanoia is structurally analogous to the Stoic premeditatio, the Buddhist awakening, and the Platonic anagoge — all names for the shift from having-mode to being-mode identity, from horizontal (duration, accumulation) to vertical (depth, participation). But in the Christian framing, it is precipitated by encounter with Agape — a love that creates the conditions for becoming rather than responding to what one already is. The appropriate response to Jesus-as-Kairos is metanoia: the personal turning-point that mirrors the historical one.
