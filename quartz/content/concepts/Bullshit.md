# Bullshit

> Frankfurt-inflected concept applied by Vervaeke: a mode of communication (or self-communication) that bypasses the truth-tracking system entirely by capturing salience without concern for whether claims are true; distinct from lying, which depends on truth-tracking.

## Vervaeke's framing
Drawing on Harry Frankfurt's essay "On Bullshit," Vervaeke distinguishes two ways of producing false effects. A liar depends on the listener's commitment to truth: they assert "P" when they know "not-P," counting on you to update your beliefs based on the assertion. Bullshit, by contrast, does not depend on truth at all — it works by making things catchy, salient, emotionally resonant, so that the question of truth never becomes focal. Beer commercials are paradigmatic: they associate beer with attractive people and good times, and the consumer does not need to believe this is literally true for the salience-capture to work. The Sophists practiced a form of rhetorical bullshit: they could argue either side of any issue because they were not trying to track truth but to win the argument through salience manipulation. Vervaeke extends this to self-bullshitting as the mechanism of self-deception: you cannot lie to yourself, but you can direct attention in ways that make certain things so salient that truth-tracking is effectively bypassed (Ep 04).
