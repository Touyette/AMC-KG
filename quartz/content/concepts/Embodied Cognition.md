# Embodied Cognition

> The view that cognitive processes are not just "in the brain" but constitutively shaped by the body's morphology, sensorimotor capacities, and patterns of action; the body is not the vehicle of the mind but one of its constitutive conditions.

## Vervaeke's framing
Classical cognitive science modeled cognition as computation: the brain takes in sensory input, processes it according to rules, and produces behavioral output. The body is, in this picture, merely the transducer — the input/output device for the real cognitive work that happens inside the skull. Embodied cognition challenges this: the body's structure, its motor capacities, its proprioceptive feedback, and its patterns of action are constitutive of cognitive content and process, not incidental to them (Ep 26).

**Lakoff and Johnson's conceptual metaphor theory**: Vervaeke draws on Lakoff and Johnson's demonstration that abstract conceptual systems are grounded in bodily schemas. "Understanding" is "grasping"; "progress" is "moving forward"; "more" is "up." These are not accidental metaphors but systematic groundings of abstract concepts in bodily experience. Without the body's capacities of grasping, moving, and orienting, these conceptual systems would not have the content they do (Ep 26).

**Merleau-Ponty's lived body**: Maurice Merleau-Ponty is the central philosopher of embodied cognition. His account of the "lived body" (Leib as opposed to Körper, the objectified body) shows that perception and action are always already organized by the body's practical competencies — the phenomenal body's "I can" rather than the objective body's mechanism. The body is not an object that the mind controls but the medium through which the world is experienced and acted upon (Ep 26, Ep 38).

**Embodied cognition and optimal grip**: Merleau-Ponty's concept of optimal grip — the body's dynamic self-adjustment to achieve the most adequate engagement with a situation — is a paradigm case of embodied cognition. The adjustment is not directed by explicit rules but by the body's own proprioceptive sense of what would improve the engagement. This is relevance realization at the bodily level (Ep 26).

**Embodied cognition and RR**: If cognitive content is partly constituted by bodily schemas, then relevance realization is also embodied — the salience landscape is not just "in the brain" but is a product of the whole organism's engagement with its environment. This aligns with Vervaeke's argument that participatory knowing (bodily, sensorimotor, pre-predicative) is the foundation of all higher-order knowing (Ep 26, Ep 38).

**Connection to 4E cognition**: Embodied cognition is the first and most foundational of the four Es in [[4E Cognitive Science]], providing the biological and phenomenological grounding for the enactivist, extended, and ecological arguments (Ep 38).
