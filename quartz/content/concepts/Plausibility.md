# Plausibility

> Vervaeke's epistemic standard replacing certainty: credibility generated by convergence (trustworthiness) + elegance (multi-apt power) + fluency, balanced — producing, at its deepest, a sense of profundity.

## Vervaeke's framing
Plausibility is not a synonym for "probable." Vervaeke uses it in Reicher's sense — "makes good sense," "stands to reason," "should be taken seriously." It has three components: (1) **Convergence / Trustworthiness**: multiple independent lines of evidence pointing to the same thing reduces the probability of self-deception. Multi-sensory perception is the paradigm; science's quantification serves the same function by allowing cross-sensory convergence. (2) **Elegance / Power**: the same model applies across many domains (multi-aptness); it opens up new possibilities, transforms rather than merely describes. Conspiracy theories fail here — they have apparent power but no trustworthy evidence. Trivial truths fail on the other side — high convergence but no transformative power. (3) **Fluency**: the model must be internalisable and usable, not merely correct in theory. When convergence and elegance are both deep and properly balanced, and the processing is fluent, what results is profundity — the sense that something is deeply, not merely incidentally, true (Ep 12).

The key application: higher states of consciousness run on the same plausibility machinery the brain uses everywhere, including in science. HSC states are not certain guides but *plausible* ones — they maximize convergence (de-centering reduces bias), elegance (optimal grip on reality is maximally multi-apt), and fluency. Science itself depends on plausibility judgments at every stage (hypothesis selection, variable control, data interpretation), so rejecting HSC on the grounds that it lacks certainty would require rejecting science too.
