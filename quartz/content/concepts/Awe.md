# Awe

> Wonder scaled up: the state in which the sense of inexhaustibleness becomes overwhelming, pressing toward ongoing accommodation and the trajectory of transframing — the positive face of encountering what exceeds all current framing.

## Vervaeke's framing
Vervaeke treats awe (Ep 33) as the intensified, scaled-up form of wonder. Where wonder is the non-focal participatory awareness of the "atmosphere of significance," awe pushes further: it is the encounter with something that so vastly exceeds one's current framing capacity that it triggers ongoing **accommodation** (Piaget's term for restructuring one's schemas to take in what cannot be assimilated). In terms of Relevance Realization: awe is the state that arises when the combinatorial explosive inexhaustibleness of reality is not just glimpsed but confronted, pressing the system into continuous restructuring.

Awe sets off the **trajectory of transframing**: instead of gaining a specific insight that reframes a focal problem, one enters a cascade in which each reframing reveals further inadequacies of the framing process itself — an insight into one's own style of framing. This is not bounded and does not stabilize on a focal object. It is the positive side of confronting the phenomenological mystery of Religio. Pushed too far, it tips into **horror** (the negative face of the transgressive sacred — domicide, alienation, combinatorial collapse). The work of the numinous (Otto) is precisely to contain and shape this trajectory so that awe remains productive rather than overwhelming (Ep 33).

Awe is associated with Piaget's accommodation mechanism and therefore with resiliency, openness to novelty, and capacity for self-transcendence. This is why researchers like Fredrickson find awe to be among the most transformative of human emotions: it reconfigures the schemas rather than just filling them (Ep 33).
