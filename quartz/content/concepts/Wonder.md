# Wonder

> The being-mode analog of curiosity: non-focal, participatory-perspectival awareness of being immersed in the atmosphere of Religio; where curiosity focuses in on specific objects, wonder opens up to the inexhaustible gestalt.

## Vervaeke's framing
Vervaeke draws on Robert Fuller's work on wonder (Ep 33) to distinguish it carefully from curiosity. **Curiosity** operates in the having mode: it is focal, directed, problem-solving — "What is that? What does it do? How does it work?" — targeting specific objects and seeking information. **Wonder** operates in the being mode: it is non-focal, open, participatory — a kind of "Oh! Ahhh!" that is not directed at a specific problem but is an awareness of the atmosphere of significance in which one is immersed.

Drawing also on Paolo Costa ("A Secular Wonder"), Vervaeke argues that wonder is the emotion through which we become participatorily-perspectivally aware of Religio — the "bubble of significance" that one did not create but is absorbed by and emerges from. Costa describes it as "an atmosphere to which a living being must attune or adjust herself." Wonder is that attunement. Its function is not to solve problems but to remember Sati, to restore the being mode, to put one in touch with the ground of all meaning-making (Ep 33).

**Ep 42 — Plato vs. Aristotle on wonder**: Both hold that philosophy begins in wonder, but they diverge on what philosophy does with it. For *Plato*, the project of philosophy is to deepen wonder into awe — to intensify and sustain the sense of inexhaustibleness, precipitating meta-accommodation and anagogic ascent. Wonder is an engine of transformation. For *Aristotle*, philosophy shapes wonder into curiosity — focuses and resolves it into determinate questions with answers; this is meta-assimilation, stabilizing and integrating experience. Plato's wonder pulls toward the open and inexhaustible; Aristotle's wonder seeks closure and resolution. Vervaeke holds that both are needed: the need for cognition involves both curiosity (Aristotle) and wonder (Plato), and wisdom must coordinate them (Ep 42).

Wonder tends toward **awe** when scaled up: when the sense of the inexhaustibleness of reality becomes overwhelming, when the trajectory of transframing begins — when one stops having insights about a specific problem and starts having insights about the inadequacy of one's entire style of framing. Wonder is also related to **mystery** (the phenomenological mystery of Religio, the inability to focal-ize the framing process itself) and to the experience of **sacredness** (homing and worldview attunement) and **the numinous** (the transgressive face of the sacred).
