# The Great Dis-embedding

> Vervaeke's term (borrowing from Charles Taylor) for the Axial shift that separates the self from its embeddedness in the cosmos, making self-transcendence possible.

## Vervaeke's framing
Before the Axial Revolution, the self was constituted by how well it fit into the cycles and powers of the Continuous Cosmos. The Great Dis-embedding is the historical moment when that sense of radical belonging is disrupted: the self becomes something that can step back from the world, examine it critically, and aspire to a higher or more real mode of being. The self is now defined by its capacity for self-transcendence — "growing up" — rather than by conformity. This shift is mythologically expressed as the image of the stranger or pilgrim in the world, someone who doesn't ultimately belong here but belongs elsewhere (the real world). Vervaeke notes that the scientific worldview is driving a re-embedding that threatens to undo this transformation without finding an alternative path to wisdom (Ep 03).
