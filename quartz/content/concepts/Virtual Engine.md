# Virtual Engine

> A self-organizing dynamical system in which enabling constraints (opening possibilities) and selective constraints (reducing possibilities) are systematically coupled to a feedback cycle, producing directed growth and development.

## Vervaeke's framing
Juarrero coins "virtual governor" for the selective side and Vervaeke extends the metaphor to include the enabling side ("virtual generator"), combining them as a "virtual engine." The term "virtual" signals that this is not a physical machine but a shaping of possibility. In Darwinian evolution the feedback cycle is reproduction, the selective constraint is resource scarcity, and the enabling constraint is variation — together they constitute the evolutionary virtual engine. Applied to persons: your character is the virtual engine of your development. The virtues you have cultivated are the enabling and selective constraints that regulate your self-organizing development, determining which possibilities get actualized and which get ruled out. To live up to your potential is to have cultivated a virtual engine adequate to realizing your rational, reflective capacities — what is most distinctively human. Wisdom, for Aristotle, is gaining the ability to cultivate virtues that create this virtual engine (Ep 06).
