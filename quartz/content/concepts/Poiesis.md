# Poiesis

> Barfield: participatory creativity beyond passive reception or active making; the ekstasis of genuine creative engagement — standing beyond oneself in flow — that enacts self-transcendence and reconnects the agent to the inexhaustible moreness of reality.

## Vervaeke's framing
Barfield picks up poiesis (the Greek root of "poetry," translated as making/bringing into being) not as mere production but as the mode of creativity in which one *stands beyond oneself* — the same ekstasis that Tillich identifies at the depths of reason, now enacted in creative practice. Barfield locates poiesis in the flow state: the creativity experienced in genuine absorption where demands just exceed skill and the self is taken up into the activity (Ep 50).

This is importantly different from both alternatives that have dominated the tradition: (1) Heidegger's Gelassenheit (letting-be, pure passivity, deeply Lutheran), which takes from Eckhart only the willful cessation of imposition but forgets Eckhart's other key term Durchbruch (breakthrough — attentional scaling, frame-breaking, making a new frame); (2) the Cartesian technological will-to-mastery, which imposes form on passive matter. Poiesis is synergistic: neither passively receiving nor actively controlling (Ep 50).

**Synergism**: Vervaeke draws on Barfield to articulate this as the Hebrew da'at structure: God (= ground of being) plays the leading role, but we contribute. We are not passive recipients of history, nor its complete authors: we participate history. We participate *in* history and work synergistically with the inexhaustible in its making. This is the mode of transformation that aspiration, individuation, and the method of correlation all require (Ep 50).

**Language and original participation**: Barfield's key evidence for original participation (and poiesis as its recovery) is the dual inner/outer meanings of words — pneuma/spiritus (wind AND self-moving psyche); "see" (visual perception AND understanding); "attack" (physical destruction AND critical argumentation). For Barfield, these dual meanings are traces of a pre-Cartesian participation in which the intelligible and the sensible interpenetrated. Vervaeke's amendment: rather than Barfield's evolutionary hypothesis (ancient people had a different consciousness), the better account follows Lakoff/Johnson — this is synchronic psychological development within individuals (sensory-motor base gets taken up into conceptual function). Further, Vervaeke adds a top-down imaginal dimension: the abstract-intellectual and concrete-sensory meet in the imaginal, explaining why so many different sensory-motor bases ("see," "grasp," "get") all converge on a single abstract notion of understanding (Ep 50).

**Re-exaptation as poiesis**: Vervaeke connects poiesis to cognitive exaptation: using the balance circuitry to enact the concept of justice (Lady Justice = imaginal re-exaptation of physical balance into a symbol for the ineffable). The imaginal is precisely where sensory-motor machinery is re-exapted into new cognitive functions through an enacted symbol. This is poiesis in its deepest sense — the imaginal re-exaptation of established circuitry to enact genuinely new functions that weren't there before (Ep 50).
