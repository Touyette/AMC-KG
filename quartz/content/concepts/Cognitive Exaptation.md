# Cognitive Exaptation

> The reuse of a cognitive capacity — originally evolved or developed for one purpose — for a radically different function.

## Vervaeke's framing

Vervaeke borrows the biological concept of exaptation (a feature evolved for one purpose repurposed for another, e.g., feathers for flight after evolving for warmth) and applies it to cognition. The shaman's ritual practices for flow induction were originally adaptive for hunting and healing, but they exapted the brain's pattern-recognition and cross-domain connectivity machinery into a general capacity for metaphor and creative insight. Similarly, alphabetic literacy trained second-order thinking as a byproduct of practical scribal skills, and coinage trained abstract symbolic reasoning as a byproduct of commerce. Cognitive exaptation is how psycho-technologies routinely generate more than they were designed for — the surplus capacity is what makes civilizational leaps possible (Ep 02, recurring).
