# Paradox of Self-Creation

> Galen Strawson: genuine self-creation requires both continuity (S1 = S2, or it's not *self*-creation) and real novelty (S1 ≠ S2, or it's not *creation*) — these requirements contradict, making self-creation apparently impossible.

## Vervaeke's framing
Strawson identifies two requirements that genuine self-creation must satisfy simultaneously (Ep 49):

**Continuity requirement**: S1 and S2 must be the same self — if they are not, it is not an act of *self*-creation; some external force simply changed S1 into an unrelated S2 (like brain damage from a motorcycle accident). The "self" in self-creation requires S1 = S2.

**Real novelty requirement**: S1 and S2 must be genuinely different — if S2 is just more of what S1 already was (more skills, more beliefs of the same type), there is no *creation*; there is only quantitative accumulation. Real creation requires S1 ≠ S2 (qualitative difference, not just quantitative increase).

**The contradiction**: S1 must both equal and be genuinely different from S2 at the same time. Strawson concludes self-creation is self-contradictory — impossible.

**The two collapse options**: 
- *Empiricism*: Embrace real novelty (S1 ≠ S2) and abandon continuity — the self is a blank slate; external forces simply rewrite it; there is no underlying self. This abandons the "self" in self-creation.
- *Romantic expressionism* (Rousseau): Embrace continuity (S1 = S2) and abandon real novelty — the self was always already there, merely expressed and unfolded authentically. This abandons the "creation" in self-creation.

**Callard and Vervaeke's resolution**: The paradox dissolves when you recognize that the identity relation between S1 and S2 is *non-logical identity* — not A=A (logical identity) but the genuine-yet-transformative identity of a person through real qualitative development. The resolution has three components:
1. **Non-logical identity**: S1 and S2 are the same person, yet genuinely different — like John in Hamilton in 1961 and John in Toronto now.
2. **Participation in emergence**: S1 neither makes S2 (pure activity) nor receives S2 from outside (pure passivity) but participates in S2's emergence — S2 emerges out of S1 such that S1 disappears into S2.
3. **Normative asymmetry**: S1 is temporally prior (causes S2), but S2 is normatively primary (only S2 can justify, explain, and legitimate S1's aspirational actions). This is Callard's proleptic rationality: the future self normatively governs the current self's aspirational behavior (Ep 49).

**Connection to Kierkegaard's leap**: The leap of faith (Kierkegaard) is the same structure: leaping into a developmental process that will bring about qualitative identity change. The leap is not irrational but aspiration-rationally motivated — proleptic rationality in action (Ep 49).
