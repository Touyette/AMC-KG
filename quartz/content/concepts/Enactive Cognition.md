# Enactive Cognition

> The view (Varela, Thompson, Rosch) that cognition is not the passive processing of pre-given representations but the active, organism-driven bringing-forth of a world of significance through sensorimotor coupling; mind enacts its environment rather than representing it.

## Vervaeke's framing
Enactivism challenges both classical cognitivism (mind as rule-governed symbol manipulation) and simple embodiment (mind in a body that still processes representations). The enactivist claim is more radical: there is no pre-given world of features "out there" waiting to be represented. Instead, the organism and environment co-determine each other through ongoing sensorimotor coupling. What counts as a relevant feature of the environment is not fixed independently of the organism's action capabilities — it is enacted through the organism's mode of being-in-the-world (Ep 26).

**Maturana and Varela's autopoiesis**: Enactivism draws on the biology of autopoiesis: living systems are self-producing, self-maintaining processes that also produce their own "cognitive domain" — the space of distinctions relevant to their maintenance. What the organism "perceives" is what is relevant to its ongoing self-production, not an objective landscape of features. Cognition is therefore inseparable from the organism's mode of life (Ep 26).

**Connection to affordances and RR**: Enactivism aligns with Gibson's ecological psychology of affordances: what an organism perceives is what it can do, not what is "objectively there." Vervaeke links this to relevance realization: enaction is the deep biological form of RR — organisms enact relevance landscapes rather than represent them. The enactivist account provides biological grounding for why RR cannot be reduced to symbolic computation (Ep 26).

**Enactivism and participatory knowing**: Enaction is a form of participatory knowing — the organism participates in the environment by enacting it, rather than standing over against it as a representing subject facing an objective world. This makes enactivism a natural ally of Vervaeke's argument for the irreducibility of participatory knowledge and against the Cartesian grammar (Ep 26, Ep 38).

**4E cognition**: Enactivism is the most radical of the four Es in 4E cognitive science — [[Embodied Cognition]], [[Enactive Cognition]], [[Extended Cognition]], [[4E Cognition]]. Where embodiment says the body matters for cognition, enactivism says the organism-environment coupling is constitutive of cognition itself. Together with extension, enactivism implies that there is no "internal" cognition cleanly separable from the organism's engagement with its world (Ep 38).
