# Physis

> Greek: blossoming forth from itself — pure self-grounded emergence; non-teleological being; used by Heidegger (via Eckhart's "live without why") as the name for the being mode that transcends narrative teleology and engages Being vertically.

## Vervaeke's framing
Physis is introduced through Heidegger's commentary on Angelus Silesius's rose poem ("the rose is without why / it blooms because it blooms") and Eckhart's maxim "live without why." The Greek physis names a mode of being that is not governed by external purpose or narrative teleology: the rose doesn't bloom in order to achieve something, or as part of a grand narrative. It blooms because it blooms — pure self-grounded emergence (Ep 48).

**Not meaninglessness**: Physis is not nihilistic. Vervaeke is careful to distinguish it from meaninglessness. The rose exists in the richest possible engagement with being — it simultaneously shines (phenomenological presence: it appears to the world) and withdraws (its ground is inexhaustible: no framing captures it fully). This is the thing beyond itself made concrete and beautiful. Physis is thus not the absence of meaning but the presence of being at its most direct — unmediated by teleological narrative (Ep 48).

**Vertical ontology vs horizontal teleology**: Ordinary narrative meaning-making operates on the horizontal axis: I have a purpose, a story, I am going somewhere in time. Physis names the alternative — vertical ontological engagement: rather than extending horizontally toward a goal, one drops deeper into the inexhaustible ground of Being itself. The non-logical identity trained by narrative can be exapted from this horizontal axis to the vertical: instead of becoming a different self through achieving a purpose, one becomes a different self through deeper contact with Being (Ep 48).

**Connection to Eckhart's gelassenheit**: "Live without why" is Eckhart's formulation of physis as a mode of human life. It is the cessation of the having mode's constant asking "what is this for?" in favor of the being mode's dwelling in what is. But Vervaeke (via Barfield) notes that Heidegger takes only this passive side of Eckhart (gelassenheit) and forgets the active side (durchbruch/breakthrough). Physis alone is necessary but not sufficient — it must be complemented by the creative breakthrough of poiesis (Ep 48, Ep 50).
