# Transcendence Into

> Vervaeke's term for the movement of self-transcendence that goes inward and downward rather than outward and upward — not transcending the world but transcending the surface self to reach greater depth in the self and thereby in reality; contrasted with "transcendence of" (escape from).

## Vervaeke's framing
The standard picture of transcendence is "transcendence of": escaping from the world, the body, the self — getting beyond or above ordinary reality to a higher plane. Vervaeke distinguishes this from **transcendence into**: a movement that goes deeper into the self and deeper into the world simultaneously, not by escaping but by penetrating to a level of reality that is shared between self and world (Ep 38).

**The direction of the movement**: Transcendence into does not go "up and out" but "in and through." The Socratic turn inward (the examined life, gnōthi seauton — know thyself) is transcendence into: by going more deeply into the self, one encounters the logos, the rational-participatory structure of reality itself. The Neoplatonic anagoge is transcendence into: not fleeing the world but ascending through it by going deeper into its ground. The mystical encounter with the ground of being (Eckhart's Gottheit, Tillich's ground of being) is transcendence into: not an encounter with something "outside" but a contact with the depth of what is (Ep 38).

**Transcendence into vs. having mode**: Transcendence of easily becomes a having-mode project: getting above ordinary reality so as to possess a higher reality. Transcendence into is constitutively anti-having: it requires exactly the kind of letting-go, the Gelassenheit, that is the opposite of grasping. You cannot possess depth by reaching for it from the outside; you discover it by relaxing the surface (Ep 38).

**Connection to contact epistemology**: Transcendence into is what Vervaeke calls for in the recovery of participatory knowing: not a retreat from the world into a private inner space, but a deepening of contact with reality through the kind of self-knowledge that opens to what is shared between self and world. The Platonic dialectic, Ignatian discernment, Zen koan practice, and psychedelic therapy (properly conducted) all aim at transcendence into — they use the self as a doorway into the depths of reality (Ep 38).
