# Self-Organizing Criticality

> Per Bak: systems that self-organize toward a critical state — oscillating between high-order structure and avalanche-collapse — allowing them to continuously evolve their organization and acquire emergent capacities; applied to the brain as the neural firing implementation of relevance realization.

## Vervaeke's framing
Vervaeke draws on Per Bak's work (Ep 32) to show how relevance realization might be implemented in the brain's firing patterns. The sand-dune analogy: grains of sand fall randomly, but through friction, gravity, and bounce (a virtual engine), they self-organize into a mound. The mound grows until it hits a critical point — then an avalanche occurs. Crucially, the avalanche spreads the base out, so the next mound that forms can be larger, with emergent capacity the previous mound lacked. The system then cycles: structure → criticality → avalanche → new structure at higher level. This is SOC.

Mapped onto the brain (Ep 32): when neurons fire in synchrony, they are doing compression/integration (line-of-best-fit style generalization — finding what's invariant). When they go asynchronous — the "neural avalanche" — they are doing particularization/differentiation (opening up variation, breaking existing structure). Then they reconfigure into new synchrony, better fitted to the current situation. This cycling is scale-invariant: it happens at every level of brain organization simultaneously, making it a plausible implementation of RR's need to operate both locally and globally. Thatcher et al. (2008/2009) found that greater flexibility in the synchrony/asynchrony oscillation (more "evolve-ability") predicts higher general intelligence — convergent with RR = g.

SOC also explains insight (Stephan and Dixon; Schilling 2005): entropy goes up right before insight (the neural avalanche — breaking the existing frame), then drops sharply as the brain restructures into a better, more optimal configuration. The avalanche converts a regular network into a small world network — a sudden optimization of RR (Ep 32).
