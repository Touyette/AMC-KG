# Spirit of Finesse

> Pascal's term for the procedural, perspectival, and participatory knowing — the sensitivity to timing, context, and mutual disclosure — that is irreducible to mathematical computation.

## Vervaeke's framing
Pascal contrasts the Spirit of Geometry (the mathematical, computational, propositional mode of knowing championed by Descartes) with the Spirit of Finesse (the knowing involved in doing anything with appropriate timing, sensitivity, and participatory engagement — like playing jazz, performing martial arts, or kissing well). Pascal's concern, which Vervaeke endorses, is that the scientific revolution has caused us to lose the Spirit of Finesse entirely, leaving us trapped in the Spirit of Geometry as our sole model of knowing. This is a direct diagnosis of the meaning crisis: the loss of procedural knowing (how), perspectival knowing (what it's like), and participatory knowing (being bound up in mutual transformation) means we have lost the machinery for transformative experience and self-transcendence. Pascal had a transformative experience himself and found the Cartesian framework incapable of addressing it (Ep 22).
