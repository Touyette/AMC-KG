# Reciprocal Narrowing

> Marc Lewis's model of addiction as a learned agent-arena narrowing: as cognitive flexibility decreases, the world's perceived options decrease, and the two reinforce each other until agency is entirely trapped.

## Vervaeke's framing
Against the biochemical compulsion model of addiction, Marc Lewis proposes that addiction is a *learned* participatory-perspectival state: a progressive narrowing of both the agent (loss of cognitive flexibility and identity options) and the arena (loss of perceived world-options) in a reciprocal loop. The agent's narrowed cognition sees fewer options in the world; the world's fewer options confirm the narrowed sense of self. This is not primarily about biochemistry — soldiers who became addicted to opioids in Vietnam mostly stopped spontaneously upon returning home, because their agent-arena relationship (identity and world) fundamentally changed.

Vervaeke reads Reciprocal Narrowing as the external analogue of **Parasitic Processing** (internal): both spirals interpenetrate — they are Dukkha understood simultaneously from within and without. The inverse of Reciprocal Narrowing is anagoge: the agent-arena relationship spiraling upward, agent and world mutually opening out, options expanding reciprocally toward enlightenment (Ep 13).
