# Geist

> Hegel's term for the living, self-developing system of patterns of intelligibility through which both mind and reality are constituted and co-evolve historically.

## Vervaeke's framing
Geist (German for "mind/spirit") names the extended, supra-individual intelligence that Hegel proposes once Kant's unknowable "Thing in Itself" is removed. If reality is just patterns of intelligibility, and if these patterns form a self-organizing, developing system, then that system — Geist — is simultaneously how we make sense and how reality is structured. Unlike Kant's individual mind, Geist operates through the whole history of human thought and culture. It develops through Dialectic (thesis, antithesis, higher synthesis), gradually becoming more systematic and self-aware, until in Hegel's "Absolute Idealism" it achieves self-understanding (Ep 24). Vervaeke reads Geist as Hegel's secularisation of the Hebraic–Christian God working through history. Its power lies in integrating mythology and philosophy, science and spirit; its danger is that it provides the template for totalitarian ideologies that claim to embody the telos of history.
