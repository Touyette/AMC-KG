# Kairos

> The qualitative "right moment" — a turning point in a narrative or historical course where transformation or redirection becomes possible and necessary.

## Vervaeke's framing
Kairos is distinct from chronos (quantitative, sequential time) and belongs to the Hebrew-inflected understanding of time as a cosmic story with direction and purpose. Where chronos simply marks duration, kairos marks the moment when action can redirect the course of history — when a prophet wakes people up to how far off course they have gone, or when an individual finds themselves at a moral turning point. Vervaeke connects kairos to the sense of being "on course" in Da'ath — participatory knowing of one's place in the unfolding story. Kairos reappears in Christian theology (the incarnation as the decisive kairos) and in secular forms whenever we speak of "seizing the moment" or recognizing that now is the time to change (Ep 03).
