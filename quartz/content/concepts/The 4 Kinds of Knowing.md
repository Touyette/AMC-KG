# The 4 Kinds of Knowing

> Vervaeke's framework distinguishing four irreducibly different modes of knowing: propositional, procedural, perspectival, and participatory; wisdom requires all four in dynamic integration.

## Vervaeke's framing
Vervaeke argues that the standard reduction of knowing to propositional knowledge (knowing-that) leaves out the vast majority of what actually matters in wisdom and meaningful engagement with the world. Procedural knowing (knowing-how) captures embodied skills that are never fully articulable in propositions. Perspectival knowing (knowing what it's like) captures the irreducible first-person character of experience. Participatory knowing captures the deepest mode — knowing through identity and mutual transformation, as in Plato's anagoge or the Hebrew Da'ath — where the knower is genuinely changed by what is known and the known is encountered in its fullness rather than merely described from outside. The failure of the modern worldview to cultivate all four kinds of knowing, and especially to honor participatory and perspectival knowing, is part of what drives the meaning crisis (Ep 05, developed across many subsequent episodes).
