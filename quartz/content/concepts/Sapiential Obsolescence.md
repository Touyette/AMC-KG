# Sapiential Obsolescence

> The cultural loss of institutions dedicated to cultivating wisdom, leaving knowledge bound only to political power.

## Vervaeke's framing
Vervaeke coins this term to describe what Luther's Reformation produced when it condemned the monasteries and dismantled the knowledge-wisdom pairing that had structured Western education. Before Luther, universities (knowledge) were paired with monasteries (wisdom) — the latter providing transformation and self-transcendence, the former providing information. Luther's attack on self-transcendence as the sin of pride led to closing the monasteries, leaving the university attached not to wisdom but to the State. Knowledge became power (Bacon), and the question of how to transform knowledge into existential wisdom became unanswerable. In modern culture we know where to go for information but not where to go for wisdom (Ep 21).
