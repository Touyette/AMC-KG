# Pure Consciousness Event

> Robert Forman's term for the mystical experience in which one is conscious without being conscious of any object — the result of radical scaling down in attentional practice.

## Vervaeke's framing
The Pure Consciousness Event (PCE) is the experience that results from pushing attentional scaling down to its limit: you step back from looking at the world, through the subsidiary layers of processing, all the way into pure consciousness itself. There is no object of consciousness — no sensation, no image, no content. You are simply conscious, fully present as awareness without any intentional directedness. Forman presents this as evidence for a non-intentional mode of awareness that cannot be explained by standard representationalist accounts of mind (which hold that all consciousness is always "of" something). Vervaeke notes that PCE is the result of taking meditation (scaling down) to its extreme, and it is a genuine and distinctive mystical experience. However, it is not the goal of Buddhist practice: it is only one pole of the opponent process. The goal is non-duality, which integrates PCE with its opposite (resonant at-onement from radical scaling up) (Ep 09).
