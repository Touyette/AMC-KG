# Sensibility Transcendence

> A bi-directional participatory insight that simultaneously transforms how one sees another person (Arena) and how one sees oneself (Agent) — both agent and arena co-transcend together.

## Vervaeke's framing
The concept comes from John Wright (building on Iris Murdoch's *Sovereignty of Good*): Murdoch's mother-in-law example shows a woman who dislikes her daughter-in-law as coarse and uncouth, and then undergoes an insight that is not a normal insight. In a normal insight, one reframes how one sees *something*. In sensibility transcendence, one simultaneously reframes how one sees another person *and* how one sees oneself — these are interfused. The mother-in-law comes to see the daughter-in-law as spontaneous rather than coarse, sincere rather than uncouth. But simultaneously she sees that her own habitual way of framing has been systematically wrong. Both agent and arena go through transcendence together, in a coupled fashion (Ep 17).

Sensibility transcendence is Vervaeke's cognitive-science gloss on anagoge: the moral and perceptual transformation that is simultaneously a self-transformation. It is what Gnosis does and what serious play enables. Its inverse is existential inertia — the inability to bring it about. The key feature is the *bi-directionality*: it is not merely a perspectival shift but a participatory one, transforming one's very identity and the object of one's attention simultaneously.
