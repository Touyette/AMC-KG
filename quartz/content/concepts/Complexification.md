# Complexification

> The developmental process by which a system becomes simultaneously more differentiated (can do more different things) and more integrated (stays together as a unified system) — generating qualitative self-transcendence and emergent abilities.

## Vervaeke's framing
Vervaeke introduces complexification (Ep 31) as the principle that makes RR inherently developmental rather than merely dynamic. The key is that when a system is engaged in opponent processing between compression/integration (Piaget's assimilation) and particularization/differentiation (Piaget's accommodation), it is simultaneously doing two things: absorbing new information into existing schemas AND restructuring its schemas to accommodate what they cannot assimilate. A system that is successfully doing both at once — highly integrated AND highly differentiated — is complexifying.

Why does this generate self-transcendence? Because a highly differentiated system can do many different things (large functional repertoire), but without integration it would fly apart. A highly integrated system holds together but without differentiation it is brittle and inflexible. When a system achieves both, it self-organizes into something qualitatively new — it acquires emergent abilities it did not have before. The biological analogy: you began as a zygote (a single undifferentiated cell); cellular differentiation produced lung cells, eye cells, neurons; integration organized them into organs and organ systems. The result: emergent abilities (language, abstract reasoning, social cognition) that the zygote had no capacity for. The system self-transcended (Ep 31).

For RR: insofar as relevance realization operates through a self-organizing dynamical system, it is inherently complexifying and therefore inherently developmental and self-transcending. The system develops by functioning (every episode of RR shapes the sensory-motor loop) and functions by developing (the development enables more sophisticated RR). This makes self-transcendence not an add-on to cognition but a structural feature of any sufficiently complex relevance-realizing system (Ep 31).
