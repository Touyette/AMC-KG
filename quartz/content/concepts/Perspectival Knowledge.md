# Perspectival Knowledge

> The third of the four kinds of knowing: the irreducibly first-person character of experience — knowing what it's like — which cannot be fully captured by any third-person description.

## Vervaeke's framing
Perspectival knowledge is what Nagel pointed to when he asked "what is it like to be a bat?" — the subjective, first-person character of experience that is not captured by any objective description of the bat's echolocation system. It is the difference between knowing the anatomy of pain and knowing what pain feels like. This mode of knowing is irreducibly perspectival: it is always knowing from a particular vantage point. It is also why the prisoner in Plato's Cave is not simply given new facts about the world — the anagogic ascent involves a transformation of perspective, a first-person shift in what it is like to engage with reality. Perspectival knowledge is at stake in the agent-arena relationship: what one knows as an agent in a particular arena is irreducibly perspectival, not capturable from outside (Ep 05).
