# Deautomatization

> The disruption of automatic cognitive framing — through mindfulness, fasting, psychedelics, or other strategies — that opens the system to new modes of attention and deeper pattern detection.

## Vervaeke's framing
Deautomatization is what disruptive strategies produce: they break the unconscious, automatic framings that normally constrain processing. The paradigm case is the nine-dot problem, where solvers automatically see a square and cannot escape it — deautomatization is the process of dissolving that bad frame. More broadly, disruptive strategies (meditation, sleep deprivation, chanting, psychedelics, even incubation like taking a shower) increase variation in what the system is attending to and how it is processing. This increased variation does two things: it helps detect bad invariants (systematic fixated errors that are blocking development) and good invariants (real patterns that are invariant across variation). When variation is opened up massively, a cognitive-developmental shift becomes possible — not just solving one problem but finding the nexus of errors and transforming the whole framing machinery. The Ep 11 argument is that the same mechanism underpins both ordinary insight and the deep self-transcendence of higher states of consciousness (Ep 11).
