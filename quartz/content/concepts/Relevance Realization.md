# Relevance Realization

> The cognitive process by which an organism homes in on what matters — selecting relevant information from an astronomically vast array of stimuli, memory, and combinatorial possibilities; Vervaeke's central explanatory mechanism across the entire series.

## Vervaeke's framing
Vervaeke argues that the core function of consciousness is to help the organism realize relevance: to select out of everything that could be processed what actually needs to be processed, and to do so reliably, flexibly, and in a self-organizing fashion without a homunculus. Drawing on Global Workspace Theory, Integrated Information Theory, and Bor & Seth's work, he shows that what consciousness is doing — when it featurizes, foregrounds, configures, and frames — is one continuous process of zeroing in on relevant information. This is not the same as saying all relevance realization is conscious; much of it is unconscious. What consciousness does is coordinate and optimize this process for novel situations requiring insight. The entire arc of the series is building toward an account of how relevance realization, running continuously from biological life to human wisdom to mystical awakening, provides the deep continuity that could ground a response to the meaning crisis. The salience landscape, presence landscape, depth landscape, and significance landscape are all aspects of how relevance realization is structured experientially (Ep 10).
