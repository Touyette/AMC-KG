# Metaphorical Cognition

> The pervasive use of cross-domain bridging in thought and language — not a rhetorical ornament but the core mechanism of insight and meaning-making.

## Vervaeke's framing

Drawing on Lakoff's work, Vervaeke argues that cognition is saturated with metaphor: "grasp", "see", "get my point", "halfway through" are all metaphors we use without noticing. What the shaman's flow state produces neurologically is increased cross-talk between brain areas that do not normally communicate. When this cross-talk is combined with enhanced insight and implicit learning, the result is a dramatic increase in the capacity to connect ideas across domains — which is precisely what metaphor does. The word "metaphor" itself (from Greek *metapherein*: to carry over, to bridge) encodes this: it connects the non-connected. "Soul flight" — the shaman's experience of flying above the world to gain a comprehensive view — is therefore a metaphor expressing what has genuinely happened cognitively: a broader, more integrated grasp of reality. Later in the series, Vervaeke distinguishes this basic metaphorical cognition from the *profound* or *participatory* metaphor at work in symbols, where the connection does not just reorganize thought but transforms participation in being (Ep 02; extended in Ep 34–35).
