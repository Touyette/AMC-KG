# Wisdom

> Vervaeke's developed definition: the capacity to keep truth-tracking and relevance/salience tightly coupled — preventing bullshit and self-deception — extended by Aristotle to include character cultivation, and ultimately described as an ecology of psycho-technologies that dynamically enhances relevance realization across all four kinds of knowing.

## Vervaeke's framing
Socrates introduces wisdom as knowing what you do not know — a direct, painful confrontation with one's own capacity for bullshitting oneself. The wise person keeps what they find salient (what they care about) tightly coupled to what is true (what is real). This is what "ta erotika" means for Socrates: knowing how to love well, i.e., knowing what genuinely deserves one's care. Plato develops this into an account of inner justice: wisdom is the right ordering of the Tripartite Psyche (reason trained, thymos trained, appetite tamed) such that inner conflict and self-deception are minimized, producing a fullness of being and clearer contact with reality (Ep 05). Aristotle adds the dimension of character: wisdom is not just a state of the psyche but a cultivated virtual engine — a set of virtues (enabling and selective constraints) that regulate one's development so that one actualizes one's potential as a rational being (Ep 06). In the context of the whole series, Vervaeke converges on wisdom as an ecology of psycho-technologies that dynamically enhances relevance realization across all four kinds of knowing: propositional, procedural, perspectival, and participatory.

Ep 39 begins the systematic cognitive science of wisdom. McKee and Barber (1999) perform a **reflective equilibrium** between all prior philosophical theories (Socratic, Aristotelian, Stoic, Platonic) and all prior psychological theories of wisdom, looking for convergent themes. Both traditions converge on: **wisdom is the systematic seeing through illusion and into reality** (or what is more real). "Seeing through illusion" means seeing through cognitive and existential self-deception. "Systematic" means this is not a one-off insight — it is a multi-domain intervention that operates across many related problem instances simultaneously (like a gestalt shift rather than a local fix). "Into reality" is the necessary counterpart: you can only know you've seen through illusion if you arrive at something less illusory. Vervaeke and Ferraro (2013) add this necessary second pole. The result: wisdom is a profound kind of systematic insight — a structural transframing that reorganizes an entire region of one's salience landscape (Ep 39).

This connects directly to the rest of AMC: systematic seeing through illusion and into reality = systematic RR (correcting misleading salience patterns and problem formulations across domains) operating at the level of existential identity and worldview, not just individual problems. Wisdom = reliably doing this. This is also what Reverse-Engineering Enlightenment is targeting: building the capacity for reliable, systematic transframing as the developmental response to the perennial problems.

**Ep 43 — Wisdom and virtue**: Schwartz & Sharpe (2006) demonstrate that virtue lists (treating virtues as logically independent) are inadequate. Virtues conflict, require contextual specification, and raise the question of which is relevant. A higher-order ability is needed — that is wisdom. Either wisdom coordinates the virtues (they are logically interdependent in a system) or each virtue is simply the way of being wise in a specific type of situation (the strongest version of the unity of virtue thesis). Vervaeke adds: wisdom must also address the *development* problem — sometimes the right response is recognizing I lack a virtue and must aspire to cultivate it (Ep 43).

**Sophia and phronesis as opponent pair**: Phronesis (practical wisdom) = domain-general contextual sensitivity; sophia (theoretical wisdom) = cross-contextual principle perception (ontological depth perception, not mere rule-possession). Wisdom requires both: principles into processes (sophia → phronesis) AND processes regulated by principles (phronesis → sophia). Their opponent relationship ensures neither abstract principle nor situated habit dominates at the expense of the other (Ep 43).

**Berlin Wisdom Paradigm (Baltes & Staudinger)**: Wisdom as metaheuristic for orchestrating mind and virtue toward excellence; five criteria include factual and procedural knowledge, lifespan contextualism, fallibilism + humility, and management of uncertainty. First empirical theory; "dripping with relevance realization" machinery. Criticized by Vervaeke for (a) understanding wisdom as expertise (domain-specific) and (b) lacking explicit perspectival and participatory knowing (Ep 43).

**Ep 44 — Product vs. Process theories**: A *product theory* specifies wisdom's features; a *process theory* specifies how one becomes wise — starting from (a) what is foolishness? (b) how does foolishness develop? (c) how can it be overcome? (d) what flourishing does overcoming it afford? Ancient traditions are process theories. Modern psychological theories are mostly product theories. Vervaeke argues both are needed in dialogue (Ep 44).

**Ardelt's three-dimensional model**: (1) *Cognitive* — grasping the significance (interpretive knowledge, not just descriptive); (2) *Reflective* — multiperspectival, self-examination, self-insight; overcomes egocentrism perspectivally; (3) *Affective* — agape; overcomes egocentrism motivationally; connects wisdom to meaning in life. All three mutually influence each other. Ardelt's key critique of Berlin paradigm: modal confusion — having knowledge about wisdom ≠ being wise; one must *realize* it in self-transformative practice (Ep 44).

**Sternberg's Balance Theory**: Optimization of intrapersonal/interpersonal/extrapersonal interests toward adapting/shaping/selecting environments, mediated by tacit understanding. Balance = Piagetian equilibration = optimization. Directed toward virtue + meaning in life (Vervaeke's amendment). Intrapersonal/interpersonal/extrapersonal ≈ three dimensions of meaning in life. Criticized for circular account of foolishness (imbalance = lack of wisdom), underspecified values, and being a product theory without process (Ep 44).

**Ep 45 — Vervaeke & Ferraro (2013) process theory and final definition**: Three integrated competences: (1) AOM + Inferential (propositional knowing, sophia, overcoming fallacious reasoning); (2) Mindfulness + Insight (procedural knowing, phronesis, overcoming misconstrual); (3) Perspectival knowing / Internalization (internalizing the sage, overcoming egocentrism). Directed toward morality, meaning in life, and mastery. Overarching cognitive style: sophrosyne — the optimization of perspectival knowing such that vice/self-deception loses its pull (second-nature disposition, not willpower). Lacunae: missing participatory knowing, transformative experience, gnosis, theory of understanding, account of aspiration (Ep 45).

**Wisdom — final definition**: "An ecology of psychotechnologies and cognitive styles that dynamically enhance relevance realization in inference, insight and intuition, internalization, understanding, and gnosis, transformation, and aspiration." Wisdom is a counteractive dynamical system opposed to self-deception that affords self-organized transformation into a deeply meaningful life. It is not a state but a continuous, self-improving process — rationality applying itself to itself toward the fullest possible enhancement of RR across all four kinds of knowing (Ep 45).
