# Bio-Economy

> The resource allocation constraints within which biological and cognitive systems operate; organisms must balance investment of energy and information-processing across competing demands, which is why relevance realization is not optional but the fundamental form of biological rationality.

## Vervaeke's framing
Every biological organism operates under a bio-economy: the total resources available (energy, time, neural tissue, attention) are finite, and allocating more to one function means allocating less to others. There is no free lunch in biological information processing. This bio-economic constraint is the deep reason that relevance realization is not a convenient feature of minded beings but a biological necessity: any organism that tried to process all potentially relevant information equally would instantly exhaust its bio-economic budget and cease to function (Ep 30).

**Bio-economy and the frame problem**: The frame problem (how does a system decide what to update after any change?) is, in bio-economic terms, the question of how to allocate limited processing resources in a world of unlimited potential relevance. The solution cannot be "process everything" — that violates the bio-economy. The solution must be a mechanism that allocates selectively before exhaustive processing: this is RR. RR is not a workaround for the computational costs of the frame problem; it is the bio-economically necessary form of cognition (Ep 30).

**Efficiency-resiliency within the bio-economy**: The [[Efficiency vs Resiliency]] trade-off can be reframed bio-economically: efficiency means concentrating resources on proven strategies (low overhead, high return on current investment); resiliency means maintaining reserves and structural flexibility (overhead, but protection against environmental shifts). Maximum efficiency in bio-economic terms is minimum overhead — which is also minimum capacity for reorganization. The bio-economy does not have a free optimization point: every choice has costs (Ep 30).

**Attention as bio-economic currency**: In the cognitive domain, attention is the primary bio-economic currency. What we attend to is what we process; what we process shapes what we perceive and do. The bio-economy of attention is why mindfulness traditions focus on attention cultivation — redirecting the bio-economic investment of attention away from parasitic patterns and toward genuine engagement is the fundamental cognitive reallocation (Ep 30).
