# Propositional Knowledge

> The first of the four kinds of knowing: statements that can be true or false — the form of knowledge that logic, science, and argument primarily operate on.

## Vervaeke's framing
Propositional knowledge is knowing-that: asserting something in a form that admits truth-value. "Water is H2O," "The cat is on the mat," "I should lose weight" are all propositional. This is the mode of knowledge that has dominated modern epistemology and that is most salient in scientific inquiry. Vervaeke's argument across the series is not that propositional knowledge is wrong or unimportant but that it is not sufficient — wisdom requires all four kinds of knowing, and the reduction of knowing to the propositional is precisely what has made our culture unable to cultivate wisdom. Propositional knowledge alone can give you truth without relevance (the Natural Philosophers' problem) or can be disconnected from lived engagement (knowing you should lose weight but still eating the cake) (Ep 05).
