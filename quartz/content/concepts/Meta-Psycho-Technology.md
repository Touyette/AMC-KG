# Meta-Psycho-Technology

> A psychotechnology for governing the ecology of psychotechnologies — not a practice that directly cultivates a cognitive capacity, but a practice for evaluating, integrating, and evolving the set of practices one uses; wisdom operating at the level of cultural self-direction.

## Vervaeke's framing
A psychotechnology (literacy, numeracy, meditation, dialectic) extends and reorganizes cognitive capacities. But a set of psychotechnologies is itself an ecology: the practices interact, can conflict or reinforce, can become parasitic, and require ongoing calibration. A meta-psychotechnology is a practice for managing this ecology — for asking which practices to adopt, how to sequence them, how to prevent any single practice from capturing the whole system (Ep 31).

**Why meta-level governance is necessary**: Any individual psychotechnology can become a parasitic processor if overextended — literacy can crowd out embodied knowing; dialectic can foster verbal cleverness over genuine understanding; any practice can be used to shore up the having-mode rather than cultivate being-mode. The wisdom traditions implicitly contain meta-psychotechnology in their structures of training: a teacher who is not just transmitting content but calibrating the student's ecology of practices. Without this governance, a well-intentioned practitioner can assemble a set of practices that conflict or reinforce the very pathologies they aim to treat (Ep 31).

**Wise Cultivation of Enlightenment as meta-psychotechnology**: Vervaeke's concept of [[Wise Cultivation of Enlightenment]] is itself a kind of meta-psychotechnology: the aspiration to integrate cognitive science, contemplative tradition, and philosophical reflection into a self-correcting ecology of practice. It is not a single practice but a practice-of-practices, asking how to design the overall trajectory of development (Ep 31, Ep 50).

**Relation to sapiential tradition**: Traditional wisdom schools — the Platonic Academy, Buddhist monasteries, Sufi tariqas — were institutionalized meta-psychotechnologies: socially embedded structures for calibrating which practices to use at which stages, for which students. The loss of these institutions (the three orders collapsing) is partly a loss of meta-psychotechnological governance at the cultural level. Part of what Vervaeke calls for is the reconstruction of such governance in a secular, scientifically-informed key (Ep 31).
