# Dynamical Systems Theory

> The mathematical and conceptual framework for describing self-organizing, time-evolving systems — here applied by Vervaeke to cognition, character development, and the relationship between agent and world.

## Vervaeke's framing
Vervaeke introduces dynamical systems theory as the contemporary framework that finally resolves Kant's problem about the impossibility of a science of living things. A dynamical system is defined by a feedback cycle (output feeds back into the system as input) regulated by enabling constraints (opening possibilities, like genetic variation) and selective constraints (reducing possibilities, like resource scarcity). The whole — virtual engine — is a self-organizing process that produces directed growth. Crucially, this framework separates causes (events that make things happen) from constraints (conditions that shape what is possible), dissolving the apparent circularity that troubled Kant. Vervaeke uses it as the language for understanding Aristotle's insights: form (idos) is a set of constraints; actuality and potentiality are the two sides of the enabling/selective constraint distinction; and character cultivation is the deliberate shaping of the virtual engine that regulates one's development (Ep 06).
