> The sudden restructuring of a problem frame; involves two stages — breaking an inappropriate gestalt (scaling down) and constructing a better one (scaling up) — and is continuous with awakening when it operates at the level of the whole agent-arena relationship.

## Vervaeke's framing
Using the 9-dot problem and the CAT/HAT example, Vervaeke shows that insight is not a simple eureka but a two-stage process. First, one must break the inappropriate frame — deautomatize one's cognition and decompose the mistaken gestalt (scaling down via transparency-opacity shift). Second, one must find and construct a better frame — reach toward wider patterns and deeper contact with reality (scaling up from features to gestalt). Neither scaling direction alone is sufficient: pure scaling down causes choking; pure scaling up causes fixation in a new inappropriate frame. Insight arises when one can fluently flow between the two through opponent processing. The same structure applies at all levels of scale: individual problem insights, developmental stage-transitions (Piaget's conservation tasks), and full mystical awakening are all instances of the same mechanism operating on increasingly large domains of the salience landscape. Systematic insight — an insight of consciousness rather than in consciousness — is what wisdom is (Ep 09, Ep 10).
