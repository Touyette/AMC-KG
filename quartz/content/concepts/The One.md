# The One

> Plotinus's apex principle: not a being or a thought, but the principle by which everything is integrated and real — like light, which is invisible yet makes everything visible; gnosis of The One is achieved not by knowing it but by being it.

## Vervaeke's framing
Plotinus's key insight is that things are more real as they are more unified and integrated. Understanding something is finding how its parts are unified. The anagogic ascent moves toward deeper integration — the self becomes more integrated as it conforms to more integrated levels of reality. But at the apex of this ascent stands something that cannot be an object of thought, because thought itself requires a subject-object split that is a form of multiplicity. The One is not the number one; it is the principle by which all unity, integration, and intelligibility are possible at all. Just as light is invisible (not a visible thing) yet makes everything visible, The One is not knowable (not an object of knowledge) yet is the condition for all knowing (Ep 18).

The implication is that the highest point of anagoge is not propositional knowing or even mystical perception, but an act of *being* — becoming so integrated and unified that one simply *is* what one is trying to know. "You can only have gnosis of The One by being one." This makes Plotinus's system the fullest integration of spirituality, theory of reality, theory of knowing, and psychotherapy in the ancient world: there is no division between them at all. In this he directly influenced Augustine, the medieval Christian tradition, Corbin's Islamic mysticism, and Barfield's Romantic vision (Ep 18, extended through Ep 50).
