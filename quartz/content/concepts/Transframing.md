# Transframing

> A competence-level shift in how one frames reality — transforming the whole machinery of framing (both agent and arena), not merely reframing particular content within an existing framework.

## Vervaeke's framing
Ordinary reframing changes the content held within a frame: I see the nine-dot problem differently. Transframing changes the framing machinery itself: I undergo a systematic, developmental shift that alters how I see a whole class of situations. The mother-in-law in Murdoch's example does not merely change her opinion of the daughter-in-law; she undergoes a transframing that simultaneously changes who she is and what the daughter-in-law can be to her. The salience landscape, the agent-arena relationship, and the machinery of co-identification are all transformed together (Ep 17).

Transframing is the successful outcome of sensibility transcendence, and it is what Gnosis aims to bring about. It is structurally analogous to a developmental stage change in cognitive development: not just solving a new problem but finding the nexus of errors that enables a systematic jump to a new level of functioning. The key criterion: transframing changes not what one thinks but how one is capable of thinking, perceiving, and participating in a world.
