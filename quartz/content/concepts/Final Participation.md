# Final Participation

> Barfield: the recovery of original participation — the pre-Cartesian transjective resonance of agent and world — integrated with the rational sciences; the response to the meaning crisis.

## Vervaeke's framing
Barfield's narrative in *The Rediscovery of Meaning* maps three stages (Ep 50):

1. **Original participation**: The pre-Cartesian, pre-Axial (or proto-Axial) mode of being in which inner and outer interpenetrated without sharp division. The dual meanings of words (pneuma = wind AND spirit; see = visual perception AND understanding) are traces of this mode. There was a more transjective anagogic resonance with reality — the wind was imaginal for these people, disclosing the self-moving aspects of reality in a highly resonant fashion.

2. **The division**: The Cartesian revolution and the meaning crisis it produces — the sharp severing of subjective and objective, inner and outer, mind and world. The two-worlds mythology, then scientific rationalism, then the loss of the imaginal.

3. **Final participation**: The recovery of original participation integrated *within the gains of the rational sciences*. Not nostalgia. Not a rejection of the scientific mind. Both sides must be involved in this integration, or it will fail. Final participation specifically requires: (a) recovery of the perspectival and participatory dimensions of knowing; AND (b) a science of meaning cultivation — how participatory knowing fits into our scientific processes of being (Ep 50).

**Vervaeke's criticism of Barfield**: Barfield does not do (b). He calls for the integration but does not provide the science of meaning cultivation that makes the integration possible from the scientific side. Vervaeke's relevance realization theory — worked out across the AMC series and in ongoing research — is his contribution to (b): the theoretical machinery that can bring participatory knowing into discourse with the sciences (Ep 50).

**Criticism of Barfield's followers**: You cannot simply import Barfield into classical theism and say he is talking about what Christian theology has always been talking about. This is not fair to Barfield's argument, which requires the transgression of onto-theology that Tillich and Heidegger perform. Final participation without the critique of onto-theology is not final participation.

**Poiesis as the mode of final participation**: Final participation is enacted through poiesis — the synergistic creativity that neither passively receives nor actively imposes, but participates in the ongoing disclosure of the inexhaustible. Gelassenheit (Heidegger's letting-be) is necessary but not sufficient; Durchbruch (breakthrough, attentional scaling, making the new frame) is equally required, and Barfield holds both together (Ep 50).
