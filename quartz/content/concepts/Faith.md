# Faith

> Tillich: not the willful assertion of propositions but the state of being grasped by what concerns one ultimately — participatory, perspectival, aspirational, and directed at the inexhaustible ground of being.

## Vervaeke's framing
Tillich explicitly distances his concept of faith from the credal sense — assenting to propositions one cannot fully justify. Instead, faith is *ultimate concern*: that which concerns you ultimately in a way that encompasses you even as you are involved in and through it. When you are concerned about something, you care about it, cope with it, are committed to it. Ultimate concern has this structure at the deepest possible level of participation and aspiration (Ep 50).

This circles back, Vervaeke notes, to the Hebrew *da'at*: the participatory knowing of God as a course of being. Tillich's faith = da'at understood as aspirational process. It is open-ended, pointing toward the inexhaustibleness of the ground of being — it cannot be had or concluded (Ep 50).

**Faith and Dasein**: Tillich directs his notion at Heidegger's Dasein — the being whose being is in question. By our perspectival and participatory knowing of our own being, we come into deeper contact with Being itself. But this requires overcoming forgetfulness: aletheia, ontological wonder, turning toward the ground of meaning and being rather than clinging to the readily available (Ep 50).

**God as icon vs idol**: God, for Tillich, is an *icon* — an imaginal symbol for the ground of being — not an *idol* (a reified object to have, possess, control, manipulate). Idolatry is using the machinery appropriate for ultimate concern on something that is not ultimate. God as imaginal symbol means God is no kind of thing. There is a *no-thingness* to God: any attempt to reify God — to think of God as a kind of supreme being — is, for Tillich, a form of idolatry (Ep 50).

**No-thingness of God vs nothingness of despair**: Tillich's central move: the no-thingness of God (inexhaustible ground, fount of meaning cultivation) takes into itself the nothingness of meaninglessness/despair and overcomes it. This is not a logical inference but an *aspect-identity shift* — the same structural shift as the Necker cube but at the level of fundamental framing and its coupling to being. Nishitani makes the same argument from Buddhism: when one participates the no-thingness of shunyata, the nothingness of nihilism is reframed as pointing to an inexhaustible source of meaning that cannot be drained by despair (Ep 50).

**Theonomy**: Tillich resolves the paradox of aspiration (same as Strawson's paradox) at the level of theology. The puzzle: aspiration cannot be something imposed from outside (heteronomy — demonic imposition of authority), nor can it be something purely self-generated (autonomy — the Enlightenment ideal). Theonomy is Tillich's resolution: God-ordered, but God = the ground of being, the ongoing epektasis of the inexhaustible. This is neither passive reception nor autonomous making; it is participation in the ongoing disclosure of the ground of being — which is synergistic, like da'at (Ep 50).
