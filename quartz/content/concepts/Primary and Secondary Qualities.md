# Primary and Secondary Qualities

> Galileo: mathematical/quantitative properties (length, mass, speed) are objective — really in the world; everything else (color, beauty, meaning, purpose, value) is subjective — produced only inside the mind; this drains the world of all meaning and traps it inside the head.

## Vervaeke's framing
Galileo uses geometry in a completely abstract fashion — a triangle can represent the relationship between distance, time, and speed with no experiential resemblance between the diagram and the reality. He shows that abstract mathematics is the only language that reaches the world as it really is. The implication: only mathematical properties are genuinely in the object, in the world — these are **primary qualities** (length, mass, velocity, geometric shape). Everything that cannot be captured mathematically — sweetness, color, beauty, meaning, purpose, the sense that the world is ordered toward something good — is not in the world. It is produced inside the mind in response to the object's mathematical properties; it is **secondary**, an illusion created by the subjective meaning-making machinery (Ep 20).

This is one of the most devastating moves in Vervaeke's genealogy of the meaning crisis. Everything the Augustinian synthesis had placed in the world — significance (the normative order: things are genuinely more or less good), purpose (the narrative order: history moves toward consummation), coherence (the nomological order: reality is rationally ordered) — turns out to be subjective, only inside the head. The interior chamber of the intensive self (established by the reading-practice shift) and Nominalism (no rational order in the world) now receives this final confirmation: the world is emptied; all meaning, beauty, and value are locked inside the mind. The universe is a purposeless machine; we are alone inside our skulls (Ep 20).
