# Sophia

> Aristotle: theoretical wisdom — cross-contextual principle perception; the capacity to see structural invariants across diverse situations (ontological depth perception); not rule-possession but perception of the principled intelligibility that transcends any particular case.

## Vervaeke's framing
Sophia and phronesis form an opponent pair, and wisdom requires both. Where phronesis is contextual sensitivity (the skillful, situationally responsive dimension of wisdom — knowing *how* to apply principles here and now), sophia is the ability to perceive cross-contextual principles — not as explicit propositions or rules to be applied but as a kind of structural vision: seeing what remains invariant across different cases and situations, perceiving the principled shape of what is good or real (Ep 43).

**Not rule-following**: The key distinction Vervaeke draws: sophia is *not* the possession of rules to be applied algorithmically. Rules always underdetermine their own application (Wittgenstein's point about rule-following). Sophia is more like ontological depth perception — a perceptual skill that allows one to *see* the structural invariants, the deep patterns, the cross-contextual principles that hold across otherwise different situations. It is more like a trained way of seeing than a database of propositions (Ep 43).

**The opponent relationship with phronesis**: Good wisdom requires both to check and balance each other:
- Sophia → phronesis: principles must be put into situational practice; cross-contextual vision must inform contextual sensitivity
- Phronesis → sophia: situated practice must be principled; contextual skill must not degenerate into unprincipled cleverness

Neither alone suffices: pure sophia without phronesis produces rigid, decontextualized moral theory; pure phronesis without sophia produces sophisticated situational maneuvering untethered from principled reality. Their opponent dynamic ensures wisdom tracks both the depth of principle and the grain of context (Ep 43).

**Connection to RR**: Sophia is a form of relevance realization at the meta-level — not just homing in on what is relevant within a situation, but perceiving what is *principally* relevant *across* situations. This is why Vervaeke links it to ontological depth perception: sophia perceives the depth dimension of situations (what really matters here, structurally, generally) not merely their surface features (Ep 43).
