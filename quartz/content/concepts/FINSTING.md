# FINSTING

> Pylyshyn's term (FINding inSTances in experience): the mind's pre-categorical capacity to track individual objects as "this one here now" across time and space, before any categorization or representation occurs; evidence that relevance realization operates sub-semantically at the level of participatory knowing.

## Vervaeke's framing
Zenon Pylyshyn's FINSTING research investigated how minds individuate and track objects prior to any categorization. The finding: there is a level of cognitive operation — primitive object tracking — that precedes representation and categorization but enables both. The mind can "find instances" (keep track of "that one, there, now") without first categorizing what the instance is. This "hereness and nowness" tracking is pre-semantic: it operates before content is assigned (Ep 29).

**Why this matters for relevance realization**: Vervaeke uses FINSTING as empirical support for the claim that relevance realization operates at a level more fundamental than propositional representation. If the mind can track what is relevant before it has categorized it, then relevance is not derived from representations — relevance precedes and enables representation. FINSTING shows the cognitive machinery working at the participatory level before the propositional level (Ep 29).

**Connection to enactive demonstrative reference**: FINSTING is the empirical foundation for what Vervaeke calls [[Enactive Demonstrative Reference]]: the enacted, non-linguistic salience-tagging of a "this" in the environment. Before you can say "the apple is red," you have to be able to pick out the apple as "this one" — and FINSTING names the cognitive operation that does this prior to any categorization (Ep 29).

**FINSTING and the hierarchy of knowing**: The discovery that FINSTING operates pre-categorically strengthens the argument for the hierarchical dependency of the four kinds of knowing: propositional knowing depends on procedural knowing (how to identify things), which depends on perspectival knowing (a point of view), which depends on participatory knowing (being already-engaged with what shows up as "this one here"). FINSTING is a window into participatory knowing's priority (Ep 29).

**The limit of computation for RR**: FINSTING also contributes to Vervaeke's argument that relevance realization cannot be achieved by computation over representations. Computation needs its inputs already specified — but FINSTING shows that the specification of "what this is" (the input to computation) itself presupposes a more primitive relevance operation. RR cannot be explained by computation because computation presupposes it (Ep 29).
