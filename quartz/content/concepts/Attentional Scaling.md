# Attentional Scaling

> Vervaeke's framework for the two-dimensional structure of attention: the transparency-opacity axis (from/to, indwelling vs. looking-at) and the feature-gestalt axis (scaling down vs. scaling up); mindfulness trains fluency in flowing between these.

## Vervaeke's framing
Drawing on Polanyi's from-to structure of awareness, Vervaeke establishes that attention is not a spotlight but a complex optimization process with at least two axes. The transparency-opacity axis captures how attention can be directed through subsidiary processes (transparent probe, transparent glasses) into a focal object, or can step back to make those processes themselves focal and opaque. The feature-gestalt axis captures how attention can break a whole into its constituent features (scaling down) or integrate features into a wider-pattern whole (scaling up). In practice these axes are deeply coupled: scaling up often goes with looking more deeply into reality; scaling down often goes with stepping back into the mind. Mindfulness training (Vipassana for scaling down, Metta/contemplation for scaling up, Tai Chi for flowing between) is training these two capacities and their integration. The opponent processing of scaling down and scaling up is what produces genuine insight: you need to break the bad frame and construct a better one (Ep 09).
