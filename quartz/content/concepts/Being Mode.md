# Being Mode

> Fromm's term for the existential mode organized around becoming — meeting developmental needs through growth, love, and expressive encounter; characterized by I-Thou relations.

## Vervaeke's framing
In the being mode, needs are met not by acquiring or possessing but by becoming — becoming mature, virtuous, loving, wise. One relates to things and people expressively rather than categorically: not as instances of a type to be controlled but as inexhaustibly individual, to be encountered. This is what Buber calls I-Thou relations. The example Vervaeke uses is love: genuine love cannot be conducted from the having mode ("I'm with you because you remind me of other women and I could easily replace you"). Love requires relating to the beloved as an irreplaceable particular, as a "thou" rather than an "it," and it involves reciprocal realization — each party is transformed by the relationship. Sati (mindfulness as the remembering of the being mode) is precisely the recovery of this mode when the having mode has produced existential crisis. The being mode is what the mendicant in Siddhartha's story embodies (Ep 07, Ep 08).
