# Higher States of Consciousness

> States beyond ordinary waking consciousness that Vervaeke argues are continuous with the machinery of relevance realization — not supernatural interruptions but systematic transformations of the salience, presence, depth, and significance landscapes.

## Vervaeke's framing
Higher states of consciousness are distinguished from ordinary altered states (dreaming, intoxication) by their ontonormativity: one returns from them convinced that "that was more real and this is less real," which demands and motivates radical life transformation. Survey data (Newberg's online survey of 1500 people; Vervaeke's own lab work) shows 30-40% of the population has experienced states of this kind, cutting across language, culture, socioeconomic status, and gender. Vervaeke ties them explicitly to the relevance realization machinery: a higher state of consciousness is not an insight in consciousness but an insight of consciousness — a systematic transformation of the whole way salience, presence, depth, and significance are organized. This is why they do not provide new propositional content (they are typically ineffable and trans-rational) but do provide wisdom: they transform the machinery of salience landscaping rather than adding new beliefs. The same machinery that produces flow states and individual insights, when pushed to its optimal integration through practices like mindfulness, produces higher states of consciousness (Ep 09, Ep 10).

Ep 11 gives the detailed phenomenological and cognitive-scientific account. Three dimensions of HSC experience: (1) **World side** — clarity, brightness/shining, comprehensiveness plus finite detail ("to see the world in a grain of sand"), beauty, aliveness, oneness; (2) **Self side** — profound peace (Platonic inner harmony), joy (deep connection to the good), dissolution of the egocentric autobiographical self, remembering the true self, energy and vitality, sense of profound insight; (3) **Relationship** — deep at-onement, participatory identification, ineffability. These features are explained by the **Continuity Hypothesis**: fluency gets enhanced in insight, insight in flow, flow can be exapted into mystical experience. Disruptive strategies (meditation, fasting, psychedelics, incubation) trigger [[Deautomatization]], increasing variation so the system detects bad invariants (fixated framings causing developmental arrest) and good invariants (real patterns). Yaden et al. (2017), 701 participants: 69% reported ontonormativity, predictive of improvement across health, family, purpose, and reduced death anxiety. The prescriptive claim: because HSC is the same cognitive machinery operating at greater depth, the transformations it authorizes are rationally defensible — these are not arbitrary conversions but improved contacts with reality (Ep 11).
