# Affordances

> Gibson's ecological psychology term: the action-possibilities a particular organism perceives in a particular environment — what the environment "offers" relative to that organism's capacities; Vervaeke uses affordances to ground the claim that relevance is not subjective projection but a real feature of organism-environment relations.

## Vervaeke's framing
J.J. Gibson introduced affordances in ecological psychology: a chair affords sitting, a cliff affords falling, a handle affords grasping — but only for organisms with the relevant capacities. Affordances are relational: they are neither purely "in" the environment (independent of the organism) nor purely "in" the mind (projected onto a neutral environment). They are properties of the organism-environment system (Ep 26).

**Affordances and relevance realization**: Affordances are the ecological form of relevance. When an organism perceives affordances, it is registering what matters relative to its action capacities — which is relevance in the most direct sense. Relevance realization, then, is not an arbitrary cognitive projection onto a neutral world but the detection and registration of real affordance relations. The world is not meaningless with meaning added by minds; it is structured by real action-possibilities that organisms with appropriate capacities can detect (Ep 26).

**Affordances and enactivism**: Varela's enactivism and Gibson's affordance theory converge: both argue that what organisms perceive is action-relevant structure, not a neutral sensory manifold that cognitive systems then interpret. The organism enacts its affordance landscape; the affordance landscape provides the organism with its real cognitive domain. Together, these positions ground Vervaeke's claim that participatory knowing has genuine cognitive contact with reality (Ep 26, Ep 38).

**Affordances and the salience landscape**: A well-calibrated RR system produces a salience landscape that accurately tracks affordances relative to the organism's current situation and goals. A poorly calibrated one (parasitic processing, self-deception, meaning crisis) produces a salience landscape that misregisters affordances — either failing to perceive real action-possibilities or "perceiving" pseudo-affordances that lead nowhere. Wisdom partly consists in cultivating the perceptual sensitivity to real affordances (Ep 26).

**Affordances and the meaning crisis**: If affordances are real relational properties, then the sense of meaning-hunger in the meaning crisis is not a misfiring of a faculty that "should" be satisfied by neutral facts, but an accurate perception that something has gone wrong in the organism-environment coupling: the affordance landscape is genuinely impoverished, or the RR machinery is genuinely misaligned with it (Ep 26).
