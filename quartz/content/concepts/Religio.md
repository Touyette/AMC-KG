# Religio

> Vervaeke's recovered Latin term for what RR is in its full spiritual significance: the pre-egoic, pre-normative binding that simultaneously grounds the self and its world — derived from 'religare' (to bind together); distinguished from 'religio' understood as credal religion.

## Vervaeke's framing
Vervaeke introduces Religio (Ep 33) as a term to name the entire right-hand side of the convergent RR diagram — everything that emerges from, and depends on, the naturalistic machinery of relevance realization as it bears on human spirituality. He chooses the Latin term deliberately to invoke the etymology of 'religio': *religare*, to bind together, to connect. Religio is the primordial connectedness — to oneself, to the world, to others — that is constituted by the transjective, embodied-embedded machinery of RR.

The defining feature is its **primordiality**: Religio is pre-conceptual (you need RR in order to categorize, so RR precedes concepts), pre-propositional (belief presupposes RR), pre-inferential (inference presupposes it), pre-egoic (the ego co-emerges from the agent-arena relationship that RR produces), and pre-normative (the capacity to judge truth, goodness, and beauty requires that things matter to you first, which is what RR establishes). This doesn't mean RR is inaccessible — it can be theorized, and higher-order processes feed back into it. But in terms of what makes everything else possible, Religio is the ground (Ep 33).

This is why the machinery of Religio is experienced as spiritual: it is deeper than our ego, our propositional beliefs, our conceptual categories. As Vervaeke puts it, "the way that can be spoken of is not the way" (echoing the Tao Te Ching): not because it is literally unspeakable, but because it cannot be *exemplified* in propositional, subject-object language — one can only point. Wonder, awe, mystery, and the experience of sacredness are the modes through which humans become perspectivally and participatorily aware of Religio (Ep 33).

Vervaeke explicitly contrasts this with Credo — religion understood as the assent to propositions — which has historically displaced Religio in the Western tradition (Ep 39).
