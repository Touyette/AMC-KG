# Synoptic Integration

> Vervaeke's definition of cognitive science as the creation of plausible and potentially profound theoretical constructs that bridge — without reducing — across the multiple levels and disciplines studying mind, addressing equivocation, fragmentation, and ignorance of causal relations between levels.

## Vervaeke's framing
Synoptic Integration is the third and best vision of cognitive science, distinguished from Generic Nominalism (just a label for related disciplines) and Interdisciplinary Eclecticism (polite borrowing). It requires creating constructs that are multi-apt (bridging many domains insightfully) and trustworthy (produced through convergent, independent lines of investigation). The goal is not to say all disciplines say the same thing, nor to let them remain isolated. It is to build something between them — a bridging vocabulary — that captures the causal interaction between levels of reality (brain, information processing, behaviour, language, culture) and avoids the equivocation that fragments our self-understanding. Vervaeke proposes Relevance Realization as the exemplary synoptic construct: it bridges cognitive science, wisdom research, consciousness studies, and spiritual practice in a plausible, non-reductive way (Ep 26).
