# Mind-Body Problem

> The apparently irresolvable difficulty, crystallised by Descartes, of explaining how mind and body causally interact given that they share no properties.

## Vervaeke's framing
The mind-body problem arises directly from Descartes's successful argument against Hobbes. In order to show that a purely material system cannot possess reasoning, consciousness, or normativity, Descartes establishes that mind and matter share no fundamental properties: mind has meaning, purpose, normativity, and qualia; matter is purposeless, meaningless, and normatively inert. This gap serves the argument against strong AI, but it makes the interaction between mind and body deeply paradoxical — mind moves matter (intentional action) and matter causes mind (pain from physical impact), yet on Descartes's own account neither should be possible (Ep 22). The problem also extends to the "problem of other minds": if the only mind my mind touches is itself, how do I know other people are not mindless automatons? Vervaeke sees this as one of the deepest drivers of the meaning crisis — the systematic disconnection of the self from its body, from other minds, and from reality.
