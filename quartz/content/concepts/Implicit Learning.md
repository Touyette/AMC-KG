# Implicit Learning

> The capacity to pick up complex patterns in the environment without conscious awareness of the rules being learned — the cognitive basis of intuition.

## Vervaeke's framing

Reber's letter-string experiments showed that people reliably identify novel strings that follow the same arbitrary grammar as training strings, yet cannot articulate the rules. Vervaeke uses this to argue that *intuition is real but not magical*: it is the product of implicit learning, which picks up causal (not merely correlational) patterns when the learning context provides clear feedback, tight coupling, and error-that-matters — exactly the conditions of the flow state. The shaman operating in a real ecological environment is therefore not merely having an exotic experience; they are training their implicit learning machinery to track genuine causal structures in the world. Hogarth calls this "educating intuition" — and Vervaeke argues that the shaman's practices constitute the earliest systematic attempt to do so. The flip side: implicit learning also generates bias when it tracks correlational rather than causal patterns (Ep 02).
