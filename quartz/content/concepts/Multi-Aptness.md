# Multi-Aptness

> The property of a theoretical construct (or solution) that bridges insightfully across multiple domains simultaneously — the cognitive science analog of elegance, and a marker of genuine insight and good problem formulation.

## Vervaeke's framing
A construct is multi-apt when it gets the right balance between identity and difference across multiple domains: like a good metaphor, it lets you "step back" through the difference into an identity that illuminates something new in each domain it bridges. Multi-aptness is the forward-facing (usage) dimension of Plausibility: the more domains a construct can bridge insightfully without becoming a trivial generalisation, the more it is multi-apt. Vervaeke distinguishes this from mere simplicity (the naive notion of elegance) by tying it to insight-generation across disciplines. Relevance Realization is multi-apt because it explains problem-solving, categorisation, communication, consciousness, wisdom, and spiritual experience from a single theoretical framework (Ep 26). A solution to a problem is also multi-apt when it works under multiple framings simultaneously — this is a marker of good insight.
