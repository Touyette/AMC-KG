# Continuous Cosmos

> The pre-Axial worldview in which the sacred, social, and natural worlds form an unbroken, power-saturated continuum.

## Vervaeke's framing
In the Continuous Cosmos, distinctions between human, divine, and natural realms are differences of degree (in power and glory) rather than differences in kind. The cosmos runs in great cycles — mirroring seasons and day-night — and ritual behavior aims to tap into the creative power circulating through those cycles. Wisdom here means knowing how to fit into and draw upon these power structures, roughly equivalent to what we now call prudence. Time is cyclical, the future is not open, and the self is defined by how well it harmonizes with the surrounding whole rather than by its capacity for self-transcendence. Vervaeke borrows the term from Charles Taylor (Ep 03).
