# Participatory Knowledge

> The deepest of the four kinds of knowing: knowing through identity and mutual transformation — where knower and known are genuinely changed by the knowing relation rather than remaining independent.

## Vervaeke's framing
Participatory knowledge is knowing at the level of being, not just at the level of representation or skill. It is modeled on relationships of deep intimacy and mutual constitution: the Hebrew Da'ath (knowing by being immersed and changed), the Buddhist non-dual state, the Platonic anagoge, the Aristotelian conformity theory. You do not stand apart from what you participate in: you take on its structural-functional organization, you conform to it, and it is transformed by your engagement with it. This is what Plato's Cave is about: the prisoner does not just acquire new beliefs about the sun; they are fundamentally changed by the encounter with it, and only the changed self can see it. Participatory knowledge is what was lost when modern epistemology reduced knowing to propositional knowledge — a loss Vervaeke sees as central to the meaning crisis. It is the mode of knowing that makes wisdom, transformation, and genuine meaning possible (Ep 05).
