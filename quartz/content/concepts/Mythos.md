# Mythos

> Vervaeke's term for the enmeshment of symbols, story, and ritual that constitutes the psycho-technology of Sacredness; explicitly contrasted with "myth" (which connotes merely fictional narrative) — Mythos is the structured practice-system that activates and celebrates Religio.

## Vervaeke's framing
Vervaeke introduces Mythos (Ep 35) as a technically precise term that he distinguishes sharply from "myth" as commonly used (fictional or false story). Mythos is three things enmeshed together:

1. **Symbols** — in the full EPIC sense (Ek-static, Participatory, Integrative-anagogic, Complex); they activate exaptive machinery and afford symbolic resonance
2. **Story** — narrative structure is necessary to bring in perspectival and participatory knowing; story makes the symbol available for appropriation from within a first-person perspective, and makes it *enactable*
3. **Ritual** — not neurotic routine, but **enactive analogy** / **enactive Anagoge**: the processes by which the machinery of transformation is actively put in motion; ritual enacts the story and embeds the symbol in practice

The three are mutually constituting: symbols need story to be inhabited perspectivally; story needs ritual to be more than abstract narrative; ritual needs symbol to engage the deep machinery of RR. Mythos is the whole unit.

**Mythos about Religio = Sacredness.** When a Mythos targets Religio itself — activating, articulating, accentuating, and celebrating the primordial RR-binding machinery — this constitutes sacredness in its full sense. The reason Mythos is the appropriate vehicle for Religio (rather than, say, propositional theology) is that Religio is pre-egoic, pre-normative, pre-propositional, and primordially participatory: it can only be related to symbolically, not described propositionally from the outside. Additionally, because RR is constitutively set up to find itself interesting (it would not be self-organizing and self-correcting if it were not), activating Religio through Mythos is doubly meaningful: it is inherently valuable (Religio is foundational to all agency and world-disclosure) and inherently interesting (RR is interested in itself as a process) (Ep 35).

Mythos can of course be used for other patterns of meaning besides Religio — but it is the canonical form for sacred practice precisely because of this alignment. This gives Vervaeke a non-supernatural account of why religious practice (ritual, myth, symbol) has the power and importance it has: not because it refers to supernatural realities, but because it seriously engages the primordial machinery of Religio through EPIC-structured symbolic vehicles.
