# Two Worlds Mythology

> The post-Axial mythological structure that posits a sharp divide between a lower, illusory everyday world and a higher, real world accessible through wisdom and self-transformation.

## Vervaeke's framing
Two Worlds Mythology is not a literal claim about ontology but a mythological framework — in Vervaeke's sense of a symbolic story about perennial patterns — that makes possible the Axial project of self-transcendence. The everyday world is characterized by self-deception, inner conflict, and disconnection from reality; the real world is what the wise, trained mind perceives. The movement from the illusory to the real is not spatial but transformative: it requires restructuring the self. Different Axial traditions interpret this movement differently — the Hebrews frame it as historical progress toward a promised future, Plato frames it as rational anagoge, the Buddha frames it as awakening from modal confusion. The two-worlds structure is now being eroded by the scientific worldview's return to a "one-world" continuous cosmos (Ep 03).
