# Three Orders

> Vervaeke's framework identifying three interlocking orders — nomological (natural), normative (moral), and narrative (story) — whose consonance makes a meaningful worldview and whose fragmentation constitutes the meaning crisis.

## Vervaeke's framing
Vervaeke proposes that a functioning worldview requires three orders to be mutually reinforcing. The Nomological Order is the natural order: how the cosmos is scientifically understood as a law-governed, intelligible structure that meshes with how we know things (the conformity theory). The Normative Order is the moral order: the standards by which actions are judged better or worse, the framework of values that guides character development. The Narrative Order is the story order: the sense that one's life and history have direction, purpose, and meaning in the form of a story moving toward resolution. In Aristotle's era these three orders were deeply consonant: the geocentric cosmos (nomological) grounded natural motion toward proper places, making moral growth (normative) natural and making human history intelligible as a narrative of actualization. The meaning crisis is, at root, the progressive fragmentation of these three orders — first the nomological order was destroyed by Copernicus, Galileo, and Newton; then the normative order lost its grounding; and finally the narrative order collapsed too (Ep 07).

In Ep 19, Vervaeke shows that Augustine achieved the fullest integration of all three orders. The Nomological Order is Aristotelian science (how the cosmos is intelligibly structured); the Normative Order is Neoplatonic anagoge (ascending from evil-as-chaos to good-as-integration); the Narrative Order is the Christian story of history moving under Agape toward consummation. These correspond exactly to the three dimensions of meaning that modern psychology identifies: coherence (nomological), significance (normative), purpose (narrative). Augustine's synthesis is so powerful precisely because it satisfies all three simultaneously in a mutually supporting way. Aquinas begins their separation by positing the natural/supernatural distinction: science/reason address the nomological order; faith/will address the rest. This structural divorce sets up the eventual collapse of the synthesis (Ep 19).
