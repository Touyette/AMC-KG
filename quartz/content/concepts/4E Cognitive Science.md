# 4E Cognitive Science

> The family of approaches (Embodied, Enactive, Extended, Emotional/Ecological) that replace the Cartesian computational model of mind with accounts grounding cognition in organism-environment coupling, body dynamics, and situational action — Vervaeke uses this framework to naturalize participatory knowing and to ground meaning in cognitive science.

## Vervaeke's framing
4E Cognitive Science is Vervaeke's umbrella term for a cluster of research programs that have in common the rejection of the Cartesian picture of mind as an inner representational theater, and the replacement of that picture with accounts of cognition as essentially embodied, enactive, extended, and emotionally/ecologically embedded (Ep 38).

**The four Es**:
1. **[[Embodied Cognition]]**: The body is not merely the vehicle of the mind but constitutive of it. Cognitive processes depend on body morphology, motor systems, and proprioception — not just brain computation.
2. **[[Enactive Cognition]]**: Cognition is the active bringing-forth of a world of significance through sensorimotor coupling, not the passive processing of pre-given representations. The organism enacts its cognitive domain.
3. **[[Extended Cognition]]**: Cognitive processes can and do extend beyond the skull into tools, environments, and other agents. The parity principle (Clark and Chalmers): if an external process plays the cognitive role an internal process would, it counts as cognition.
4. **Emotional/Ecological**: Affect and emotional valence are not post-cognitive additions but co-constitutive of cognitive processing; ecological embedding (organism-niche coupling) is the natural home of cognition.

**Why this matters for Vervaeke's argument**: The 4E framework is Vervaeke's scientific case for the reality of [[Participatory Knowledge]]. If cognition is embodied, enactive, extended, and emotional, then the Cartesian grammar — mind as inner propositional representation space — is wrong as cognitive science, not just as phenomenology. This grounds the argument that propositional knowledge is not the fundamental form of knowing, and that participatory and perspectival knowing are not merely subjective add-ons (Ep 38).

**4E and the meaning crisis**: The Cartesian grammar, taken seriously, generates the meaning crisis: if mind is inner representation and world is outer mechanism, then meaning (the felt resonance of participation) has no place — it is either projected onto a meaningless world or reduced to subjective states with no grip on reality. The 4E correction removes the philosophical underpinning of this alienation (Ep 38).

**Francisco Varela's contribution**: Varela (with Maturana, Thompson, and Rosch) is the key figure for enactivism within the 4E framework. His synthesis of autopoiesis and enaction provides the biological grounding for why cognition is organism-constitutive rather than computation-over-representations (Ep 38).
