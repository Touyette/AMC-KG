# Truth as Aletheia

> Heidegger: truth not as correctness/correspondence (a statement matching reality) but as the prior uncovering/disclosure in which things show themselves as simultaneously shining into experience and withdrawing beyond any frame; the ground of propositional truth.

## Vervaeke's framing
The Greek word *lethe* means to cover or forget — in Greek mythology, the River Lethe in the underworld made souls forget their previous life. *Aletheia* (adding the negation *a-*) means uncovering, disclosure, un-forgetting. Heidegger uses this etymology to make a philosophical claim: truth, properly understood, is not about propositions matching facts. It is about the prior *disclosure* that makes any statement-to-fact matching possible in the first place (Ep 47).

Vervaeke develops aletheia's two-sided structure:
1. **Attunement side (relevance)**: The attuning relationship with being discloses things as meaningful, as calling for response, as salient. This is the side that maps onto relevance realization.
2. **Independence of Being side (moreness/inexhaustibleness)**: Things simultaneously *withdraw* beyond any frame we apply — they are always more than how they show up for us. This withdrawal is the "thing beyond itself" (Harman). The moreness is not in our experience as an object, but it contributes to the *realness* of experience and must function as an ongoing constraint on our relevance (not just acknowledged at the limit).

Together, aletheia is the attuning to mutual disclosure: things are simultaneously shining into subjectivity and withdrawing from framing. Being in contact with this — not forgetting it — is what it means to be in the truth (aletheia mode), as opposed to just having correct propositions (Ep 47).

**Aletheia as existential remembering**: Vervaeke connects aletheia to sati (Buddhist mindfulness/remembering) but at the existential-ontological level. It is a deep remembering — not autobiographical memory but the modally appropriate recall of the being mode, the openness to the mystery of being. This is what Heidegger's quasi-Socratic texts are trying to trigger: a phenomenological remembering, not a theoretical argument (Ep 47).

**Forgetfulness as the opposite**: When attunement is forgotten — when one slides into the having mode, treating beings as manipulable objects — aletheia gives way to mere correctness. The propositional level of truth is still possible (statements can still be correct), but it floats without its ground. This is the *forgetfulness of Being*: not a failure to know facts but a failure to maintain the attuning, disclosing relationship with being that makes knowing meaningful (Ep 47).
