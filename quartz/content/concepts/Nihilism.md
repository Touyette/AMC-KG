# Nihilism

> Nietzsche/Heidegger: the condition resulting from the collapse of the framework that once gave things worth, meaning, and value — "nothing matters" not as a logical claim but as an existential condition in which the machinery of meaning-making has broken down.

## Vervaeke's framing
Nihilism is the terminus of the meaning crisis's historical trajectory. Nietzsche diagnoses it in *The Will to Power* and elsewhere: with the "death of God" — the collapse of the onto-theological framework that grounded value, truth, and purpose — the possibility of a shared, authoritative sense of meaning disappears. This is not primarily a philosophical conclusion one argues to; it is an existential condition one finds oneself in (Ep 23).

**Nietzsche's response and its failure**: Nietzsche recognizes the problem and attempts to respond with the will to power and eternal recurrence. But his response overcompensates — like Descartes' complete subjective activity, Nietzsche's response is, for Vervaeke, ultimately an over-assertion of the subjective will that cannot recover the genuine relationship to Being. He saw the abyss but did not look long enough into it (Ep 23, Ep 50).

**Heidegger's deepening**: Heidegger traces nihilism to onto-theology: if the metaphysical tradition has confused Being with a supreme being (God), then when that supreme being is undermined, it appears as if Being itself has disappeared. Nihilism is therefore not an accident but the inevitable result of the onto-theological enterprise: having grounded everything in a supreme being (rather than in Being itself), the collapse of that being takes everything down with it. The history of metaphysics IS the history of nihilism in slow motion — which is why [[Forgetfulness of Being]] is at root a nihilistic tendency (Ep 47).

**The response**: Nihilism cannot be answered propositionally (arguing that things do matter). It requires recovering the being mode of engagement — the participatory, perspectival contact with the depths of Being that the having mode of nihilism has foreclosed. The prophets of the meaning crisis (Heidegger, Corbin, Jung, Tillich, Barfield) each propose versions of this recovery. Tillich's specific answer: the no-thingness of God (ground of being) takes into itself and overcomes the nothingness of nihilistic despair through an aspect-identity shift (Ep 50).
