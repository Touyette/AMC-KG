# Worldview Attunement

> The ongoing mutual reinforcement between a worldview's account of how things are and how they are known, such that existential modes (agent-arena relationships) arise naturally from the intellectual framework.

## Vervaeke's framing
Citing Clifford Geertz, Vervaeke uses worldview attunement to describe the dynamic process by which a worldview does not merely describe the world but generates a felt sense of belonging, fitting, and acting meaningfully within it. In Aristotle's case, the conformity theory of knowing and the geocentric cosmos of natural motion mutually support each other: knowing as conforming to real patterns in things makes it plausible that the patterns in the world are the same as those in the mind, and the cosmos organized by purposeful natural motion confirms that the mind's patterns are indeed tracking the real order. This attunement generates existential modes — ways of being in the world as a particular kind of agent — naturally and reliably. The breakdown of worldview attunement (as the scientific revolution destroyed the Aristotelian cosmos without providing an existentially livable replacement) is a central driver of the meaning crisis (Ep 07).
