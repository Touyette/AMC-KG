# Heuristics

> Fast, frugal, ecologically rational cognitive shortcuts that allow navigation of combinatorially explosive environments; not biases or errors but the necessary operating mode of any finite mind — rationality *is* good heuristic deployment.

## Vervaeke's framing
Heuristics are simplified search strategies that trade comprehensiveness for tractability. The standard view (Kahneman/Tversky) treats them as biases — cognitive shortcuts that deviate from normative statistical reasoning. Vervaeke follows Simon and Gigerenzer in contesting this framing: given combinatorial explosion, there is no such thing as exhaustive rational search for a finite system in an open-ended world. Heuristics are not shortcuts away from rationality but the *form* that rationality necessarily takes under real-world constraints (Ep 26).

**Ecological rationality**: A heuristic is not assessed against a frictionless ideal of deductive completeness but against whether it fits the environment in which it operates. "Take the best" (pick on the single most predictive cue and stop) outperforms complex multi-variable regression in many natural environments. The rationality of a heuristic is measured by its ecological fit, not its conformity to abstract norms (Ep 26).

**Heuristics and bounded rationality**: Simon's concept of bounded rationality — organisms optimize given their constraints rather than globally — is the theoretical home for heuristics. We don't find optima; we satisfice. But satisficing with well-calibrated heuristics in a well-matched environment is not inferior to optimization in an idealized frictionless world; it is rational in the only sense that matters for real agents (Ep 26).

**Heuristics and relevance realization**: Heuristics are the explicit, propositionally accessible tip of the iceberg of RR. Below the level of articulable heuristics lies the non-propositional relevance realization machinery that determines the salience landscape on which heuristics operate. RR is to heuristics what tacit knowledge is to explicit rules: the enabling condition that cannot itself be cashed out propositionally. This is why merely giving people better heuristics does not fix systematic misalignment — the underlying RR machinery needs to be recalibrated, not just the surface rules (Ep 26, Ep 28).

**Failure modes**: When environmental cues change (mismatch between trained environment and current one), heuristics that were once rational become biases. Cognitive bias is not a property of the heuristic in isolation but of the heuristic-environment interaction. This connects to Vervaeke's account of parasitic processing and the cultivation of proper attunement (Ep 26).
