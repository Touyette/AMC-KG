# Eightfold Path

> The fourth of the [[Four Noble Truths]]: the Buddhist practical path of development organized into eight dimensions (right view, right intention, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, right concentration); for Vervaeke, the paradigm integrated ecology of practices for addressing the meaning crisis at its cognitive-affective root.

## Vervaeke's framing
The Eightfold Path (Pali: ariya atthangika magga) is the Buddhist response to dukkha — not a series of commands or rules but a description of the kind of life in which the cultivation toward nirvana can proceed. Vervaeke treats it as an integrated ecology of practices: each dimension supports and is supported by the others, and the whole is more than the sum of its parts (Ep 08).

**The three groupings**: Traditionally the Eightfold Path is organized into three groups: (1) **Wisdom** (prajna): right view (seeing the three marks and the four noble truths accurately) and right intention (renouncing craving, ill will, and harm); (2) **Ethics** (sila): right speech, right action, right livelihood — the behavioral embodiment of non-harm that creates the conditions for stable practice; (3) **Meditation** (samadhi): right effort (energy and commitment to practice), right mindfulness (sati — meta-attentive awareness), right concentration (development of meditative absorption). These three support each other: ethics reduces distraction and harm; meditation deepens the cognitive-affective insight that right view requires; right view motivates and guides the ethical and meditative dimensions (Ep 08).

**Ecology of practices**: The Eightfold Path is precisely an ecology of practices in Vervaeke's sense: no single element is separately sufficient; the whole system is organized so that each dimension corrects the potential pathologies of the others. Right view without ethical grounding can produce arrogance; ethical practice without meditative depth can produce moralism; meditation without right view can produce dissociation. The path holds them together in a self-supporting system (Ep 08).

**The path as RR recalibration**: In Vervaeke's terms, the Eightfold Path is a systematic recalibration of the relevance realization machinery: right mindfulness directly works on the salience landscape; right view restructures the propositional and perspectival knowing; right action and right livelihood restructure the behavioral-procedural layer; right intention recalibrates the motivational orientation. The whole practice aims at the systematic dismantling of tanha (craving) as the dominant organizing principle of the salience landscape (Ep 08).
