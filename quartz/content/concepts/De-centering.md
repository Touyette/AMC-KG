# De-centering

> The shift from egocentric (self-relative) to allocentric (world-relative) orientation that characterizes higher states of consciousness; the narcissistic super-salience of the ego is eclipsed by the salience of reality itself.

## Vervaeke's framing
In ordinary waking, one's perspective is organized egocentrically: left/right/front/back are defined relative to the self. De-centering is the move to an allocentric frame — describing position relative to external coordinates (e.g., the compass). In higher states of consciousness, people describe their experience from a markedly less egocentric standpoint: they are released from the prison-cell of the ego's super-salience, echoing the Buddhist concepts of Nirvana (to blow out) and Moksha (release). Yaden et al. (2017) identified de-centering as a key structural feature of these reports and found it was predictive of significant life improvement across multiple domains. Vervaeke reads this through the ontonormativity lens: when the salience of reality finally eclipses the self-idealizing glow of the ego, this is not ego-death but a rebalancing — the world registers its actual weight (Ep 11).
