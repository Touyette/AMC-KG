# Ontological Depth Perception

> The capacity to use reason and observation to see into the underlying structure of reality — not spatial depth but the depth of what underlies appearances.

## Vervaeke's framing
Vervaeke introduces this concept while explaining what Thales inaugurated with natural philosophy. Just as spatial depth perception allows us to see how far away objects are, ontological depth perception allows the mind to perceive the underlying substance and forces that make things what they are, beneath their surface appearances. Thales was doing this when he asked "what is everything made of?" — he was not generating myth or narrative but using rational analysis to probe beneath phenomena. This capacity produces awe and wonder as its characteristic emotional tone: to see into the depths of reality is to encounter something that exceeds one's comprehension but draws one forward. Ontological depth perception is a key feature of the Natural Philosophers that Socrates both inherits and redirects toward the self (Ep 04).
