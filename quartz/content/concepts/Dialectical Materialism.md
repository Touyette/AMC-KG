# Dialectical Materialism

> Marx's inversion of Hegel's Dialectic: history is driven not by ideas (Geist) but by the material conflict between socioeconomic classes, working through contradictions toward a final secular utopia.

## Vervaeke's framing
Marx accepts Hegel's dialectical structure but rejects its idealist premise. For Hegel, the clash is between ideas that differentiate and then integrate at a higher level. For Marx, following Feuerbach's critique of religion as projection and alienation, the real motor of history is the material "monster" in Plato's tripartite soul — the gut, the economic. Classes (socioeconomic modes of life) are in systematic tension and political conflict; their dialectic is not conceptual but material and violent. Capitalism contains inherent self-contradictions that must eventually be resolved in a classless, peaceful, free society — the secular promised land. Vervaeke reads this as completing Hegel's secularisation of the Judaeo-Christian narrative: God's plan → Geist's plan → the dialectic of economic forces. The revolutionary call to arms is participation in the Kairos of history. Like all pseudo-religious ideologies, it promises utopia through violent historical participation but lacks the wisdom and psycho-technologies for genuine human transformation (Ep 24).
