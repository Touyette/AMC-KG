# Depth Psychology

> The family of psychological traditions that posit and work with unconscious mental processes — depths beneath conscious awareness — as central to understanding the human psyche; founded by Freud's psychoanalysis, extended into Adlerian and Jungian schools; the tradition that (in Jung's version) becomes one of Vervaeke's five prophets.

## Role in the argument
Depth psychology enters AMC primarily through [[Carl Jung]], whose analytic psychology Vervaeke treats as one of the five prophets of the meaning crisis. But the broader tradition provides the context: all three of the major depth psychologists (Freud, Adler, Jung) engaged with the cultural collapse that produced the meaning crisis and developed practices for addressing the psychological damage it causes (Ep 49).

**Freud's hydraulic model**: [[Sigmund Freud]]'s foundational contribution — the systematic account of the unconscious as the reservoir of repressed drives — is the "hydraulic model" Vervaeke contrasts with Jung's organic model. For Freud, psychic energy (libido) builds up like pressure and must be discharged or sublimated; neurosis is the result of failed discharge. Vervaeke notes that this is a fundamentally Cartesian-mechanistic picture of the psyche (Ep 49).

**Jung's organic model**: [[Carl Jung]] replaces the hydraulic model with an organic/autopoietic one: the psyche is a self-organizing system that seeks wholeness ([[Individuation]]) through the dialectical relationship between the conscious ego and the unconscious Self. The unconscious is not a pressure reservoir but a source of compensatory wisdom — the [[Archetypes]] are virtual engines that organize salience and meaning-making. This makes Jung's model far more amenable to Vervaeke's 4E account of cognition (Ep 49).

**Tillich's dialogue**: [[Paul Tillich]]'s existentialist theology was developed in explicit dialogue with depth psychology — he saw the psychoanalytic account of unconscious anxiety as converging with his existentialist account of existential anxiety. Tillich appears as one of the prophets partly because of this depth-psychological engagement (Ep 50).

**The prophets cluster**: The depth psychology tradition shows up in the prophets arc as both a problem (Buber's critique of Jung's intra-psychism) and a solution (Jung's active imagination and dream interpretation as concrete psycho-technologies). Vervaeke wants to retrieve what is valuable in depth psychology (the imaginal, the individuation process, the engagement with the unconscious) while grounding it in 4E cognitive science rather than Cartesian-Kantian assumptions (Ep 49).
