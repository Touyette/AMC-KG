# Protestant Reformation

> The 16th century religious movement initiated by Martin Luther (1517) that challenged the authority of the Catholic Church, established the doctrines of sola fide and sola scriptura, and restructured Western Christianity; for Vervaeke, a major contributor to the meaning crisis through its credalization of religion and destruction of sapiential institutions.

## Role in the argument
The Protestant Reformation is for Vervaeke one of the key historical events that produced the conditions for the meaning crisis. It is not simply a theological dispute but a restructuring of the entire cognitive-cultural ecology through which Western Christendom had organized meaning, wisdom cultivation, and the relationship between individual conscience and institutional authority (Ep 21).

**Credalization**: The Reformation's emphasis on sola fide (faith alone) over sacramental practice shifted the center of religious life from participatory, embodied practice (confession, Mass, pilgrimage, penance, monastic life) to propositional assent (do you believe the correct doctrines?). This is [[Credo]] displacing [[Religio]]: religion becomes primarily about what you believe rather than what practices you engage in and how they transform you. The long-term cultural consequence is a frame in which religion is evaluated by its beliefs, not its transformative practices (Ep 21).

**Sapiential obsolescence**: The Catholic Church, whatever its corruption, had been the institutional carrier of wisdom cultivation in Western Christendom: universities, monasteries, confraternities, the system of confessor-penitent relationships, spiritual direction, the whole infrastructure of what Vervaeke calls an ecology of practices. The Reformation's attack on Church authority, combined with the subsequent fragmentation (hundreds of Protestant denominations), dismantled this ecology without providing an adequate replacement. The individual is left with the Bible and private conscience — but no institutional framework for the patient, progressive cultivation of wisdom (Ep 21).

**The proliferation of sects and the credal arms race**: Once sola scriptura was established, the question became which interpretation of scripture was authoritative. The answer splintered Christianity into ever-more-numerous denominations, each with their own creedal formulations. Religion became increasingly credal (belief-focused) and decreasingly participatory, as each group distinguished itself from others primarily by doctrinal differences rather than by distinctive practices (Ep 21).
