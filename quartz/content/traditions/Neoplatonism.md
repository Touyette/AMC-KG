# Neoplatonism

> Plotinus's 3rd-century CE synthesis of Platonic anagoge, Aristotelian theory of knowing and levels of being, and Stoic therapeutic practice — the grand unified field theory of ancient Mediterranean spirituality.

## Role in the argument
Vervaeke presents Neoplatonism as the culmination of the Greek Axial tradition: Plotinus integrates Plato's anagoge (ascent toward the real), Aristotle's conformity theory of knowing and levels of actuality/potentiality, and Stoic self-cultivation into a single, mutually supporting system. The key insight is that reality and knowing are unified: things are more real as they are more integrated, understanding is finding the underlying unity, and anagogic ascent simultaneously makes one more real and brings one into contact with what is more real. At the apex stands The One — not a being but the principle of all integration, accessible only through gnosis (being one), not propositional knowledge.

For Vervaeke's narrative, Neoplatonism's importance is threefold: (1) it shows that in ancient spirituality there was no split between science, therapy, and spirituality — they were fully integrated; (2) it directly shaped Augustine and thereby the entire Christian medieval synthesis; (3) it provides the template — through Plotinus → Proclus → Corbin → Barfield — for how the meaning crisis response can recover participatory knowing without two-worlds mythology (Ep 18, Ep 19, Ep 48–50).
