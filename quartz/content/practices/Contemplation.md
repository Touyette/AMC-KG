# Contemplation

> The practice of sustained, non-discursive attention directed at what is most real, most fundamental, or most worthy of attention — from Platonic theoria (direct intellectual contact with the Forms) through Christian contemplatio (resting in God) through Buddhist samatha-vipassana to modern mindfulness; a paradigm psychotechnology for the cultivation of participatory knowing.

## Role in the argument
Contemplation is the practice that Vervaeke returns to most consistently as the paradigm of wisdom-tradition psychotechnology. It is not "mere" meditation or relaxation but a rigorous training in the quality and direction of attention — aimed at genuine contact with what is most real. Vervaeke treats it as continuous with the cognitive processes of flow and insight but amplified and systematized into a transformative trajectory (Ep 05, Ep 08, Ep 18).

**Platonic theoria**: In Plato and the Neoplatonists, theoria (contemplation) is the intellectual vision of the Forms — the culminating activity of the philosophical life. It is not discursive reasoning (which moves through propositions step by step) but direct, non-sequential apprehension of what is real. The philosopher ascends through mathematical objects and dialectic toward the Form of the Good, which is known by a kind of intellectual vision that transcends ordinary cognition (Ep 05).

**Christian contemplatio**: In the Christian mystical tradition (Origen, Gregory of Nyssa, Augustine, Meister Eckhart), contemplation is the soul's rest in God — beyond discursive thought, beyond image, in a kind of loving attention that transcends the subject-object divide. This is the "apophatic" dimension of Christian spirituality: God is known by unknowing, by the silencing of the ordinary cognitive modes (Ep 18, Ep 20).

**Buddhist bhavana**: Buddhist meditation practice (bhavana: "cultivation") includes both samatha (tranquility/concentration) and vipassana (insight). Together they constitute a contemplative path: samatha stabilizes and clarifies attention; vipassana uses that stable attention to see the three marks of existence (impermanence, no-fixed-self, unsatisfactoriness) directly. This seeing is not intellectual assent to propositions but a participatory reconfiguration of how one relates to experience (Ep 08).

**Contemplation and RR**: In Vervaeke's framework, contemplation works by deautomatizing habitual patterns of salience (the usual relevance assignments), temporarily releasing the grip of parasitic processing, and allowing a more adequate relevance realization to emerge. The quality of attention that contemplation cultivates — open, receptive, non-grasping — is the attentional structure that allows genuine contact with what is (Ep 11).

**Contemplation as meta-practice**: In the ecology of practices, contemplation often has a meta-role: it is the practice that calibrates the quality of the other practices. Without some form of contemplative attention, even intellectually rigorous practices can become parasitic — running in the having mode rather than generating genuine participation (Ep 31).
