# Prosoche

> Stoic practice of continuous, moment-to-moment attention to one's own inner states — judgments, impulses, desires, and aversions — measured against the standard of what the sage would think or do; Pierre Hadot identifies prosoche as the central Stoic spiritual exercise and the one from which the others derive their power.

## Vervaeke's framing
Prosoche is the Stoic psycho-technology for sustained metacognitive self-monitoring. It is not meditation in the sense of a set-aside time but an ongoing practice woven through every moment of daily life: before acting, notice the impulse; before speaking, notice the judgment; before deciding, consult the internalized sage. Marcus Aurelius's *Meditations* is the best surviving evidence of what prosoche looks like across a lifetime — he is essentially practicing prosoche in writing (Ep 43).

**What prosoche attends to**: Epictetus teaches that prosoche should focus exclusively on what is "up to us" — our judgments, impulses, desires, and aversions — because these are the only things genuinely within our power. The aim is not to suppress all feeling but to ensure that one's inner responses are endorsed by reason and calibrated to what is genuinely good. This is a continuous process of recalibrating the agent's relevance realization system: what gets flagged as important, what gets dismissed as indifferent (Ep 43).

**Prosoche as metacognition**: For Vervaeke, prosoche is a sustained metacognitive practice: it trains the agent to maintain a second-order perspective on their own first-order responses — not getting absorbed into impulses but watching them, evaluating them, choosing which to endorse. This is precisely the "standing back from oneself" that addresses the reflectiveness gap and that the Stoics saw as constitutive of philosophical wisdom. It integrates all four kinds of knowing: propositional (what is true of the situation), procedural (how to apply the Stoic framework), perspectival (taking the sage's perspective), and participatory (genuinely becoming a different kind of agent through practice) (Ep 43).

**Hadot's recovery**: Hadot argued that prosoche was not merely a theoretical position but an actual daily practice — the text evidence (Meditations, Epictetus's Discourses) shows not doctrine but practice in action. Vervaeke uses this Hadotian recovery of prosoche as evidence that the ancient philosophical traditions were wisdom-cultivation systems, not merely theoretical enterprises (Ep 43).
