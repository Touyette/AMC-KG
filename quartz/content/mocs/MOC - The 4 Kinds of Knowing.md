# MOC - The 4 Kinds of Knowing

Vervaeke's epistemological framework, introduced in Ep 05 and operative throughout AMC. The claim is that the dominant philosophical tradition has conflated "knowledge" with propositional knowledge alone — and that this conflation is a symptom of the meaning crisis, not an innocent category error. Wisdom requires all four modes in dynamic integration.

---

## The Four Modes

### 1. Propositional Knowledge (knowing-that)

[[Propositional Knowledge|Knowing-that]]: statements that can be true or false; the knowledge that logic operates on; captured in declarative sentences. "Water is H₂O." "Socrates is mortal." The dominant currency of academic philosophy and science (Ep 05).

**Why it's not enough**: Propositional knowledge presupposes a framing — a selection of what aspects of a situation are relevant to represent. The framing is not itself propositional; it is already delivered by the RR machinery before any proposition is assessed. A person can know all the relevant propositions about a life decision and still not understand what to do — because understanding requires the other three kinds (Ep 28, Ep 40).

### 2. Procedural Knowledge (knowing-how)

[[Procedural Knowledge|Knowing-how]]: skill and competence; the knowledge that enables performance; largely tacit and not fully articulable. Riding a bicycle. Delivering a perfect Socratic cross-examination. Cooking by touch. You cannot learn to ride a bicycle by reading propositions about bicycle-riding (Ep 05).

**The Polanyi connection**: [[Michael Polanyi]]'s subsidiary/focal awareness model (SA/FA) is the clearest account of how procedural knowledge works: when riding, attention is focal on the goal (where to go) while the mechanics of pedaling, balancing, steering are subsidiary — taken for granted, not attended to. Expertise consists partly in having more and more made subsidiary. This is the cognitive structure of [[Attentional Scaling]] (Ep 05, Ep 43).

### 3. Perspectival Knowledge (knowing what it's like)

[[Perspectival Knowledge|Knowing what it's like]]: knowledge from a particular point of view; irreducible to propositions. Thomas Nagel's "what is it like to be a bat?" is the canonical example — no amount of propositional neuroscience captures the first-person quality of bat sonar. But perspectival knowing is not just about qualia; it is about the *meaning* of a situation as apprehended from a particular vantage point (Ep 05).

**Perspectival and the meaning crisis**: The Cartesian grammar displaces secondary qualities (beauty, meaning, purpose) into the subjective mind while treating the objective world as primary-quality-only. This severs perspectival knowing from any claim to track reality — turning it into "mere subjective experience." Recovering perspectival knowing as genuine (as tracking real features of the organism-environment relationship) is part of what the 4E account and the RR framework accomplish (Ep 22, Ep 38).

### 4. Participatory Knowledge (knowing-by, knowing through at-onement)

[[Participatory Knowledge|Knowing-by]]: the mode of knowing in which the knower and the known share something — are in genuine contact, are in some sense conforming to each other. The [[Conformity Theory of Knowing]] (Aristotle): the mind conforms itself to what is known; knowing is participation, not mere representation. Da'at (Hebrew): knowing God through intimate participatory relation (Ep 05, Ep 03).

**Why this is foundational**: The four kinds form a dependency hierarchy: propositional depends on procedural (you need skill to engage propositions), which depends on perspectival (you need a situated vantage point), which depends on participatory (you need a basic contact with what you are knowing). RR is grounded in participatory knowing — the organism's basic co-constitution with its environment (Ep 29).

**Gnosis as participatory knowing at its deepest**: [[Gnosis]] is participatory knowing at the level of identity — the kind of knowing where who you are changes in the knowing. "Like is known by like": to know the Forms, you must become more like the Forms; to know God, you must become more like God. This is the deepest form of participatory knowing, and the model for all genuine wisdom (Ep 16, Ep 17).

---

## The Hierarchy of Dependency

```
Participatory (foundation)
  └── Perspectival (depends on participatory)
        └── Procedural (depends on perspectival)
              └── Propositional (depends on procedural)
```

This hierarchy matters because: attempts to explain RR through representations (propositional) or computation (propositional + procedural) fail because they presuppose what they are trying to explain — [[Aspectuality]] presupposes RR (Ep 29). RR must be grounded at the participatory level.

---

## Wisdom as Integration of All Four

[[Wisdom]] is not mastery of propositional knowledge about life. It is the dynamic integration of all four kinds in an ongoing ecology of practices:

- **Propositional**: rich factual knowledge about fundamental pragmatics of life (Berlin Wisdom Paradigm criterion 1)
- **Procedural**: knowing how to apply that knowledge in practice; phronesis (criterion 2)
- **Perspectival**: lifespan contextualism; the ability to take the view from outside one's own current concerns; Solomon effect (criterion 3–4)
- **Participatory**: identity-level transformation; [[Gnosis]]; the love of wisdom that changes who you are

The [[Berlin Wisdom Paradigm]] is valuable but misses the perspectival and participatory dimensions. The [[Socratic Method]] integrates all four: propositional examination (is this definition true?), procedural training (how to cross-examine), perspectival shift (taking Socrates's vantage point on your own soul), and participatory transformation (becoming a different kind of person through the dialogue).

## The 4 Kinds and the RR Cascade

Each stage of the [[Continuity Hypothesis|RR continuum]] is dominated by a different kind of knowing:
- Ordinary problem-solving: propositional + procedural
- [[Insight]]: restructuring of the framing — propositional gives way to perspectival
- [[Flow State]]: absorbed in procedural; perspectival and participatory
- [[Higher States of Consciousness]]: perspectival undergoes deautomatization; participatory foregrounds
- [[Enlightenment (Reverse-Engineered)]]: deep participatory transformation — identity-level change

---

## The 4 Kinds Across the Historical Narrative

The meaning crisis is partly the story of what happens when one kind of knowing colonizes all others:
- **Propositional dominance** (scholastic period, Averroes): definitions and propositions displace participatory knowing; the intensive self replaces extensive conformity
- **Procedural dominance** (modern techne): instrumental reason — knowing how to achieve goals — displaces knowing what goals are worth pursuing
- **Perspectival collapse** (Cartesian grammar): perspectival knowing relocated to the "merely subjective" — beauty, meaning, purpose become "secondary qualities" with no grip on reality
- **Participatory starvation**: religio, sacredness, gnosis — the participatory dimension of knowing — starved out of the culture; replaced by credo (propositional assent) on one side and pure technique on the other

The response (4E cognitive science, Vervaeke's RR theory, the prophets' framework) is a restoration of the full ecology of knowing — re-grounding propositional and procedural in their perspectival and participatory base.
