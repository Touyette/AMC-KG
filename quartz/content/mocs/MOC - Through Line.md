# MOC - Through Line

The single continuous argument of *Awakening from the Meaning Crisis*. This note traces the spine from the first lecture to the last. Each section is a chapter in the argument; the links are the nodes where the argument pivots.

---

## Chapter 1 — The Origin: The Axial Revolution (Ep 01–05)

The series begins with the question: *Why do so many people feel their lives lack meaning?* Vervaeke locates the problem historically. Around 800–300 BCE, the [[Axial Revolution]] produced a global transformation: the invention of [[Second-Order Thinking]] through [[Alphabetic Literacy]], [[Psycho-Technology|psycho-technologies]] like money and writing, and new practices of self-examination. This shattered the [[Continuous Cosmos]] — the pre-Axial worldview in which the sacred permeated the natural without division — and replaced it with the [[Two Worlds Mythology]]: a sharp divide between the sacred/real world and the mundane/fallen world. This is [[The Great Dis-embedding]].

The Axial revolution is not a mistake. It enables [[Dialectic]], the [[Socratic Method]], [[The 4 Kinds of Knowing]], the [[Myth of the Cave]], [[Anagoge]], and eventually science. But it sets a trap: the two-worlds mythology enables meaning-making at scale while severing the individual from the participatory connection to the ground of being.

---

## Chapter 2 — The Peak: Socrates, Plato, and the Philosophical Response (Ep 04–07)

[[Socrates]] is the pivot figure: he invents dialectic as a psycho-technology for combating [[Self-Deception]] and [[Bullshit]], opening the way to [[Participatory Knowledge]] through the examination of one's own soul. His death at Athens' hands is paradigmatic: the meaning crisis is partly the story of what happens when a culture loses its Socratic capacity.

[[Plato]] systematizes this into: [[The 4 Kinds of Knowing]] (propositional, procedural, perspectival, participatory); [[Anagoge]] (upward transformative movement through symbols); the [[Tripartite Psyche]] and [[Thymos]]; [[Eros]] as erotic knowing; and the [[Conformity Theory of Knowing]] (knowing is participatory conformity to what is known). The [[Myth of the Cave]] encapsulates the whole argument. This is the height of the Greek response to the Axial situation.

---

## Chapter 3 — The Hellenistic Meaning Crisis and Its Responses (Ep 08–19)

The death of Alexander the Great triggers [[Domicide]] at scale. The Hellenistic meaning crisis is the first instance of what we now face globally. The responses multiply: [[Middle Path|Stoic middle path]]; [[Epicurus|Epicurean friendship]]; [[Cynicism|Cynic radical self-sufficiency]]; and eventually [[Neoplatonism]] through [[Plotinus]] ([[The One]]; emanation; anagoge as ontological ascent).

Simultaneously: [[Mindfulness|Buddhist mindfulness]] and the [[Four Noble Truths]] offer a non-theistic practice-path that dissolves [[Dukkha]] through the [[Middle Path]]. [[Gnosis]] emerges through Paul ([[Da'ath|da'at]]; [[Agape]]; knowing by being transformed) and through the [[Gnosticism|Gnostic movements]].

Running through this section is Vervaeke's core cognitive theory: [[Relevance Realization]] (RR) as the mind's mechanism for homing in on what matters; [[Insight]] as RR at the meta-level; [[Flow State]] as optimal RR; [[Higher States of Consciousness]] as the continuum of the same exapted machinery ([[Continuity Hypothesis]]). All of these will be needed to understand the prophets.

## Chapter 4 — The Three Orders and the Fall (Ep 07, 20–27)

The Roman imperial synthesis united three orders: [[Three Orders|nomological]] (the natural order, understood as rationally structured); normative (the moral order, grounded in that structure); and narrative (the story order, telling how individuals fit into both). Christianity's success is to hold these three together in a worldview that makes individual lives meaningful. But the three orders begin to fragment:

- [[Nominalism]] (Ockham): universals are mere names; only particulars exist. Severs [[Conformity Theory of Knowing]] and the basis for the normative order.
- [[Galileo Galilei|Galileo]]'s [[Primary and Secondary Qualities]]: beauty, meaning, purpose are "secondary" (in the mind); the objective world is mathematical-mechanical. Drains the world of meaning.
- [[Martin Luther|Luther]]'s [[Individual Conscience]]: interiorizes religion; produces [[Sapiential Obsolescence]] (the church can no longer provide the meaning-making machinery it once did).
- [[René Descartes|Descartes]]' [[Mind-Body Problem]]: subjective/objective split becomes philosophically foundational. [[Cartesian Cultural Grammar]] now frames all our default thinking.

Result: the [[Three Orders]] fragment. The nomological order (science) can no longer ground the normative order (morality); the narrative order (personal story) is untethered from both. This is the structural cause of the [[Meaning Crisis]].

## Chapter 5 — The Meaning Crisis Diagnosed (Ep 27–37)

The meaning crisis is not a single event but a constellation of conditions: [[Parasitic Processing]] (meaning-machines hijacked by addictive loops); [[Modal Confusion]] (having mode displacing being mode); [[Reflectiveness Gap]] (reflection undermines commitment); [[Absurdity]] (Nagel: cosmic pointlessness vs. life's demands for seriousness); [[Alienation]]; [[Existential Entrapment]]; and [[Existential Anxiety]] (non-specific, no known response).

Vervaeke introduces the perennial response framework: [[Religio]] (re-linking attention and being; the structural core of all genuine religious practice); [[Sacredness]] (the transjective property of inexhaustible drawing-into-self-transcendence); [[Numinous]] (Otto: mysterium tremendum et fascinans); [[Mythos]] (symbol + story + ritual as a whole); [[Communitas]] (Turner: transformative encounter through dissolved roles). The positive face of the numinous is [[Wonder]]; its cognitive structure is [[Mystery]] (inexhaustible surplus) + [[Awe]] (vastness that challenges framing).

[[Symbol]] is the central concept here: participatory, transjective representation that affords [[Anagoge|anagogic transformation]]; distinct from sign; made of surplus meaning; the cognitive hinge between current and aspired self.

## Chapter 6 — The Cognitive-Scientific Foundation (Ep 28–45)

Running in parallel to the historical narrative, Vervaeke builds the cognitive-scientific framework that will allow him to talk about spirituality, wisdom, and enlightenment naturalistically. Key moves:

- [[Relevance Realization]] as the solution to the [[Frame Problem]] and [[Combinatorial Explosion]]
- [[Salience Landscape]] (four layers: salience, presence, depth, significance)
- [[Transjectivity]] (neither purely subjective nor objective; applies to relevance, symbols, sacredness, attunement)
- [[The 4 Kinds of Knowing]] (propositional, procedural, perspectival, participatory) — wisdom requires all four in integrated ecology
- [[Wisdom]] defined: an ecology of psycho-technologies that dynamically enhance RR across all four kinds of knowing
- [[Rationality (Expanded)|Expanded rationality]]: any systematically reliable psycho-technology that overcomes self-deception and cultivates flourishing
- [[Enlightenment (Reverse-Engineered)]] as the cognitive-existential transformation of RR machinery: deautomatization → insight → flow → higher states → mystical → enlightenment (the [[Continuity Hypothesis]])
- [[Wise Cultivation of Enlightenment]] (WCE): Vervaeke's synthesis of wisdom + enlightenment + religio + sacredness in a scientific worldview + co-op community of practice

## Chapter 7 — The Prophets Arc (Ep 46–50)

Vervaeke's own response to the meaning crisis takes the form of putting RR theory into dialogue with five prophets:

**[[Martin Heidegger]]** (Ep 46–47): Dasein ([[Dasein]]) as the being-whose-being-is-in-question. [[Attunement]] (Stimmung) = pre-cognitive, non-subjective grounding of the agent-arena relationship ≈ RR. [[Truth as Aletheia]] (disclosure, not correspondence). [[Forgetfulness of Being]] as the epochal structural cause of the meaning crisis. [[Onto-Theology]] → [[Nihilism]]. [[Contact Epistemology]] through [[Edmund Husserl|Husserl]]/[[Hubert Dreyfus|Dreyfus]]. Heidegger's gap: political complicity (Nazism) and insufficient practices.

**[[Henry Corbin]]** (Ep 48): [[Imaginary vs Imaginal|Imaginal vs. imaginary]] distinction. The [[Imaginary vs Imaginal|symbol's four imaginal properties]] (translucent, transjective, trajective, transformative, transtemporal). [[Divine Double]] (Stang): the sacred second self one aspires to become; Corbin's angel as its imaginal symbol. [[Gnosis]] as dynamic coupling (like known by like). Corbin's gap: too intra-psychic in a different sense — the world transforms but the body/community practices are underdeveloped.

**[[Carl Jung]]** (Ep 49): [[Archetypes]] as virtual engines of salience landscaping (imaginal, not imaginary). [[Individuation]] as lifelong ego-Self anagogic dialogue. The [[Non-Logical Identity|non-logical identity]] of S1 → S2 through participation in emergence. Jung's gap: Kantian/intra-psychic; [[Martin Buber|Buber]]/Corbin criticize him for not properly representing the transjective encounter.

**[[Paul Tillich]]** (Ep 50): [[Faith]] as ultimate concern (participatory da'at directed at the ground of being). [[Ground of Being]] as non-onto-theological name for God. [[Method of Correlation]] as anagogic loop between existential ekstasis and depths of reality. [[Non-Theism]]: God beyond the God of theism; rejects four shared premises of theism and atheism. [[Epektasis]] (Gregory of Nyssa): infinite self-transcendence, no resting in the ground of being. Tillich's gap: lacks practices (no active imagination equivalent).

**[[Owen Barfield]]** (Ep 50): [[Final Participation]]: recovery of original participation integrated with rational science. [[Poiesis]]: participatory creativity as ekstasis; synergistic (Durchbruch + Gelassenheit). [[Infinity of Reality]]: finite longing for the infinite; the inexhaustible moreness that draws into self-transcendence. Barfield's gap: calls for a science of meaning cultivation but doesn't provide one.

---

## Chapter 8 — The Resolution: Relevance Realization as Response

Vervaeke's closing move: his own RR theory is the science of meaning cultivation that completes Barfield's project, bridges Heidegger's attunement theory, Corbin's imaginal, Jung's individuation, and Tillich's method of correlation — all into a naturalistically respectable, experimentally tractable framework. The AMC series is itself a practice of putting these great thinkers into "deep dialogue, critical but creative, between them" to "afford a potential synoptic integration."

The WCE framework ([[Wise Cultivation of Enlightenment]]) is Vervaeke's positive proposal: wisdom + enlightenment + religio + sacredness, situated in a scientific worldview, embodied in co-op communities of practice, supported by a wisdom wiki. This is the response to the meaning crisis.
