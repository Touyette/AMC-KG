# Galileo Galilei

> The scientist who kills the universe: inertial motion eliminates teleology and inner life from matter; the primary/secondary quality distinction traps all meaning, purpose, and beauty inside the mind.

## Role in the argument
Vervaeke presents Galileo as the figure who completes the epistemological catastrophe begun by Nominalism and Copernicus, making two interlocking moves (Ep 20).

**Inertial motion.** The Aristotelian/Augustinian universe was alive: everything moved purposefully, driven by inner drives toward where it belonged, making the whole cosmos a vast teleological narrative. Galileo shows this is false: things do not move because they have inner drives or because they are trying to reach their proper place. Things move because an external force has struck them, and they will continue in that direction until another external force intervenes. Motion is not purposeful — it is inertial. Matter is by definition **inert**: not alive, not self-moving, not directed. Vervaeke's phrase: "Before Galileo, everything is alive. After Galileo, everything is dead." The universe becomes a purposeless machine. Humans are now "ontological castaways" — a tiny island of purpose in a vast desert of purposelessness; weird, alone, not belonging to the cosmos. Evil also loses its metaphysical basis: the old explanation was that evil is a hole in being, pure chaos, disintegration. But now matter is just neutral, inert stuff — no metaphysics of evil is possible (Ep 20).

**Primary and secondary qualities.** Galileo uses mathematics in a completely abstract fashion — a triangle can represent the relationship between distance, time, and speed with no experiential resemblance to reality. He concludes that mathematics is the real language of the universe. Mathematical properties — length, mass, velocity, geometric shape — are **primary qualities**: they are objective, genuinely in the world. Everything else — sweetness, color, beauty, meaning, purpose, value — cannot be captured mathematically. Therefore it is not in the world; it is **secondary**, produced inside the mind in response to primary properties. It is a kind of illusion created by the subjective meaning-making machinery. Meaning, beauty, purpose, and value are locked inside the head. The world is drained of significance (Ep 20).

Together these two moves complete the fragmentation of the three orders. The nomological order (how the world is) is now a purposeless machine of inert matter with only mathematical properties. The normative order (what is better and worse) has no grounding in the structure of reality. The narrative order (where things are going) is gone — nothing moves toward anything. Galileo also gives us the scientific method, which Vervaeke reads as a legacy of the Axial Revolution: a way of letting the resistance of the inert world correct our willful self-deception (Ep 20).
