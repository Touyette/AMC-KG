# L.A. Paul

> American philosopher (b. 1966); developed the concept of transformative experience — experiences that are simultaneously epistemically and personally transformative, meaning you can't know in advance what the experience will be like AND you emerge from it as a different person; her work is central to Vervaeke's analysis of what wisdom traditions are asking of practitioners.

## Role in the argument
L.A. Paul enters AMC through her book *Transformative Experience* (2014). Vervaeke uses her framework to articulate the philosophical challenge at the heart of all wisdom traditions: how can one rationally commit to a transformative practice whose value cannot be known in advance, and whose product (a transformed self) is not the same person as the one who would make the commitment? (Ep 17, Ep 45).

**Epistemically transformative experiences**: Some experiences provide genuinely new kinds of knowledge — not just new information but new types of experience that could not be anticipated from prior information. Having a child, undergoing religious conversion, or experiencing severe illness are Paul's paradigm cases: no amount of testimony or description can tell you what it is like, because knowing-what-it-is-like is the thing you gain by having the experience. This is irreducibly perspectival knowledge (Ep 17).

**Personally transformative experiences**: Some experiences change who you are — your values, your self-conception, what you care about. The person who emerges from the experience is not the same person in the relevant sense as the person who entered it. This creates a peculiar challenge for rational decision-making: you cannot fully endorse the outcome in advance because the endorsing self doesn't yet exist (Ep 17).

**The aspiration paradox**: For Vervaeke, Paul's analysis crystallizes the central paradox of wisdom traditions: they ask you to undergo a transformation whose value and content you cannot know in advance, and which requires the exercise of a judgment you don't yet have. This is the aspiration paradox — how can you rationally begin a journey whose destination you cannot see? Vervaeke's solution draws on Callard's concept of proleptic rationality: one acts from the aspiring self, guided by glimpses and partial transformations rather than complete foreknowledge (Ep 17, Ep 45).

**Attentional scaling and transformative experience**: Vervaeke connects transformative experience to attentional scaling and insight: at the small scale, the insight experience has the structure of a mini-transformation — you emerge from the impasse as a different cognitive agent. The wisdom traditions are systematically amplifying this structure to the level of sustained identity transformation (Ep 17).
