# Michael Polanyi

> Hungarian-British philosopher of science (1891–1976); developed the concept of tacit knowledge ("we know more than we can tell"); articulated the subsidiary/focal awareness (SA/FA) model of skilled knowing; for Vervaeke, provides the epistemological framework for how symbols work and how psychotechnological internalization occurs.

## Role in the argument
Polanyi enters AMC through his account of tacit knowledge and the SA/FA model. Vervaeke uses these to explain: (1) how the 4 kinds of knowing relate (tacit knowledge is the procedural-participatory base that enables focal propositional knowledge); (2) how symbols function (the symbol is subsidiary to what it points at, which is focal); (3) how psychotechnological internalization works (a tool or practice begins as an explicit focal object and through mastery becomes subsidiary, extending the body-schema) (Ep 43).

**Tacit knowledge**: Polanyi's key insight: all knowledge has a tacit dimension. We know how to ride a bike without being able to fully articulate the principles involved; we recognize faces without being able to describe the algorithm; we perform skilled actions without being able to specify the knowledge that guides them. "We know more than we can tell." This tacit dimension is what Vervaeke calls procedural and participatory knowing — it cannot be fully converted into propositional form (Ep 43).

**Subsidiary/Focal Awareness**: Polanyi distinguishes between what we attend FROM (subsidiary awareness) and what we attend TO (focal awareness). When I use a hammer, I attend focally to the nail while attending subsidiarily to the feel of the hammer — the hammer has become an extension of my body-schema. When I read, I attend focally to meaning while attending subsidiarily to the letters. This structure of subsidiary-to-focal is how every skilled performance and every act of understanding is organized (Ep 43).

**Application to symbols**: Vervaeke applies the SA/FA model to symbolic knowing: a symbol functions subsidiarily — you attend FROM the symbol TO what it discloses. When the symbol becomes focal (you attend TO the symbol rather than FROM it), it ceases to function symbolically and becomes a mere sign or object. This is why the Reformation's shift from participatory ritual to propositional doctrine is a SA/FA collapse: the ritual became an object of belief rather than a vehicle of symbolic participation (Ep 43).
