# Victor Turner

> British-American anthropologist (1920–1983); developed the concepts of liminality and communitas; author of *The Ritual Process* (1969); for Vervaeke, the anthropologist whose work on ritual transformation provides the framework for understanding how collective practices address the perennial problems.

## Role in the argument
Turner enters AMC through his analysis of ritual process. His account of liminality (the threshold state between old and new identity) and communitas (the mode of being in which ordinary social structure dissolves, enabling transformative encounter) provides Vervaeke with the anthropological vocabulary for understanding how ritual and communal practice can produce the conditions for genuine transformation (Ep 37).

**Liminality**: In ritual processes (van Gennep's three-stage model: separation → liminality → reintegration), the liminal period is the transitional state in which the initiate has left their old social position but not yet taken on the new one. They are "betwixt and between" — outside the normal social structure, in a dangerous but fertile state of openness. Vervaeke connects liminality to the aporia of Socratic dialectic and the impasse-before-insight structure: the temporary dis-structuring that enables genuine restructuring (Ep 37).

**Communitas**: In the liminal state, ordinary social hierarchies dissolve and a different mode of relation becomes possible — what Turner calls communitas: a mode of togetherness characterized by equality, openness, and mutual recognition as human beings rather than as occupants of social roles. This is the mode of relation in which genuine transformative encounter — including the encounter with one's own deeper self — becomes possible. For Vervaeke, communitas is the social form that optimal wisdom-cultivation practices aim to create (Ep 37).

**Anti-structure**: Turner describes communitas as "anti-structure" — not the absence of structure but its productive undoing, which reveals the deeper human bonds beneath social roles. Anti-structure is not permanent (communities need structure to persist) but cyclically necessary: regular re-immersion in communitas regenerates the social bonds that structural roles can reify and deaden. This is why rituals in sustainable wisdom traditions are cyclical rather than one-time events (Ep 37).

**Ritual and religio**: Turner's work grounds Vervaeke's account of ritual as re-ligare — re-binding — in anthropological data: rituals do not just represent transformation but enact it, through the liminal disruption of ordinary self-understanding and the emergence of communitas. This makes ritual a form of religio rather than credo: it works through participatory transformation, not propositional assent (Ep 37).
