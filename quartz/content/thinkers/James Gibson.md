# James Gibson

> American psychologist (1904–1979); developed ecological psychology and the theory of affordances; argued that perception is direct (not inferential) and that what organisms perceive are action-possibilities relative to their capacities, not neutral sense data.

## Role in the argument
Gibson provides Vervaeke with the ecological-psychological foundation for the reality of affordances — and through them, the reality of relevance as a feature of organism-environment relations rather than a subjective projection onto a neutral world. If what we perceive is always already action-relevant structure (affordances), then relevance realization has genuine cognitive contact with a structured world, not just with internal representations (Ep 26, Ep 38).

**Direct perception**: Gibson's radical claim is that perception is direct — the organism does not infer a world from neutral sense data but directly picks up invariant information in the structured ambient optic array. This "ecological optics" bypasses the representationalist bottleneck and connects cleanly to enactivism: the organism and environment are in direct informational coupling, not mediated by internal models (Ep 26).

**Affordances as relational properties**: Gibson's key insight is that affordances are neither purely objective nor purely subjective but relational: they are real features of the organism-environment system. A surface that affords walking is objectively supportive and subjectively registered — neither side of this relation can be reduced to the other. This gives Vervaeke the philosophical grounding to argue that meaning is not projection but real fit (Ep 26).
