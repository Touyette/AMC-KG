# Sigmund Freud

> Austrian neurologist and founder of psychoanalysis (1856–1939); developed the theory of the unconscious, the hydraulic model of mind (libido as energy), the Oedipus complex, and the techniques of free association and dream interpretation; for Vervaeke, Freud is the foil against which Jung's more participatory approach to the unconscious is defined.

## Role in the argument
Freud enters AMC primarily as a contrast to Jung. Both took the unconscious seriously; both developed therapeutic practices aimed at bringing unconscious content to consciousness. But their models and methods differ in ways that are crucial to Vervaeke's argument (Ep 49).

**The hydraulic model**: Freud's model of the psyche is hydraulic: the mind is a system of energies (libido) that press for expression and are dammed up or redirected by repression, sublimation, and defense mechanisms. Psychological health is the adequate management of these energies — not their transformation into something fundamentally different. This is a fundamentally mechanistic picture of the mind, aligned with the scientific naturalism of Freud's era (Ep 49).

**The Oedipus complex and the personal unconscious**: Freud's unconscious is populated primarily by repressed personal content — childhood experiences, particularly those organized around the Oedipus complex (the child's ambivalent desire for the opposite-sex parent and rivalry with the same-sex parent). This makes the unconscious primarily a repository of personal history rather than a source of transpersonal meaning (Ep 49).

**The hermeneutics of suspicion**: Paul Ricoeur described Freud (alongside Marx and Nietzsche) as a "master of suspicion": the interpreter who treats manifest content as distortion and seeks the hidden, repressed motivation behind it. Vervaeke uses this framing: Freudian interpretation is always a decoding of concealment — what appears is not what it is, and the real meaning is what is being disguised. This contrasts with the wisdom tradition's aspiration toward honest, direct encounter with reality (Ep 49).

**Contrast with Jung**: Jung accepted Freud's emphasis on the unconscious but transformed the model: (1) the unconscious contains not just repressed personal content but transpersonal archetypes; (2) the goal is not management of libido but individuation — the self-realization of the whole psyche; (3) religion and symbol are not illusions to be debunked but genuine expressions of the psyche's self-understanding. These differences mark Jung's greater compatibility with the wisdom tradition (Ep 49).
