# Thomas Hobbes

> English philosopher (1588–1679); author of *Leviathan*; developed mechanistic materialism and the social contract theory; for Vervaeke, his significance lies in articulating the materialist alternative to Descartes within the 17th century scientific revolution, and in highlighting the political-social conditions required when the three orders have collapsed.

## Role in the argument
Hobbes enters AMC in Episode 22 as the foil to Descartes: where Descartes grounded the new scientific worldview in a mind-body dualism (res cogitans vs res extensa), Hobbes drew the opposite conclusion from the same scientific premises — there is only matter in motion. The mind is nothing but the motions of the brain; thinking is just sophisticated matter-motion (Ep 22).

**Mechanistic materialism**: Hobbes took the Galilean-Newtonian picture of the world as matter in motion and extended it all the way: not just bodies but minds, not just physics but psychology and politics, all reducible to the motions of matter. This was a radically different response to the scientific revolution than Descartes' dualism, but it faced the same problems: if everything is matter in motion, where does meaning come from? (Ep 22).

**State of nature and the social contract**: Hobbes's political philosophy is, in Vervaeke's reading, a response to the loss of the normative order (one of the three orders). Without a shared moral framework, human beings in the state of nature are in "war of all against all" — life is "solitary, poor, nasty, brutish, and short." The solution is the social contract: rational self-interest motivates the transfer of individual rights to the sovereign (Leviathan). But this is a purely procedural solution — it creates order without meaning, peace without virtue (Ep 22).

**Hobbes and the meaning crisis**: The Hobbesian picture — mechanistic universe, competitive individuals, order maintained only by the threat of violence — is what Vervaeke describes as the culturally impoverished endpoint of the collapse of the three orders. Hobbes was honest about what follows from the scientific materialism: no natural teleology, no normative order beyond utility and power, no sacred cosmos. The answer is the sovereign enforcing the contract — which is order without the conditions for meaning (Ep 22).
