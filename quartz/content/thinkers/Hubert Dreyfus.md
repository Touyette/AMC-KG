# Hubert Dreyfus

> American philosopher; key interpreter of Heidegger for cognitive science; founding critic of computational AI via Heidegger's frame problem insight; developer of optimal grip.

## Role in the argument
Dreyfus is the critical bridge between Heidegger's phenomenological ontology and Vervaeke's 4E cognitive science / RR framework. He shows that Heidegger's insights about attunement, participatory knowing, and the primacy of non-propositional coping are not just philosophical claims but have direct implications for understanding what cognition is and what AI cannot do (Ep 47).

**The Heidegger–Dreyfus–RR chain**: Dreyfus's key Heidegger-derived point: "Facts and rules are by themselves meaningless. To capture what Heidegger calls significance or involvement, they must be assigned relevance. But the predicates that must be added to define relevance are just more meaningless facts; and paradoxically, the more facts the computer is given, the harder it is to compute what is relevant to the current situation." This is the frame problem: staying at the propositional computational level produces combinatorial explosion and loss of coping ability. Dreyfus thus discovers/names the frame problem as an implication of Heidegger's critique of Cartesian cognitive science (Ep 47).

This gives Dreyfus's major works their shape: *What Computers Can't Do* (foundational critique of computational AI from a Heideggerian perspective) and *What Computers Still Can't Do* (updated version). *Being-In-The-World* is his deep commentary on Heidegger's *Being and Time*. With Charles Taylor he wrote *Retrieving Realism* (contact epistemology via phenomenology and 4E cog sci).

**Optimal grip**: Dreyfus develops the concept of optimal grip (from Merleau-Ponty's embodied cognition) as the non-propositional, non-computational process by which an organism adjusts its engagement to achieve the most adequate fit with its environment. Vervaeke uses this extensively as a phenomenological expression of relevance realization at the level of lived, bodily experience (Ep 47).

**Dreyfus's argument**: The move from Heidegger → Dreyfus → 4E cognitive science shows that Vervaeke's RR framework is not an imposition on the phenomenological tradition but a natural development of it. When Heidegger says attunement grounds the agent-arena relationship, and Dreyfus says this non-propositional coping is what computers can't do, and Vervaeke says this is relevance realization — these are not three different claims but a single insight at three different levels of analysis (Ep 47).
