# Immanuel Kant

> German philosopher who identified why the Newtonian linear-causal model cannot account for living things, inadvertently revealing the need for a dynamical systems approach.

## Role in the argument
Vervaeke introduces Kant primarily through the problem he poses rather than the solution he offers. Kant recognized why Newton's model (linear causation: A causes B causes C) was so successful: it prevents circular explanations, where you use the very thing you're trying to explain in order to explain it. But Kant then encountered a devastating problem: trees are self-organizing (the tree makes the leaves, the leaves make the tree), which means any explanation of living things falls into circularity — and therefore, he concluded, there could be no science of living things. This is taken by Vervaeke (via Juarrero) not as Kant's failure but as his achievement in identifying the exact conceptual gap that Aristotle's actuality/potentiality distinction and modern Dynamical Systems Theory fill. Kant's broader philosophical contributions (categories of understanding, phenomena/noumena, limits of theoretical reason) play a larger role in later episodes dealing with the meaning crisis and German Idealism (Ep 06).
