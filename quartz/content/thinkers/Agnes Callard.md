# Agnes Callard

> Philosopher at University of Chicago; developed the concept of *aspiration* and proleptic rationality in *Aspiration: The Agency of Becoming* (2016).

## Role in the argument
Callard's contribution to AMC is the account of aspirational, incremental transformative knowing — as a complement to LA Paul's account of sudden transformative experience. Aspiration is the rational pursuit of values and ways of seeing that one does not currently have; its rationality is *proleptic* (guided by the future self one is becoming). The music appreciation paradox demonstrates the structure: you cannot appreciate music for its own sake without the class, but if you already appreciated it, you wouldn't need it. Resolution: use a placeholder/symbol as an interim value, enter the liminal space, and gradually align with the new valuation (Ep 45).

This is deeply important for AMC because it shows: (a) that becoming wise (aspiring to wisdom) is not irrational — on pain of performative contradiction; (b) that aspiration involves a non-inferential form of rationality appropriate to identity change; (c) that this connects naturally to gnosis, wonder, and the symbolic structures that Vervaeke has been developing throughout the series. Aspiration bridges the gap between product theories of wisdom (what wisdom is) and process theories (how one becomes wise) — showing that the process has its own rationality (Ep 45).
