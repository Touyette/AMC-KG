# Karl Marx

> German philosopher, economist, and revolutionary (1818–1883); developed dialectical materialism and the theory of historical materialism; analyzed alienation as a product of capitalist labor relations; for Vervaeke, a key figure in the production of pseudo-religious ideology through the distortion of Hegelian dialectic.

## Role in the argument
Marx enters AMC primarily as a case study in the production of [[Pseudo-Religious Ideology]]. His taking of Hegel's dialectical method and applying it to material history — rather than the history of Spirit — produced a system with all the features of religious meaning-making (eschatological narrative, righteous cause, sacred community, martyrdom) but without the participatory-transformative knowing that makes wisdom traditions genuinely wisdom-bearing (Ep 24, Ep 25).

**Dialectical materialism**: Marx "inverted" Hegel: where Hegel's dialectic was the self-movement of Spirit through contradiction, Marx's dialectic is the movement of material forces of production through class conflict. The fundamental dynamic is not the development of consciousness but the conflict between those who own the means of production (bourgeoisie) and those who sell their labor (proletariat). History moves dialectically through modes of production toward the synthesis: communist society without class antagonism (Ep 24).

**Alienation**: Marx's concept of alienation (Entfremdung) — workers alienated from their product, their labor, their species-being, and each other under capitalism — diagnoses a genuine condition. For Vervaeke, this is a real and important analysis: capitalism does create conditions of systematic disconnection. But Marx's diagnosis, while pointing at real symptoms, misidentifies the level at which the problem is ultimately located (it is cognitive-existential, not just economic-structural), and his solution (collective ownership of means of production) addresses the structural level without the transformative dimension (Ep 36).

**The pseudo-religious inheritance**: The 20th century communist movements took Marx's system and gave it a fully pseudo-religious form: the Party as the Church, Marxist-Leninist doctrine as the creed, the proletariat as the chosen people, the revolution as the apocalypse, and the communist future as the eschaton. This is the structure of religious meaning without wisdom cultivation — which is why, when the narrative failed (the Soviet Union collapsed, the revolution did not arrive), the adherents faced a meaning crisis structurally similar to the Christian faith crisis (Ep 25).
