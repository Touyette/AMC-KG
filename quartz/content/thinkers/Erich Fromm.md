# Erich Fromm

> Humanistic psychologist and social philosopher who developed the Having Mode / Being Mode distinction as a framework for diagnosing the existential confusion at the heart of modern Western culture.

## Role in the argument
Vervaeke uses Fromm's distinction between the having mode and the being mode as the key to understanding modal confusion — and, through Siddhartha's palace myth, as the key to understanding what Buddhist awakening is a response to. In the having mode, needs are met by acquiring, categorizing, and controlling things; one relates to the world as a set of objects (I-It relations in Buber's language) and uses intelligence to solve problems. In the being mode, needs are met by becoming — becoming mature, virtuous, loving — and the world is encountered expressively rather than categorically. Modal confusion arises when we try to meet being needs (love, maturity, meaning) within the having mode: we buy more cars to meet a need for maturity, accumulate more sex to meet a need for love. This confusion is self-perpetuating because the real need is never met and so we pursue more of the same. Vervaeke links modal confusion directly to market-economy dynamics and to the perennial existential crises described in the world's wisdom traditions (Ep 07).
