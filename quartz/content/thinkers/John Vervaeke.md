# John Vervaeke

> Cognitive scientist and professor at the University of Toronto; the primary architect of the AMC argument and developer of relevance realization theory.

## Role in the argument

Vervaeke is both the lecturer and a central intellectual figure in the series. His original contributions woven throughout AMC include: the cognitive account of flow as an insight cascade (with Bennett and Ferraro, 2013); the framework of the four kinds of knowing (propositional, procedural, perspectival, participatory); relevance realization as the foundational cognitive mechanism underlying consciousness, wisdom, and meaning-making; the "perennial problems" framework identifying the structural existential problems any adequate response to the meaning crisis must address; the concept of the "wise cultivation of enlightenment" as a practical program; and the argument for "a religion that is not a religion" as the culturally appropriate response to the meaning crisis in the contemporary West. His project is to show that cognitive science, when pushed to its depths, converges with the deepest insights of the philosophical and contemplative traditions — and that this convergence is not accidental.
