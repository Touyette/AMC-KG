# Rudolf Otto

> German theologian and historian of religion whose concept of the **numinous** gives Vervaeke a rigorous, pre-moral vocabulary for the transgressive-experiential face of sacredness.

## Role in the argument
Otto's central contribution to AMC is his insistence that the experience of the sacred must be understood before it is moralized. His 1917 work *The Idea of the Holy* (German: *Das Heilige*) argues that "holy" in English usage has become dominated by moral connotations, obscuring a prior and more fundamental experience that Otto calls the **numinous** — from *numen*, divine power or will. The numinous names the pre-moral encounter with something wholly other (*das Ganz Andere*) — not merely unknown, but ontologically uncanny, exceeding all familiar categories.

Otto characterizes the numinous through the formula **mysterium tremendum et fascinans**: the numinous thing is simultaneously (1) mysterious — ontologically strange, beyond all framing; (2) *tremendum* — overwhelming, awe-inspiring, evoking dread and majesty; and (3) *fascinans* — irresistibly attractive, drawing one toward it despite the dread. Vervaeke develops this into a three-pole structure in Ep 34: mystery (the inexhaustibility of the framing process; sensibility transcendence), fascination/horror (the positive and negative faces of the overwhelming quality), and uses it to explain the meta-accommodation pole of sacredness (Ep 33–34).

Otto's key contribution within the AMC narrative: he provides a framework for talking about what religion is *doing* at the experiential level that is prior to belief (propositional knowledge), prior to morality (normative systems), and prior to metaphysical claims about God's existence. This resonates with Vervaeke's whole project of recovering a non-supernatural account of spirituality grounded in the transjective machinery of Relevance Realization.
