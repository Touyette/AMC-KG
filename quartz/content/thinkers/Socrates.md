# Socrates

> Athenian philosopher who embodies the Greek Axial Revolution by making wisdom — the tight coupling of truth-tracking and relevance — the central project of a meaningful life.

## Role in the argument
For Vervaeke, Socrates is not primarily a theorist but a practitioner who incarnates the Axial project of overcoming self-deception. His method — elenchus, systematic questioning — exposes the ever-present gap between what people find salient (which races ahead) and what they genuinely understand. The Delphic Oracle's pronouncement that no human is wiser than Socrates is interpreted not as praise but as a diagnostic: Socrates is wisest because he alone knows what he does not know, and that knowing is a direct, painful confrontation with his own capacity for bullshit. He draws a sharp contrast with the Sophists, who promote rhetoric that disconnects relevance from truth, and with the Natural Philosophers, whose truths are existentially irrelevant. Socrates' famous claim to know only "ta erotika" — how to love well — encapsulates his view that wisdom requires knowing what genuinely deserves one's care. He is also marked by shamanic residues: standing in meditation for 24-48 hours, extreme bodily control, a divine inner voice. His willingness to die rather than abandon philosophy demonstrates how central the cultivation of wisdom is to a life worth living, and his death becomes the founding trauma of Plato's philosophical system (Ep 04).
