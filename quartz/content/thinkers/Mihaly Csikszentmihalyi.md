# Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi

> Psychologist who identified and named the flow state as "optimal experience."

## Role in the argument

Csikszentmihalyi's work on the flow state (1990, *Flow: The Psychology of Optimal Experience*) provides Vervaeke with the empirical anchor for his claim that the shaman's ritual practices were psycho-technologies for inducing a specific, universal cognitive state. Csikszentmihalyi specified the environmental conditions (skill/demand match, tight coupling, clear feedback, meaningful failure) but did not account for the *cognitive mechanisms*. Vervaeke, with Adrian Bennett and Leo Ferraro (2013), provides the mechanistic account: flow is an insight cascade, which also explains why flow training enhances implicit learning. Csikszentmihalyi's finding that flow reliably produces a sense of meaning in life is central to Vervaeke's broader argument about the relationship between cognition and meaning (Ep 02, recurring).
