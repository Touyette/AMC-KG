# Gregory of Nyssa

> Eastern Orthodox bishop and theologian; one of the Cappadocian Fathers; in AMC his key contribution is *epektasis* — the structure of infinite self-transcendence in the inexhaustibility of God.

## Role in the argument
Gregory of Nyssa enters AMC through Tillich's appropriation of his mystical theology. Where the standard teleological salvation model promises rest in God as terminus (the Promised Land, the beatific vision), Gregory introduces epektasis: there is no resting in God, because God is inexhaustible. The human trajectory is one of *constant disclosure* of the inexhaustibleness of the ground of being — transjectivity and transframing never stop (Ep 50).

This is not a deficiency model (failing to reach a goal) but a positive description: the divine inexhaustibleness is itself what the soul participates in, and that participation is necessarily endless. John Scotus Eriugena picks up this concept and transmits it through the early medieval Western tradition.

Vervaeke uses epektasis to ground the non-idolatrous structure of Tillich's faith and method of correlation: because the ground of being cannot be "had," the proper relationship to it is always aspirational, always transframing, always ahead of any achieved frame — the same structure as proleptic rationality and the sacred second self (Ep 50).
