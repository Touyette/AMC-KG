# Galen Strawson

> British analytic philosopher; best known in AMC for the paradox of self-creation, which is used to sharpen the philosophical problem that aspiration and individuation must overcome.

## Role in the argument
Strawson's contribution is the **paradox of self-creation**: genuine self-creation requires both continuity (S1 = S2, or it's not *self*-creation) AND real novelty (S1 ≠ S2, or it's not *creation*) — these requirements contradict. Strawson concludes self-creation is self-contradictory, and the only alternatives are empiricism (no underlying self, novelty from outside) or romantic expressionism (pure continuity, no real novelty).

Callard and Vervaeke use this paradox productively: rather than accepting Strawson's conclusion, they resolve it via non-logical identity (the S1 → S2 relation is neither logical identity nor logical non-identity but genuine transformation) and participation in emergence (S1 participates in S2's coming-to-be, which is neither pure making nor pure receiving). Strawson's paradox thus clarifies *why* the aspiration to wisdom, rationality, and the sacred second self requires a non-inferential, imaginal mode of rationality (proleptic rationality) — because the strictly logical identity requirements he articulates cannot be met by any merely inferential process (Ep 49).
