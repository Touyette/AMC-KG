# Friedrich Schlegel

> German early Romantic philosopher and literary critic; one of the Jena Romantics (post-Kantians); in AMC his key contribution is the infinity of reality and the finite longing for the infinite — the foundational insight that Barfield inherits and develops into final participation.

## Role in the argument
Schlegel represents the early Romantic / post-Kantian tradition that Di Fuccia identifies as the deep philosophical substrate of Owen Barfield's thought (via Coleridge as conduit). The early Romantics (distinguished by Vervaeke from the later Romantics and from Hegel) emphasized the *infinity of reality*: the inexhaustible surplus of being that cannot be fully bounded by any finite frame (Ep 50).

Schlegel's formulation: the *finite longing for the infinite*. Finite beings — always framed, always limited — experience an epek-static pull toward the inexhaustible moreness that continually exceeds any frame deployed. This is not irrational yearning but the cognitive-existential structure of all genuine self-transcendence: the RR machinery attuning to a combinatorially explosive reality, drawn by what cannot be fully framed into ongoing transframing.

Schlegel's early Romantics emphasize that the locus of this contact is *creativity* (poiesis) and *flow* — not the irrational (later Romanticism) and not the dialectic of a propositional system (Hegel). The self-transcendence found in genuine creative engagement is where the finite participates the infinite. Barfield inherits this and develops it into final participation: the recovery of original participation integrated with rational science (Ep 50).
