# Francisco Varela

> Chilean biologist and cognitive scientist; with Maturana developed autopoiesis; with Thompson and Rosch authored "The Embodied Mind"; pioneered enactivism and the integration of phenomenology with cognitive science; key figure in 4E cognitive science.

## Role in the argument
Varela is Vervaeke's primary scientific anchor for the enactivist and participatory turn in cognitive science. Vervaeke draws on Varela extensively (via "The Embodied Mind" and "Steps towards a Science of Interbeing") to establish that cognition is not computation-over-representations but organism-environment coupling — providing biological and cognitive-scientific grounds for the irreducibility of participatory knowing (Ep 29, Ep 30, Ep 38).

**Autopoiesis and cognitive domains**: Varela and Maturana's concept of [[Autopoiesis]] (self-producing biological systems) grounds the claim that organisms don't represent a pre-given world but produce their own cognitive domain through ongoing self-maintenance. What the organism "perceives" is what matters to its self-production, not an objective landscape of neutral features (Ep 30).

**Enactivism**: In "The Embodied Mind" (with Evan Thompson and Eleanor Rosch), Varela argued that cognition is enaction — the organism actively brings forth a world of significance through sensorimotor coupling, rather than passively representing an external world. This directly supports Vervaeke's argument that [[Relevance Realization]] is non-representational (Ep 29, Ep 38).

**Integration with phenomenology**: Varela was committed to a neurophenomenological research program: integrating first-person phenomenological description (Husserlian tradition) with third-person cognitive science, arguing that neither can proceed adequately without the other. This methodological commitment mirrors Vervaeke's insistence that the meaning crisis requires both scientific and phenomenological-transformative responses (Ep 38).

**The deep continuity hypothesis**: Varela's biology of cognition (organism as self-organizing process continuous with its environment) provides scientific grounding for Vervaeke's [[Deep Continuity Hypothesis]]: that the mechanisms of relevance realization are continuous with — not merely analogous to — the self-organizing processes of biology and ecology (Ep 38).
