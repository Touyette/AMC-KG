# Jonathan Baron

> Professor Emeritus of Psychology at University of Pennsylvania; develops *good thinking* as a trained dispositional virtue; coined active open-mindedness.

## Role in the argument
Baron's contribution to AMC is primarily through his account of *active open-mindedness* — the cognitive style that Stanovich identifies as the key predictor of rationality beyond intelligence. Baron frames rational thinking not as possession of logical competence but as a disposition: a trained tendency to seek disconfirming evidence, check one's own biases, and weight evidence independently of one's prior commitments. This is explicitly virtue-like: it requires cultivation, not just ability, and it must be applied to the right degree (not maximized, which produces combinatorial explosion) (Ep 41).

Vervaeke notes convergence with Stoic prosoche and CBT — not because Baron designed his account with these in mind, but because they independently identified the same structure of self-corrective rationality. This convergence is evidence that something real is being pointed to (Ep 41).
