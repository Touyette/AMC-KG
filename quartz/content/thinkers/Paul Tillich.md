# Paul Tillich

> German-American Lutheran existentialist theologian; one of the four prophets of the meaning crisis; author of *The Courage to Be*; develops faith as ultimate concern, the ground of being, and the God beyond the God of theism.

## Role in the argument
Tillich's position in AMC is the theological integration of everything the prophets arc has built: he brings together Heidegger's critique of onto-theology, Jung's depth psychology and individuation, and the imaginal/symbol tradition (Corbin-adjacent without a direct connection) into a practical framework for responding to the meaning crisis. *The Courage to Be* is Vervaeke's key text: a masterpiece that takes the meaning crisis seriously and gives us guidance on how to live (Ep 50).

**Courage as existential virtue**: Tillich rehabilitates courage — not mere bravery (facing danger) or fortitude (enduring difficulty), but seeing through the illusion and distortion of fear or despair to what is truly good and acting accordingly. This is a form of wisdom built into courage: the courageous person has the insight (transframing capacity) to see through existential distortion. The process of *encouragement* (literally, in-couraging oneself) is how Tillich frames the response to meaninglessness (Ep 50).

**Faith as ultimate concern**: Tillich's faith is not the willful assertion of propositions. It is being grasped by what concerns one ultimately — participatory, perspectival, aspirational, and pointing toward the inexhaustible ground of being. This circles back to the Hebrew da'at: participatory knowing of God as a course of being. Faith is the deepest expression of Dasein's questioning of its own being, resolving toward aletheia and the ontological wonder appropriate to the ground of meaning (Ep 50).

**God as icon (not idol) and the ground of being**: God is for Tillich an *imaginal* symbol for the ground of being — not a supreme being (onto-theology), not a reified object to have and possess. God simultaneously grounds meaning, reality, and their relationship. Any reification of God is idolatry. The no-thingness of God (God is no kind of thing) is his transgressive response to both classical theism and atheism. The "God beyond the God of theism" is his explicit formulation (Ep 50).

**Method of correlation**: Tillich's methodology is widely misread as aligning Bible propositions with existential questions. It is actually an anagogic loop: the existential ekstasis (depths of reason standing beyond themselves, drawing on all the RR machinery) resonates with the depths of reality (shining/miracle + withdrawal/mystery) through an imaginal symbol — the same structure as the sacred second self relationship and anagoge. The method of correlation IS the transformative anagogic process (Ep 50).

**Epektasis (Gregory of Nyssa)**: Tillich appropriates epektasis to ground the non-idolatrous character of faith: there is no resting in God, no achieved terminus; only the ongoing disclosure of the inexhaustibleness of the ground of being. The method of correlation therefore spirals infinitely rather than terminating. Transjectivity and transframing never stop (Ep 50).

**Existential vs essential self / theonomy**: Tillich frames the S1/S2 aspiration structure as existential self vs essential self. The essential self (S2) is normatively ahead — not causally but normatively — beckoning the existential self toward fuller being. The resolution to the aspiration paradox (can't be heteronomous/imposed from without, can't be autonomous/self-generated): theonomy. God-ordered, but God = ground of being = the ongoing epektatic affordance of transframing. Synergistic, like the Hebrew da'at (Ep 50).

**Historical arc of meaninglessness**: *The Courage to Be* traces three historical epochs of meaninglessness: (1) Ancient: finitude/mortality confronted by Stoic courage; (2) Christian Reformation: guilt confronted by Lutheran justification; (3) Modern: despair confronted by existentialist engagement and what lies beyond it — the method of correlation and non-theism (Ep 50).

**Non-theism**: Tillich rejects all four shared premises of theism and atheism. His "God beyond the God of theism" is the non-theist formulation: neither a supreme being (theism) nor its denial (atheism), but the inexhaustible ground that requires transformative participatory knowing to encounter. This is deeply continuous with the gnosis within Tillich (Ep 50).

**Criticism of Tillich**: Although superior to Heidegger in giving guidance on how to live (courage and faith, deeply reinterpreted), Tillich does not provide practices of transformation. Jung created active imagination and dream interpretation; Tillich provides the framework but not the psychotechnologies. This is the main gap in Tillich's prophetic response (Ep 50).
