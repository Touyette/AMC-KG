# John Scotus Eriugena

> Irish Catholic Neoplatonist philosopher; mediated Neoplatonism and apophatic theology to the medieval West; in AMC known for transmitting and developing Gregory of Nyssa's concept of epektasis.

## Role in the argument
Eriugena appears in AMC as the transmitter of [[Gregory of Nyssa]]'s [[Epektasis]] into the Western philosophical-theological tradition. He picks up the idea that the soul's journey toward God has no terminus — the infinite self-transcendence within God's inexhaustibleness — and develops it within his own Neoplatonic framework.

Vervaeke cites both Gregory of Nyssa and Eriugena together as the Eastern Orthodox Neoplatonic sources for the concept that Tillich will appropriate: there is no resting in God, only the constant disclosure of inexhaustible ground. This non-terminus structure is crucial to Tillich's argument that faith-as-ultimate-concern cannot become idolatry: the ground of being perpetually recedes from any achieved frame, perpetually affording further transframing and aspiration (Ep 50).
