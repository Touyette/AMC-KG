# Igor Grossman

> Psychologist (colleague of Vervaeke); developed the Solomon effect (Solomon Paradox) — the finding that first-person perspective impairs wise reasoning while third-person perspective enhances it; develops experimental measures of wisdom.

## Role in the argument
Grossman's Solomon effect research demonstrates empirically that the perspectival dimension of wisdom is causally important: asking people to redescribe their own problems from a third-person perspective reliably produces wiser reasoning — they notice structural features and relevant considerations that were invisible from within their first-person framing. The name invokes the biblical Solomon: when advising others, we naturally occupy a de-centered perspective and reason more wisely (Ep 12, 43).

This grounds several convergent threads in AMC: the Socratic practice of examining others (and oneself under dialogue); the Stoic practice of internalizing wise interlocutors; Baltes & Staudinger's experimental finding that simulated internal dialogue is as effective as actual dialogue; and Vervaeke's general account of wisdom as involving perspectival transformation rather than mere propositional knowledge accumulation (Ep 43).
