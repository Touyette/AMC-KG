# Andy Clark

> British philosopher and cognitive scientist; with David Chalmers developed the extended mind thesis (1998); author of "Being There" and "Supersizing the Mind"; key figure in embodied and extended cognition.

## Role in the argument
Clark provides the philosophical articulation of [[Extended Cognition]] that Vervaeke uses to ground the claim that cognitive processes are not skin-bounded. The Clark-Chalmers "parity principle" — if an external process plays the functional role an internal cognitive process would, it is part of cognition — is the argument that enables Vervaeke to treat psychotechnologies (writing, ritual, contemplative practice) as cognitive extensions rather than external aids (Ep 38).

**The extended mind thesis**: Clark and Chalmers' famous thought experiment: Otto (with Alzheimer's) uses a notebook as external memory; Inga uses biological memory. If they are functionally equivalent, Otto's notebook is his memory. The cognitive boundary is not the skull but the functional role. This has significant implications for how to understand the cultural transmission of psychotechnologies and the cognitive significance of environment design (Ep 38).

**Scaffold cognition**: Clark's concept of "scaffolded cognition" — organisms routinely offloading cognitive operations onto the environment to expand what they can accomplish — connects directly to Vervaeke's account of how psychotechnologies reorganize and extend human cognitive capacities. Writing is not just communication but a cognitive scaffold that enables forms of thought impossible "in the head" (Ep 38).
