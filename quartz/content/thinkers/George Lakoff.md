# George Lakoff

> Cognitive linguist whose work on conceptual metaphor theory grounds Vervaeke's account of how meaning is embodied and cross-domain.

## Role in the argument

Lakoff (with Mark Johnson, *Metaphors We Live By*, 1980) demonstrated that human thought and language are saturated with metaphor — not as rhetorical decoration but as the fundamental mechanism by which abstract reasoning is structured through bodily and sensorimotor experience. Vervaeke uses this to explain what the shaman's flow state produces neurologically: increased cross-talk between brain regions generates a massive expansion of metaphorical cognition, making the Upper Paleolithic Transition intelligible as a cognitive revolution. Lakoff's work also underpins Vervaeke's later distinction between basic metaphorical cognition and the deeper *participatory* or *profound* metaphor at work in symbols — where the connection does not merely reorganize thought but transforms one's mode of participation in being (Ep 02, Ep 34).
