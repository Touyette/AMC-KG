# Christopher Cherniak

> American philosopher and neuroscientist (University of Maryland); coined the *finitary predicament*; argues for minimal rationality.

## Role in the argument
Cherniak's contribution is the *finitary predicament* — the observation that finite cognitive systems cannot derive all implications or check all contradictions without combinatorial explosion, making formal logic and probability theory inappropriate as normative standards for human rationality. His "ought implies can" argument: if following a normative standard would lead to cognitive suicide (infinite computation), that standard is the wrong one (Ep 41).

Vervaeke takes the argument seriously and sees it as a largely negative characterization of what relevance realization positively describes: not arbitrary selection, but ecologically grounded, relevance-guided selection from an otherwise explosively large space. Stanovich and West accept Cherniak's point but redirect it: the finitary predicament describes *intelligence* (g), not *rationality* — which is why the correlation between g and reasoning performance is only 0.3 (Ep 41).
