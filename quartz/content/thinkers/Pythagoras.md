# Pythagoras

> Pre-Socratic philosopher-mystic who fused shamanic soul-flight with mathematical rationalism, forming a crucial bridge between the shamanic tradition and Plato's Axial synthesis.

## Role in the argument
Pythagoras represents a pivotal moment in the Greek Axial Revolution: he takes the shamanic project of altered consciousness and self-transcendence and allies it explicitly with the new psycho-technology of rational argumentation and mathematical insight. His discovery that music can be expressed by mathematical ratios showed that abstract rational patterns underlie and reveal the deep structure of reality. This became the template for Platonic anagoge — ascending through rational insight into the real patterns of things leads to liberation. Pythagoras is also significant for inventing the word "cosmos" (from the same root as cosmetics: revealing the beauty and order of things) and the word "philosophy" (love of wisdom). His influence on Plato is enormous: the Pythagorean idea that soul flight is liberation from the body through rational insight into forms becomes central to Plato's two-worlds mythology and the Myth of the Cave (Ep 03, Ep 04).
