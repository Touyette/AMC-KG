# William James

> American philosopher and psychologist (1842–1910); founder of pragmatism (with C.S. Peirce); author of *The Varieties of Religious Experience* (1902) and *The Principles of Psychology* (1890); argued that the truth of an idea is its practical workability — its power to guide successful action and experience; the first major scientific-philosophical engagement with mystical and religious experience.

## Role in the argument
James enters AMC through *The Varieties of Religious Experience* — the founding document of the empirical study of religious experience. Vervaeke draws on James's four marks of mystical experience (noetic quality, transiency, passivity, ineffability) to ground the phenomenology of higher states of consciousness and to show that the continuity hypothesis has historical precedent: James himself argued that these states are genuinely noetic (they feel like knowing something real) even if their content cannot be fully expressed propositionally (Ep 11, Ep 38).

**The Varieties of Religious Experience**: James collected first-person reports of religious and mystical experiences and subjected them to philosophical-psychological analysis. He identified common features across traditions and denominations, arguing that the variation in doctrinal interpretation conceals an underlying unity of experience. This is an early form of the comparative approach Vervaeke uses: looking for the cognitive-existential mechanisms that the experiences share rather than adjudicating between traditions (Ep 11).

**Four marks of mystical experience**: James identified: (1) noetic quality — mystical states seem like knowledge, not just feeling, they reveal something about reality; (2) transiency — they do not last long; (3) passivity — they come upon the subject, not as a result of effort; (4) ineffability — they cannot be adequately conveyed in ordinary language. Vervaeke engages these especially the noetic quality: if these states track something real about the organism-environment relationship (the deep structure of RR), then the noetic claim is warranted (Ep 11).

**Pragmatic truth**: James's broader philosophical position — that the truth of an idea is its practical working, its power to guide experience successfully — provides the background for how Vervaeke treats psycho-technologies. Practices that reliably produce transformative insight, wisdom, and flourishing are not "merely psychological" in a dismissive sense but are tracking something real. Pragmatic truth overlaps with Vervaeke's account of how relevance realization homes in on real features of the organism-environment relationship (Ep 38).
