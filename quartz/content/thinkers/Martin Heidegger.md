# Martin Heidegger

> One of the central prophets of the meaning crisis; argues that the entire history of Western metaphysics is the history of nihilism; develops phenomenology into an account of participatory knowing, Dasein, and the question of Being.

## Role in the argument
Heidegger is the most pivotal figure in AMC's "prophets" arc — both because of the depth of his diagnosis and because of his downstream influence on the other prophets (Corbin, Kyoto School, speculative realism) and on the postmodern tradition. His philosophical attitude toward the Cartesian-Platonic-ontotheological tradition is: it did not merely get some things wrong — it inexorably generated nihilism, and thereby the meaning crisis. This makes him a prophet in the Old Testament sense: telling forth a crisis and galvanizing a response (Ep 46).

**Background influences**: Husserl (Heidegger is his student and primary successor); Eckhart and the Rhineland Mystics (the Gnostic underground running through Germany, especially between the Wars — John Caputo's *The Mystical Element in Heidegger's Thought* documents this directly); Kant and Descartes (against whom much of Heidegger is reacting) (Ep 46).

**Critique of Husserl**: Husserl's phenomenology aimed at contact epistemology but failed because (1) it did not account for participatory knowing — the ontological modes in which agent and arena are co-constituted before any intentional act; (2) it remained trapped inside the Cartesian cultural cognitive grammar (explicitly naming his late work *Cartesian Meditations*), making phenomenology ultimately a kind of idealism rather than genuine realism. Merleau-Ponty partially repairs (1) via embodied perception (Ep 46).

**Dasein**: Heidegger's term for the human being — the being whose being is in question. By phenomenologically exploring Dasein, one simultaneously accesses (a) participatory knowing (the existential modes of being-in-the-world) and (b) ontological questioning (putting being itself into question). This is the bridge Husserl lacked: self-inquiry opens directly onto the structure of being. Neologisms (Dasein, aletheia, Seinsvergessenheit) are deliberate: they break the cultural cognitive grammar that keeps us in familiar Cartesian categories (Ep 46).

**Thesis**: The history of Western metaphysics is the history of nihilism — the entire axial-revolution inheritance, as it developed through Platonic two-worlds mythology, Christianity, Scholasticism, and the Scientific Revolution, drove inexorably toward the loss of contact with Being (forgetfulness of Being), which IS the meaning crisis. The response is a phenomenological-existential investigation of Dasein that re-establishes participatory contact with Being — not theoretically but existentially (Ep 46).

**On the Essence of Truth** (*Vom Wesen der Wahrheit*): Truth as propositional correspondence (statement ↔ reality) is grounded in the agent-arena relationship, which is itself grounded in **attunement** (Stimmung) — a pre-cognitive, non-subjective fittedness-to-being. Attunement is NOT a subjective feeling; it is what makes feeling and experience possible. Vervaeke maps attunement onto RR. Ek-sistence (standing out into being) corresponds to salience. Forgetfulness of this attunement → modal confusion → being trapped in the having mode of propositions ("man clings to what is readily available and controllable even where ultimate matters are concerned"). Heidegger recuperates Plato's distinction: *philia sophia* (love of wisdom as transformative existential quest) vs. *philia nikia* (love of victory through propositional manipulation) — the history of metaphysics is an increasing dominance of philia nikia (Ep 47).

**Thing beyond itself / truth as aletheia**: Every object simultaneously shines into subjectivity (phenomenological disclosure) AND withdraws from any frame (Harman's explication). Both are transjective and co-constitute the realness of things. The moreness (withdrawal) must be an ongoing constraint, not just acknowledged at the limit. Truth as aletheia = attunement to this mutual disclosure — a deep existential remembering (like sati) + disclosure of things as simultaneously shining and withdrawing. Two poles: relevance (attunement) + moreness/inexhaustibleness (independence of Being). Ground of propositional correctness, not a species of it (Ep 47).

**Onto-theology → nihilism**: When attunement is forgotten (having mode), Being (capital B, the ground of realness) is misidentified as a supreme being — classical theism's God as the highest entity. This is onto-theology: a having-mode attempt to possess Being propositionally. When the supreme being is then undermined (Nietzsche's "death of God"), Being appears to vanish, generating nihilism. But Being only seemed to vanish because it was never understood as more than the supreme being in the first place (Ep 47).

**Dreyfus connection**: Dreyfus interprets Heidegger for cognitive science; his *Being-In-The-World* shows how Heidegger's account grounds the critique of computational/propositional AI (frame problem: "facts and rules are by themselves meaningless; they must be assigned relevance"). Dreyfus → optimal grip → RR. This chain shows Vervaeke's framework is continuous with phenomenology, not imposed on it (Ep 47).

**Corbin connection**: Corbin was one of Heidegger's first major translators into French (*Introduction to Metaphysics*). Robert Avens (*The New Gnosis*) shows that Corbin explicitly calls Heidegger's participatory knowing — self-knowing and world-knowing dynamically coupled ("like can only be known by like") — gnosis: "a salvational redemptive knowledge because it has the virtue of bringing about the inner transformation of man." Gnosis is the appropriate response to Dasein (the being whose being is in question) because it is a knowing that simultaneously transforms who you are and discloses what the world is (Ep 47).

**Downstream**: Existentialism (Sartre, Merleau-Ponty, Tillich); postmodernism/deconstruction (Derrida); speculative realism/OOO (Harman's object-oriented ontology as response to the correlationist limits of phenomenology); Kyoto School (Nishitani synthesizes Heidegger with Zen Buddhism in *Religion and Nothingness*); Corbin (Heidegger shapes Corbin's treatment of the imaginal) (Ep 46).

**Ambivalence**: Vervaeke names Heidegger as pivotal but controversial — his eventual Nazism is "despicable" and led him to turn on Husserl for the worst reasons. The philosophical engagement is genuine but the ambivalence is also genuine (Ep 46).
