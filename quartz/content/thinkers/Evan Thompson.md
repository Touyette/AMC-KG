# Evan Thompson

> Canadian philosopher of mind; with Varela and Rosch co-authored "The Embodied Mind" (1991); author of "Waking, Dreaming, Being"; a leading figure in enactivism and neurophenomenology.

## Role in the argument
Thompson, as co-author of "The Embodied Mind" with [[Francisco Varela]] and Eleanor Rosch, is part of the founding of enactivism and the embodied cognition turn. His contribution to Vervaeke's argument is primarily through the enactivist framework: the claim that cognition is the active bringing-forth of meaning through organism-environment coupling, not the internal manipulation of representations (Ep 38).

**Neurophenomenology**: Thompson extended Varela's neurophenomenological program, seeking rigorous methodological integration of first-person phenomenological description (Husserl, Merleau-Ponty) with third-person cognitive neuroscience. This methodological stance directly parallels Vervaeke's approach of combining cognitive science with phenomenological and wisdom-tradition insights (Ep 38).

**"Waking, Dreaming, Being"**: Thompson's later work explores consciousness across its states — waking, dreaming, deep sleep, and meditation — from an enactivist perspective. This provides cognitive-scientific context for Vervaeke's use of the [[Continuity Hypothesis]]: different states of consciousness are continuous variations in the enactive coupling of organism and world, not categorically different kinds of experience (Ep 38).
