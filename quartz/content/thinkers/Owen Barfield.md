# Owen Barfield

> British philosopher, author, and one of the Inklings (with Tolkien, C.S. Lewis, Charles Williams); one of the four prophets of the meaning crisis; develops final participation and poiesis as the response.

## Role in the argument
Barfield enters AMC's final movement as the thinker who most directly proposes a *practice*-oriented response to the meaning crisis through poiesis and final participation. Where Tillich gives framework (faith, ground of being, method of correlation) but lacks psychotechnologies, Barfield's notion of poiesis at least points toward the participatory creativity that could enact final participation. Vervaeke's self-placement at the series' close is that his own RR theory provides the science of meaning cultivation that Barfield calls for but doesn't deliver (Ep 50).

**Influences**: Barfield is deeply influenced by Gnosticism (via Rudolf Steiner, whom he esteemed highly — best understood as a modern Gnostic generating gnostic mythology); by Neoplatonism (via Samuel Taylor Coleridge, who transmitted early Romantic / post-Kantian thought to Barfield); and by the early Romantics, particularly Friedrich Schlegel's emphasis on the infinity of reality and the finite longing for the infinite (Ep 50).

**Participation narrative**: Barfield's central narrative is three-stage: (1) *original participation* — the pre-Cartesian mode in which inner and outer interpenetrated transjectively, visible as traces in the dual meanings of ancient (and modern!) words (pneuma = wind AND spirit; see = visual AND conceptual); (2) *the division* — the Cartesian revolution producing the meaning crisis, two-worlds mythology, loss of imaginal; (3) *final participation* — recovery of original participation integrated within rational science (Ep 50).

**Poiesis**: Barfield's key contribution to the practices question. Poiesis (participatory creativity / the ekstasis of creative engagement) is neither passively receiving (Heidegger's Gelassenheit alone) nor actively imposing (Cartesian will). It is synergistic: God (= ground of being) plays the leading role, we contribute, in the spirit of the Hebrew da'at. Breakthrough (Durchbruch) is as essential as Gelassenheit — Heidegger took only the passive Eckhart and forgot the active one (Ep 50).

**Language and re-exaptation**: Barfield studies etymology as evidence of original participation: dual inner/outer meanings of words (attack = physical destruction AND critical argumentation; see = visual AND conceptual understanding) reveal the imaginal interpenetration that preceded the Cartesian split. Vervaeke's amendment: rather than Barfield's evolutionary hypothesis (ancient humans had a different consciousness), the better account is synchronic psychological development (Lakoff/Johnson) plus Corbin's imaginal: the abstract-intellectual and concrete-sensory meet in the imaginal, explaining why disparate sensory bases ("see," "grasp," "get") all converge on understanding. This is re-exaptation — poiesis in its deepest cognitive sense (Ep 50).

**Vervaeke's criticism of Barfield**: (1) Does not provide the science of meaning cultivation that final participation requires from the scientific side — Vervaeke's RR theory is his attempted contribution here. (2) Evolutionary hypothesis is likely wrong; synchronic/developmental account is better. (3) Barfield's followers should not simply import him into classical theism — this fails to honor his argument, which requires the onto-theological critique that Tillich and Heidegger perform. (4) Barfield's non-theism: Vervaeke suspects Barfield, if he had conversed with Tillich, Jung, Corbin, and Heidegger, would also have been led to non-theism; this is already clearly the case for Schlegel (Ep 50).
