# Karl Jaspers

> German existentialist philosopher who named the Axial Age.

## Role in the argument

Jaspers coined the term "Axial Age" to name the observation that the religious and philosophical figures of ~800–300 BCE (Socrates, Plato, Buddha, Confucius, the Hebrew prophets) remain formative for us today in a way that earlier Bronze Age civilizations do not. Vervaeke takes this as his primary historical framing device and builds a cognitive explanation for why this transformation is so structurally significant: the Axial Revolution produced the psycho-technology of second-order thinking, which is literally constitutive of the kind of mind that reads Plato and finds it relevant (Ep 02).
