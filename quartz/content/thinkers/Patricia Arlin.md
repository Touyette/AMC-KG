# Patricia Arlin

> Canadian psychologist; developed the concept of *problem finding* as a post-formal cognitive operation; argued in 1990 that problem finding is central to wisdom.

## Role in the argument
Arlin's work grounds Vervaeke's concept of the problem nexus and establishes the connection between creativity, problem-finding, and wisdom. Her core argument (1990): most psychological models of cognition focus on problem-solving — what you do when given a well-defined problem. But a crucial capacity, neglected by formal models, is *problem finding*: the ability to identify significant problems that others have not yet seen. This is not just an intellectual skill but a post-formal cognitive operation — it arises after, and goes beyond, the formal operations of Piagetian development (Ep 42).

Vervaeke extends her insight: not all problem-finding is equally valuable. The highest form is generating a *problem nexus* — a hub problem whose solution would cascade across multiple existing problems. This connects Arlin's work directly to wisdom: the wise person does not merely accumulate knowledge or solve given problems but identifies the deeper problems that others have not yet articulated. This is why wisdom and creativity overlap — both require the synoptic capacity to see across frames (Ep 42).
