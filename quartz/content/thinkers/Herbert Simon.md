# Herbert Simon

> American cognitive scientist, economist, and Nobel laureate; developed *bounded rationality* and *satisficing*; co-creator of the General Problem Solver.

## Role in the argument
Simon establishes the ecological context for rationality: human cognition is not unlimited computation but *bounded* by limited information, time, and processing capacity. Therefore rational agents do not maximize — they *satisfice*, searching until a good-enough option is found within real-world constraints. This challenges both the classical economic homo economicus and the logicist model of rationality as exhaustive inference (Ep 41).

Vervaeke treats bounded rationality as convergent with Cherniak's finitary predicament and with his own account of relevance realization. Simon's General Problem Solver work with Newell also shows that effective problem-solving relies on heuristics — domain-general search strategies that are fallible but ecologically adapted to the finitary predicament. The question Simon leaves open (and which Vervaeke answers via RR) is what governs the selection of relevance — what makes a heuristic appropriate for a situation (Ep 41).
