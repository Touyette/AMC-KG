# Susan Wolf

> American philosopher (b. 1952); author of *Meaning in Life and Why It Matters* (2010); developed the "fitting fulfillment" account of meaning in life: meaning arises when subjective attraction and objective attractiveness coincide; for Vervaeke, the philosopher who identifies the problem that agape resolves — that meaning requires connection to something of independent value.

## Role in the argument
Wolf enters AMC through her analysis of what conditions are sufficient for meaning in life. Her account — meaning requires that one is subjectively drawn toward (finds fulfilling) what is objectively worthy of engagement — sets up the philosophical problem that Vervaeke uses agape and the ground of being to resolve (Ep 38).

**Subjective attraction + objective attractiveness**: Wolf argues that meaning in life requires both: (1) subjective attraction — you genuinely care about and are engaged by what you are doing (not just going through the motions); (2) objective attractiveness — what you care about has genuine value independent of your caring about it (pursuing trivial things with great enthusiasm is not meaningful). Meaning is the happy coincidence of these two: what you care about matters, and what matters you care about (Ep 38).

**Wolf's problem**: Wolf identifies a philosophical puzzle she cannot fully solve: how can we know that something has objective attractiveness? If we appeal to what seems objectively attractive to us, we seem to be going in circles (we are attracted to it, and call it objectively attractive). Vervaeke calls this "Wolf's problem" and uses it as the setup for the agape response: agape is the mode of attending to others and the world that lets the objectively attractive register as such — it is the recalibration of the RR machinery that enables genuine response to what is genuinely worthy (Ep 38).

**Agape as the solution**: For Vervaeke, agape — understood as the mode of attending to the conditions for personhood and flourishing — is precisely the cognitive-relational mode in which objective attractiveness becomes reliably salient. When one attends to persons and situations with agapic love (attending to their genuine good rather than their instrumental value), the RR machinery is calibrated to register what is genuinely worthy. This resolves Wolf's problem without requiring an external criterion (Ep 38).
